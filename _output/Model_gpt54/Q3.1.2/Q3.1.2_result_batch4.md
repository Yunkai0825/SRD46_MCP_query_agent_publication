Based on the SRD-46 results I could actually retrieve, the **best amino-acid ligand for selectively complexing Cu²⁺ over Ni²⁺ at pH 7 is histidine** (SRD-46 ligand entry: **L-2-Amino-3-(4-imidazolyl)propionic acid**, i.e. histidine).

| Ligand | SRD-46 evidence relevant to Cu²⁺ vs Ni²⁺ | pH 7 implication | Selectivity call |
|---|---|---|---|
| Histidine | Both **Cu²⁺** and **Ni²⁺** entries are present for the same ligand (**ligand_5898**); histidine also appeared in the pKa search with a neutral-pH-relevant donor set | At pH 7, histidine offers **carboxylate + amine + imidazole**, a donor combination that strongly favors Cu²⁺ | **Best supported choice** |
| Glycine / alanine / serine / Asp / Glu family | These were discussed in pKa results as mostly simple O/N donors without a strong Cu-favoring side chain | Weaker Cu/Ni discrimination near neutral pH | Inferior |
| Cysteine-type ligands | Thiol chemistry can complicate Cu²⁺ speciation/redox near pH 7 | Less clean for selective Cu²⁺ vs Ni²⁺ capture | Not preferred |

A key limitation is that the available SRD-46 tool outputs here did **not expose matched numerical Cu and Ni logβ values** for the same simple amino acids under identical conditions, so I cannot give a defensible numeric selectivity ratio from the retrieved records alone. Still, among the amino-acid ligands actually supported by the database hits, **histidine is the strongest Cu²⁺-selective option**.

If you meant **amino-acid-derived ligands including peptides**, then the pKa search pointed to **glycyl-L-histidine (Gly–His)** as an even stronger Cu-biased choice at pH 7 than simple free amino acids.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 equilibrium/pKa search results retrieved above.

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
There are gaps. The subagent gives a plausible qualitative choice—**histidine**—but it does **not** provide the requested **best selectivity ratio** at pH 7, nor a defensible numeric Cu²⁺/Ni²⁺ comparison from matched data.

**Explanation**  
Scientifically, histidine is a strong candidate because its imidazole donor tends to stabilize Cu²⁺ more than Ni²⁺ near neutral pH, so Cu binding is expected to be favored. However, without matched conditional stability constants or speciation calculations at pH 7, the actual selectivity ratio remains unquantified; cysteine also has important redox/speciation complications that make “selectivity” less straightforward.
