## Verdict
There are gaps: the subagent gave a plausible qualitative answer (**histidine**) but did not provide the requested **best selectivity ratio** at pH 7 from matched Cu²⁺ and Ni²⁺ data, so it did not fully answer the question.

## Explanation
Scientifically, histidine is a strong candidate because its imidazole side chain is an N-donor that binds Cu²⁺ especially well near neutral pH, where the imidazole is partially available (pKa ~6). Simpler amino acids like glycine, or side chains protonated at pH 7 such as lysine, usually discriminate less between Cu²⁺ and Ni²⁺. But without matched Ni²⁺–histidine stability data and protonation-corrected conditional constants at pH 7, the selectivity ranking remains provisional.
