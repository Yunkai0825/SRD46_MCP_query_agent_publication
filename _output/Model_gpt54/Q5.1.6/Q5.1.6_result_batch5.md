## Rare earths are unusually broad in coverage — and EDTA makes that especially visible

SRD-46 is dominated by common ions like H⁺ and Cu²⁺, but the rare earths show up as a **cluster** rather than a single dominant metal. The database includes many trivalent rare-earth/related EDTA systems: **La³⁺, Ce³⁺, Pr³⁺, Nd³⁺, Sm³⁺, Eu³⁺, Gd³⁺, Tb³⁺, Dy³⁺, Ho³⁺, Er³⁺, Tm³⁺, Lu³⁺, plus Sc³⁺ and even Pm³⁺**.

## One concrete example: La³⁺–EDTA is strongly bound
For **La³⁺ + EDTA ⇌ LaEDTA**, SRD-46 reports:

| System | Conditions | Reported values |
|---|---|---|
| La³⁺–EDTA | 25 °C, I = 0.1–0.5 M | logK = 14.48–15.36 |
| LaEDTA protonation | 20–25 °C, I = 0.1–1 M | logK = 2.0–2.24 |
| La³⁺–EDTA thermodynamics | 25 °C region | ΔH = −12.1 to −22.2; ΔS = +222.6 to +253.6 |

That is interesting because it shows rare-earth complexation here is not just “present” but **quantitatively strong and thermodynamically characterized**.

## A second interesting contrast: La³⁺–oxalate shows precipitation behavior
SRD-46 also has a smaller **La³⁺–oxalate** dataset:

| System | Conditions | Reported values |
|---|---|---|
| La³⁺ + oxalate ⇌ LaL | 25 °C, I = 0.1 M | logK = 4.71 |
| La³⁺ + 2 oxalate ⇌ LaL₂ | 25 °C, I = 0.1 M | logK = 7.83 |
| Higher oxalate complexes | 25 °C, I = 1.0 M | logK(ML) = 4.3; logK(ML2) = 7.9; logK(ML3) = 10.3 |
| Lanthanum oxalate solid | 20 °C, I = 0.1 M | logK = −25 for dissolution entry |

## Why this is chemically cool
| Observation | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Rare earths appear as a broad trivalent family in SRD-46 | Their chemistry is systematic and comparable across the series |
| EDTA has measurements for many REE³⁺ ions | It serves as a benchmark ligand for comparing rare-earth binding |
| Some REE systems include ΔH and ΔS, not just logK | You can study not only strength, but also the driving force of binding |
| Oxalate data show both soluble complexes and very insoluble solids | This connects coordination chemistry directly to separation and precipitation chemistry |
