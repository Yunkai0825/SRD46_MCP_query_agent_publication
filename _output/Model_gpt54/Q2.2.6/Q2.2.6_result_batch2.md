# Q2.2.6 -- Result (batch 2)

**Section:** 2.2 -- Schema Discovery & Data Profiling

**Prompt:** How many metals in the database have fewer than 5 VLM measurements total? What fraction of all metals is that?

**Tool calls:** 4  |  **Time:** 79s planning + 15s execution + 11s verdict = 105s total

---

Using the SRD-46 counts provided:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Total metals in `metal_card` | 230 |
| Metals appearing in `ligandmetal_card` with **5 or more** measurements | 122 |
| Metals with **fewer than 5** total measurements | 108 |
| Fraction of all metals | 108 / 230 = 0.4696 |
| Percentage of all metals | 46.96% |

So, **108 metals** have **fewer than 5 VLM measurements total**, which is **about 47.0% of all metals** in the database.

A small note: this includes metals with **zero** measurements, since those do not appear in the grouped measurement table and are captured by subtracting from the full metal count. Source: **NIST SRD-46**.

---

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent fully answered the question. It reports 108 metals with fewer than 5 VLM measurements, equal to 46.96% of the 230 metals in the database.

**Explanation**  
Scientifically, this indicates the VLM dataset is quite sparse across the periodic table: nearly half of all metals have very limited coverage (<5 measurements), including some with none at all. That pattern means analyses based on VLM measurements are likely concentrated in a subset of better-studied metals, so broad cross-metal comparisons may be biased by uneven data availability.
