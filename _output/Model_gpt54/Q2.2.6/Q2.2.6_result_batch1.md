# Q2.2.6 -- Result (batch 1)

**Prompt:** How many metals in the database have fewer than 5 VLM measurements total? What fraction of all metals is that?

**Tool calls:** 10  |  **Time:** 104s planning + 78s execution + 13s verdict = 195s total

---

Using a direct SRD-46 SQL count derived from `metal_card` LEFT JOIN `eq_node` on `metal_id`:

| metric | value |
|---|---:|
| Total metals | 230 |
| Metals with fewer than 5 total VLM measurements | 114 |
| Fraction of all metals | 0.4956521739 |
| Percentage | 49.6% |

So, **114 metals** in the database have **fewer than 5 VLM measurements total**, which is **114/230 = 0.4957**, or **about 49.6%** of all metals.

This was computed by counting **distinct `vlm_id` values per metal** from the equilibrium data, using a **LEFT JOIN** so metals with zero measurements were included.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 (`metal_card` and `eq_node` via `execute_srd46_sql`).

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent fully answered the question. It provided both the count of metals with fewer than 5 VLM measurements and the fraction of all metals.

**Explanation**  
The data indicate that **114 of 230 metals** have **<5 total VLM measurements**, so nearly **half the database (49.6%)** is sparsely measured for VLMs. Scientifically, this suggests the VLM coverage across metals is highly uneven, with many metals having little or no measurement support; including metals with zero measurements is important because it captures true database sparsity.
