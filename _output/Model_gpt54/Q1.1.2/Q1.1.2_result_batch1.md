**Explanation**  
Scientifically, citric acid is triprotic, so three stepwise acid dissociation constants are expected, corresponding to loss of its three carboxylic protons. The reported values (2.90, 4.35, 5.65 at 25 °C, I = 0.1 M) are reasonable and show increasingly difficult deprotonation as negative charge builds, but pKa can shift with ionic strength, temperature, and measurement method, so multiple measured values may exist.
