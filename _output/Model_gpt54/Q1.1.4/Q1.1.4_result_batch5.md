A few important interpretation notes:

| Note | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `logK` for Ag(NH₃)⁺ | This is the **1:1 formation constant** for Ag⁺ + NH₃ ⇌ Ag(NH₃)⁺ |
| `logK` for Ag(NH₃)₂⁺ | In this SRD-46 entry, this corresponds to the **overall cumulative constant** for Ag⁺ + 2 NH₃ ⇌ Ag(NH₃)₂⁺ |
| No separate stepwise K₂ was directly listed in the retrieved catalog | So it should **not** be derived unless you specifically want a calculated estimate from β₂/β₁ |

Chemically, this is exactly the classic **silver diammine** system: Ag⁺ binds NH₃ strongly, and the **Ag(NH₃)₂⁺** complex is the dominant simple ammine species in aqueous solution. The much larger cumulative logK for the 1:2 complex is consistent with the well-known stability of Tollens-type silver–ammine coordination chemistry.

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent substantially answered the question by identifying the available Ag⁺–ammonia stability constants and related thermodynamic data. A minor gap is that it did not explicitly state whether only overall β values, not a directly tabulated stepwise \(K_2\), are available across all entries.

**Explanation**  
SRD-46 shows Ag⁺ forms two simple aqueous ammine complexes: Ag(NH\(_3\))\(^+\) and especially the more stable Ag(NH\(_3\))\(_2\)\(^+\). The reported log stability ranges indicate stronger cumulative binding for the diammine complex, while negative ΔH values show complexation is exothermic; data are mostly near 25 °C over a broad ionic-strength range, so constants vary somewhat with medium.
