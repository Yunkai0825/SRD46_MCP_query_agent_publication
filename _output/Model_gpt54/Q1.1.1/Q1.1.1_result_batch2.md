### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent largely answered the question, but it did not include all reported constants: SRD-46 shows 30 measurements, while only 18 log β entries were listed. So there is a gap if the user wanted every reported stability-related value.

**Explanation**  
For Cu(II) with glycine, SRD-46 supports two main stoichiometries: CuGly and CuGly₂, with CuGly₂ much more stable overall (log β about 14.7–16.3) than CuGly (about 8.0–8.8). The constants vary with temperature and ionic strength, so values should only be compared under matched conditions; thermodynamic entries also indicate complex formation is exothermic and entropically favorable.
