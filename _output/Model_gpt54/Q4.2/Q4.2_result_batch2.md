**Prompt:** Look at the stability constants for Hg²⁺ across different ligand classes. Can you form a hypothesis about whether Hg²⁺ prefers sulfur-donor, nitrogen-donor, or oxygen-donor ligands? Support your hypothesis with specific log K values from the database.

SRD-46 supports the hypothesis that **Hg²⁺ is strongly sulfur-loving (thiophilic)**: overall, **S-donor ligands > N-donor ligands > simple O-donor ligands**.

## Representative Hg²⁺ log K values from SRD-46

| Donor class | Ligand | Representative Hg²⁺ log K values from SRD-46 | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| S-donor | Thiourea | **HgL = 8.8**, **HgL₂ = 17.1**, **HgL₄ = 32.5** | Very strong Hg–S binding |
| S-donor | Thiocyanate | **HgL = 9.8**, **HgL₂ = 17.8**, **HgL₄ = 21.9** | Ambidentate ligand, but Hg²⁺ commonly favors S-coordination |
| S-donor | 2,2′-Thiodiethanol | **HgL = 11.9**, **HgL₂ = 21.6** | Strong S-containing donor |
| N-donor | Ethylenediamine | **HgL = 9.1**, **HgL₂ = 16.7** | Stronger than simple monodentate N ligands because of chelation |
| N-donor | Imidazole | **HgL = 6.9**, **HgL₂ = 11.6** | Moderate Hg²⁺ affinity |
| N-donor | Ammonia | **HgL = 2.3**, **HgL₂ = 4.0** | Weak compared with S-donors |
| N-donor | Pyridine | **HgL = 2.6**, **HgL₂ = 4.8** | Also much weaker than S-donors |
| O-donor | Hydrogen carbonate | **HgL = 3.2** | Relatively weak simple O-donor binding |
| O-donor / hydroxo | Hydroxide | **HgOH⁺ = 10.3**, **Hg(OH)₂ = 21.7** | Strong hydrolysis, but not a neutral O-donor comparison |
| Mixed O/N donor | EDTA | **HgL = 21.8** | Very high, but boosted by multidentate chelation; not a simple O-donor benchmark |

## Hypothesis

| Preference rank | Donor type | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sulfur donor** | Thiourea, thiocyanate, and thiodiethanol all give very large Hg²⁺ constants, with **HgL ≈ 8.8–11.9** and higher cumulative values up to **32.5** |
| 2 | **Nitrogen donor** | N-donors bind, but most are weaker: **ammonia 2.3**, **pyridine 2.6**, **imidazole 6.9**; chelating en is stronger at **9.1** |
| 3 | **Simple oxygen donor** | Simple O-donors such as carbonate are much weaker, e.g. **HgL = 3.2** |

## Conclusion

The SRD-46 numbers are most consistent with **Hg²⁺ preferring sulfur-donor ligands**. Nitrogen donors can bind moderately well, especially if they chelate, but simple oxygen donors are generally weaker. That pattern matches classic **HSAB** behavior: **Hg²⁺ is a soft acid**, so it binds best to **soft sulfur bases**.

Source: **NIST SRD-46**.
