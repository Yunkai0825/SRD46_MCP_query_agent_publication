| Preference order | Hypothesis |

### Verdict & Explanation

## Verdict
There are gaps. The answer gives a plausible preference order, but the cited numeric support is inconsistent: its strongest sulfur examples come from “broader” values rather than the explicitly comparable 1:1 constants, and one listed 1:1 nitrogen value exceeds the sulfur 1:1 example.

## Explanation
The database pattern still supports Hg²⁺ being **thiophilic** overall: sulfur ligands such as thiocyanate and thiourea show very large reported log K values (about 21.8 and 19.8 in the broader search), while common nitrogen donors like imidazole, ammonia, and pyridine are lower (~9.0, 7.2, 5.8). Oxygen donors are generally weakest or even unfavorable in simple cases, as shown by hydrogen carbonate with negative 1:1 log K values near -11.
