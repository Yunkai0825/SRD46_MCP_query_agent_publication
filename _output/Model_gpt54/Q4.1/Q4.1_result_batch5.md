## 3) Reasoned qualitative ranking

For **neutral ligands in water**, expected intrinsic binding to iron should follow donor hardness/chelation:

| Metal | Expected order among your targets |
|---|---|
| Fe²⁺ | ethylene glycol > DMF ≳ THF > acetonitrile |
| Fe³⁺ | ethylene glycol > DMF > THF > acetonitrile |

Why:
- **Ethylene glycol** can act as a **neutral O,O-chelate**, giving an entropic advantage over monodentate donors.
- **DMF** is a decent **carbonyl O donor**.
- **THF** is a weaker neutral ether O donor.
- **Acetonitrile** is usually a relatively weak ligand for hard aqueous Fe ions, especially versus water/hydrolysis competition.
- For **Fe³⁺**, hydrolysis competes so strongly in water that weak neutral ligands are often effectively suppressed unless the medium is strongly acidic or nonaqueous.
