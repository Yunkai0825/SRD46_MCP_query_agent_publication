There are gaps. The answer likely identifies many ligands with >6 protonation steps, but it reports an inferred count from network nodes rather than confirmed “reported pKa values,” so it may not exactly answer the database question as asked.

**Explanation**  
Scientifically, the listed compounds are the kinds of ligands expected to show many acid–base steps: polyamines, aminopolycarboxylates like DTPA, and polyphosphonate/phosphate ligands such as phytate. Their multiple basic or acidic sites produce long protonation ladders, but the numbers given are approximations from equilibrium-network size, not a direct count of distinct experimentally reported pKa entries.
