# Q2.1.5 -- Result (batch 5)

**Section:** 2.1 -- Comparison & Ranking

**Prompt:** Between ammonia and ethylenediamine, which ligand binds more strongly to Ni²⁺? Quantify the difference in log K.

**Tool calls:** 9  |  **Time:** 96s planning + 172s execution + 4s verdict = 272s total

---

Ethylenediamine binds **much more strongly** to Ni²⁺ than ammonia.

| Ligand | Ni²⁺ + L ⇌ NiL | log K at 25 °C, I = 0.1 M | log K at 25 °C, I = 0 M |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Ammonia | ML | 2.73 | 2.72 |
| Ethylenediamine | ML | 7.30 | 7.32 |

Using the directly matched **25 °C, 0.1 M** data:

| Comparison | Δlog K |
|---|---:|
| log K(Ni–en) − log K(Ni–NH₃) | 4.57 |

So, **ethylenediamine is stronger by about 4.6 log units**, which corresponds to roughly **10^4.57 ≈ 3.7 × 10⁴ times** larger formation constant for the 1:1 complex.

A short chemistry note: this is the classic **chelate effect**—ethylenediamine is bidentate, so it forms a more stable five-membered chelate ring with Ni²⁺, whereas ammonia is only monodentate.

*Source: NIST SRD-46, Ni²⁺ stability data for ammonia (ligand_10103) and ethylenediamine (ligand_7029), β definition [ML]/[M][L].*
