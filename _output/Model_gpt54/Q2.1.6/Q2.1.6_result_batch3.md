Using the SRD-46 equilibrium networks for **Hydrogen thiocyanate (thiocyanic acid), ligand_10092**, the comparable **1:1 formation constant** is the **[M] + [L] ⇌ [ML]** entry (**beta_def_812**, reported as logK).

| Metal ion | SRD-46 system | Conditions used | 1:1 logK |
|---|---|---|---:|
| Hg²⁺ | Hg^[2+] + Hydrogen thiocyanate | 20–30 °C, I ≈ 0.00–0.25 M | 9.64 |

| Pb²⁺ | Pb^[2+] + Hydrogen thiocyanate | 16.5–31.5 °C, I ≈ 0.78–1.23 M | 0.49 |

## Ranking
| Rank | Metal ion | logK |
|---|---|---:|
| 1 | Hg²⁺ | 9.64 |
| 2 | Cd²⁺ | 1.93 |
| 3 | Pb²⁺ | 0.49 |


**Hg²⁺ has by far the highest affinity** for thiocyanate/thiocyanic acid in SRD-46.

This ranking, **Hg²⁺ > Cd²⁺ > Pb²⁺**, is consistent with soft-acid/soft-base chemistry: **SCN⁻ is a soft donor ligand**, and **Hg²⁺** is the softest of the three metals, so it binds most strongly.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 equilibrium network data for ligand_10092 (Hydrogen thiocyanate / thiocyanic acid), beta_def_812.*

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent essentially answered the question: it ranked Cd²⁺, Pb²⁺, and Hg²⁺ by comparable 1:1 log K values and identified Hg²⁺ as highest-affinity. A minor caveat is that the cited measurements are not all at identical ionic strengths.

**Explanation**  
The SRD-46 data show a clear trend, **Hg²⁺ >> Cd²⁺ > Pb²⁺** (about 9.64, 1.93, 0.49), meaning mercury(II) binds thiocyanate far more strongly than cadmium(II) or lead(II). This fits HSAB chemistry because thiocyanate is a soft donor and Hg²⁺ is the softest, most polarizable metal ion of the three.
