# Q3.2.7 -- Result (batch 3)

**Section:** 3.2 -- Thermodynamic Reasoning

**Prompt:** Compare the stepwise constants K₁, K₂, K₃, K₄ for Cu²⁺–ammonia. Do they decrease monotonically? What does this tell you about the coordination geometry?

**Tool calls:** 10  |  **Time:** 96s planning + 53s execution + 4s verdict = 153s total

---

Using the matched low-ionic-strength reference network in SRD-46 for **Cu²⁺ + NH₃ at ~25 °C**, the reported **cumulative** constants are:

| Species | log β |
|---|---:|
| Cu(NH₃)²⁺ | 4.1 |
| Cu(NH₃)₂²⁺ | 7.4 |
| Cu(NH₃)₃²⁺ | 10.2 |
| Cu(NH₃)₄²⁺ | 12.3 |

From these, the **stepwise** constants are:

| Step | Relation | log K |
|---|---|---:|
| K₁ | log β₁ | 4.1 |
| K₂ | log β₂ − log β₁ | 3.3 |
| K₃ | log β₃ − log β₂ | 2.8 |
| K₄ | log β₄ − log β₃ | 2.1 |

So yes — **K₁ > K₂ > K₃ > K₄**, i.e. they **decrease monotonically** in this matched dataset.

What this suggests about geometry:

| Observation | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Smooth decrease from K₁ to K₄ | Normal statistical/crowding effect as more NH₃ ligands bind |
| Still substantial binding through K₄ | Cu(II) strongly favors formation of a **four-coordinate equatorial set** of NH₃ donors |
| No anomaly before the 4th ligand | Consistent with **square-planar / tetragonally distorted octahedral** Cu(II), rather than a simple regular octahedral picture |

Chemically, this is the classic **Jahn–Teller d⁹ Cu(II)** behavior: four ligands bind strongly in the equatorial plane, while any additional axial interactions are weaker. So the monotonic K₁–K₄ pattern, together with the persistence of significant binding up to the fourth ammonia, supports a **4-strong-bond equatorial coordination environment**.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46, Cu²⁺–ammonia reference equilibrium network (`ref_eq_net_28241`) and associated stability entries.

---

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**

The subagent largely answered the question fully: the stepwise constants decrease monotonically in the cited low-ionic-strength dataset, and the geometry interpretation is appropriate. A small caveat is that this conclusion is tied to those specific solution conditions.

**Explanation**

The data show log K values dropping from about 4.1 to 3.3 to 2.8 to 2.1, so each successive NH₃ binds less strongly than the previous one, consistent with normal crowding/statistical effects. For Cu(II), the fact that binding remains substantial through the fourth ligand supports four strong equatorial Cu–N bonds, characteristic of Jahn–Teller–distorted Cu(II) (square-planar/tetragonally elongated octahedral), with weaker axial interactions beyond four.
