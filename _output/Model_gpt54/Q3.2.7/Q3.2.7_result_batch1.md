# Q3.2.7 -- Result (batch 1)

**Section:** 3.2 -- Thermodynamic Reasoning

**Prompt:** Compare the stepwise constants K₁, K₂, K₃, K₄ for Cu²⁺–ammonia. Do they decrease monotonically? What does this tell you about the coordination geometry?

**Tool calls:** 8  |  **Time:** 128s planning + 42s execution + 21s verdict = 191s total

---

Using the matched **25 °C, aqueous, I = 0** SRD-46 cumulative constants for Cu²⁺ + NH₃:

| Species | log β |
|---|---:|
| Cu(NH₃)²⁺ | 4.02 |
| Cu(NH₃)₂²⁺ | 7.4 |
| Cu(NH₃)₃²⁺ | 10.2 |
| Cu(NH₃)₄²⁺ | 12.3 |

The stepwise constants are:

| Step | Relation | log K |
|---|---|---:|
| K₁ | log β₁ | 4.02 |
| K₂ | log β₂ − log β₁ | 3.38 |
| K₃ | log β₃ − log β₂ | 2.80 |
| K₄ | log β₄ − log β₃ | 2.10 |

So yes: **K₁ > K₂ > K₃ > K₄**, i.e. they **decrease monotonically** in this dataset.

What that suggests geometrically:

| Observation | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Smooth decrease from K₁ to K₄ | No special extra stabilization at the 4th ligand; each added NH₃ binds less strongly than the previous one, as expected from statistical/electrostatic crowding |
| Cu²⁺ still forms a substantial tetraammine complex | Four NH₃ ligands are strongly favored in the primary coordination plane |
| No “jump” at K₄ | This is more consistent with **Jahn–Teller-distorted Cu(II)** having a preferred **4 strong equatorial bonds** rather than a regular octahedral environment with six equivalent sites |

Overall, the constants support the usual picture for **Cu(II)–ammine**: a **tetragonally elongated octahedral / effectively square-planar equatorial core**, where **four NH₃ ligands occupy the strong equatorial positions** and any axial interactions are weaker.  
**Source: NIST SRD-46.**

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent fully answered the question. It correctly shows that K₁–K₄ decrease monotonically and links that pattern to Cu(II)’s preferred 4-strong-bond environment.

**Explanation**  
From the cumulative constants, the stepwise log K values drop smoothly (4.02, 3.38, 2.80, 2.10), so there is no anomalous stabilization at any one addition step. For Cu²⁺, that pattern is consistent with progressive crowding plus Jahn–Teller-distorted d⁹ coordination: four NH₃ ligands bind strongly in an equatorial set, while axial sites are weaker, giving an effectively square-planar core within an elongated octahedral geometry.
