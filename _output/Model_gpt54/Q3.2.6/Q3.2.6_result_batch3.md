**Section:** 3.2 -- Thermodynamic Reasoning

**Prompt:** Using pKa values for malonic acid and the Cu²⁺–malonate log K, estimate the conditional stability constant at pH 4. Explain your calculation.

Using the **matched 25 °C, I = 0.1 M** data from SRD-46:

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---:|
| pKa1 of malonic acid | 2.65 |
| pKa2 of malonic acid | 5.27 |
| log K for Cu²⁺ + L²⁻ ⇌ CuL | 5.04 |

Here **L²⁻ = malonate**. At **pH 4**, only a fraction of total malonate is present as \(L^{2-}\), so the **conditional** constant is

\[
K' = K\,\alpha_{L^{2-}}
\]

For diprotic malonic acid,

\[
\alpha_{L^{2-}}=
\frac{K_{a1}K_{a2}}{[H^+]^2+[H^+]K_{a1}+K_{a1}K_{a2}}
\]

with

\[
K_{a1}=10^{-2.65},\quad K_{a2}=10^{-5.27},\quad [H^+]=10^{-4}
\]

Numerically:

| Term | Value |
|---|---:|
| \(K_{a1}\) | \(2.24\times 10^{-3}\) |
| \(K_{a2}\) | \(5.37\times 10^{-6}\) |
| \(K_{a1}K_{a2}\) | \(1.20\times 10^{-8}\) |
| \([H^+]^2\) | \(1.00\times 10^{-8}\) |
| \([H^+]K_{a1}\) | \(2.24\times 10^{-7}\) |
| Denominator | \(2.46\times 10^{-7}\) |
| \(\alpha_{L^{2-}}\) | \(4.90\times 10^{-2}\) |

So only about **4.9%** of total malonate is in the binding form \(L^{2-}\) at pH 4.

Now:

\[
K' = 10^{5.04}\times 0.0490
\]

or in log form,

\[
\log K' = 5.04 + \log(0.0490) = 5.04 - 1.31 \approx 3.73
\]

## Estimated conditional stability constant at pH 4

| Constant | Value |
|---|---:|
| \(K'\) | \(\approx 5.4\times 10^3\) |
| \(\log K'\) | \(\approx 3.73\) |

So the **Cu²⁺–malonate conditional stability constant at pH 4 is about \(\log K' \approx 3.7\)**.

A useful interpretation is that protonation lowers the effective binding strength by about **1.3 log units** relative to the intrinsic Cu²⁺–malonate \( \log K = 5.04 \), because most malonate is still protonated at pH 4.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 data for malonic acid pKa and Cu²⁺–malonate stability constant.

**Verdict**  
The subagent fully answered the question and gave a reasonable estimate of the pH-4 conditional stability constant. The calculation and interpretation are complete.

**Explanation**  
Malonic acid is mostly not in the fully deprotonated malonate form at pH 4, so only about 4.9% is available as \(L^{2-}\) to bind Cu\(^{2+}\). Multiplying the intrinsic Cu–malonate stability (\(\log K \approx 5.04\)) by this fraction gives a conditional value \(\log K' \approx 3.73\) (\(K' \approx 5 \times 10^3\)). This shows proton competition substantially weakens effective binding at mildly acidic pH.
