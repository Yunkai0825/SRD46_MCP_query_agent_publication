## 2) What do the studied Zn²⁺ amino-acid systems imply?

The retrieved Zn²⁺ dataset shows a clear donor-type trend:

| Ligand | Donor character | Zn²⁺ dataset richness |
|---|---|---|
| Glycine | simple N,O | moderate |
| Alanine | simple N,O | moderate |
| Aspartic acid / Glutamic acid | harder O-rich | present, but not unusually rich |
| Serine / Threonine / Tyrosine | OH/phenol side chains | present, limited |

| Cysteine | thiolate S + N,O | richest/most favorable-looking among canonical amino acids retrieved |

This is chemically consistent with Zn²⁺ favoring **borderline-soft donor atoms**, especially **S** and good **N** donors, more than purely hard O-donor side chains.

## 4) Why methionine is the best unstudied prediction

Among canonical amino acids apparently lacking Zn²⁺ data in the retrieved set, **methionine** is the most plausible strongest binder because it is the only common missing residue with a **sulfur-containing side chain**.

| Unstudied candidate type | Expected Zn²⁺ affinity |
|---|---|
| Valine / Leucine / Isoleucine / Phenylalanine | mostly simple α-amino acid behavior; no strong extra donor |
| Proline | N,O chelation possible, but no especially strong side-chain donor |
| Lysine / Arginine | side chains are strongly protonated in water; poor Zn donor advantage near neutral pH |
| Tryptophan | indole is not a strong coordinating donor in water |
| **Methionine** | adds a **thioether sulfur**, which should enhance Zn²⁺ affinity over O/N-only amino acids |

The strongest evidence chain from SRD-46 is:

| Observation from retrieved data | Implication |
|---|---|
| Glycine/alanine are ordinary Zn²⁺ binders | simple N,O donors are baseline |
| Histidine is stronger-looking because of imidazole N | extra side-chain donor matters a lot |
| Cysteine is the standout sulfur-containing canonical ligand with rich Zn²⁺ complexation | sulfur donors are especially favorable for Zn²⁺ |
| Methionine is the obvious missing canonical sulfur amino acid | likely strongest among the unstudied residues |

## 5) Final prediction

| Predicted amino acid | Why |
|---|---|
| **Methionine** | It is the strongest plausible **unstudied canonical amino acid** because the retrieved Zn²⁺ trend favors side-chain donor atoms beyond simple N,O chelation, and sulfur-containing amino acids are the most favorable class in the observed dataset. Methionine’s thioether sulfur should give it an advantage over aliphatic, hydroxy, amide, aromatic, or strongly protonated basic side chains. |

## Bottom line

**Prediction: methionine**.

The reasoning is that SRD-46 shows Zn²⁺ complexes become notably stronger when amino acids offer **side-chain donor groups**, with **histidine (imidazole N)** and especially **cysteine (sulfur)** standing out over simple amino acids. Among the apparently unstudied canonical amino acids, **methionine is the best sulfur-containing candidate**, so it is the most likely to form the most stable Zn²⁺ complex.

### Verdict & Explanation

## Verdict
There is a gap: the subagent gives a plausible prediction, but it does not firmly establish that methionine is truly “not yet studied” with Zn²⁺ in the database, nor compare all missing amino acids with retrieved pKa data. So the answer is reasonable but not fully proven from the retrieved evidence.

## Explanation
The data support the main chemical trend that Zn²⁺ binds most strongly to amino acids with softer donor atoms, especially sulfur and imidazole nitrogen, with cysteine and histidine outperforming simple O/N donors. On that basis, methionine is a sensible unstudied candidate because its thioether sulfur should bind Zn²⁺ better than aliphatic, hydroxyl, amide, or strongly protonated basic side chains.
