### 2) The Zn-studied set establishes a donor-strength trend
The catalog/search results already indicate the trend:

| Ligand type in Zn data | Representative SRD-46 examples | Zn-binding implication |
|---|---|---|
| Simple amino/carboxylate | glycine, β-alanine, aminobutanoic acids | Baseline, moderate Zn binding |

That trend is chemically sensible for Zn²⁺: extra donor atoms, especially **anionic carboxylate O** or strong N/S donors, improve stability.

## Why aspartic acid is the best unstudied standard amino acid

Among the standard amino acids not seen in the Zn results you retrieved, **aspartic acid** stands out because its acid–base pattern should make it a particularly good Zn ligand in water.

| Feature | Aspartic acid | Relevance to Zn²⁺ |
|---|---|---|
| α-amino group | Yes | N donor |
| α-carboxylate | Yes | Strong anionic O donor |
| Side-chain carboxylate | Yes | Second anionic O donor |
| Expected deprotonation behavior from amino-acid pKa logic | Two acidic groups can be deprotonated before the amino group | Gives an anionic, multidentate donor set favorable for Zn²⁺ |
| Chelation | Likely O,N and possibly O,O/N-assisted modes | Higher stability than simple mono-carboxylate amino acids |

### Verdict & Explanation

**Explanation**
