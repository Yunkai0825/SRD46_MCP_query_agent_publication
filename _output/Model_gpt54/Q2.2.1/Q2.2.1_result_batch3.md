# Q2.2.1 -- Result (batch 3)

**Prompt:** What percentage of stability-constant measurements in the database were conducted at 25 °C?

**Tool calls:** 6  |  **Time:** 52s planning + 53s execution + 14s verdict = 119s total

---

Using SRD-46, there are **79,063** stability-constant measurement records in `ligandmetal_stability_measured`, and **67,237** of them have `temperature_c = 25`.

| Quantity | Count |
|---|---:|
| Total stability-constant measurements | 79,063 |
| Measurements at 25 °C | 67,237 |
| Percentage at 25 °C | 85.04% |

So, **about 85.0%** of stability-constant measurements in the database were conducted at **25 °C**.

| Note | Value |
|---|---|
| Temperature field used | `temperature_c` |
| Missing-temperature treatment | Percentage computed over **all** records, since a reliable `NOT NULL` count was not obtained from the count tool in this run |

A practical chemistry note: 25 °C is the standard reference temperature for much solution-equilibrium work, so such a strong concentration at 25 °C is expected and makes cross-study comparison much easier.

*Source: NIST SRD-46.*

**Verdict**  
There is a minor gap: the answer gives the percentage over all records, not specifically over records with known temperature. Still, it likely answers the user’s wording reasonably: about **85.0%** of measurements are recorded at **25 °C**.

**Explanation**  
The data indicate a strong concentration of stability-constant measurements at **25 °C** (**67,237 of 79,063**), showing that this is the dominant experimental temperature in the database. Scientifically, that makes sense because **25 °C** is the standard reference condition for many aqueous equilibrium studies, aiding comparison across experiments; however, missing temperature values could slightly affect the exact percentage among temperature-reported measurements.
