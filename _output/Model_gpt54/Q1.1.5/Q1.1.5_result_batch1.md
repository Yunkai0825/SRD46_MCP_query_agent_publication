### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
There are gaps. The subagent answered that oxalic acid is diprotic with two steps, but the reported pKa values are not scientifically reliable for ordinary oxalic acid behavior.

**Explanation**  
Oxalic acid has **two dissociation steps** because it is **H₂L**: \( \mathrm{H_2C_2O_4 \rightleftharpoons HC_2O_4^- \rightleftharpoons C_2O_4^{2-}} \). In aqueous chemistry, oxalic acid is known to have **two acidic protons**, with both deprotonations occurring at relatively low pH; a reported **negative first pKa** would be a major caveat and suggests condition-specific or misassigned constants rather than the usual aqueous pKa values.
