From SRD-46, **oxalic acid (ethanedioic acid)** is **diprotic**, so it has **2 dissociation steps**.

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | T (°C) | Ionic strength (M) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | H₂L ⇌ HL⁻ + H⁺ | -1.2 | 25 | 0.1 |
| 2 | HL⁻ ⇌ L²⁻ + H⁺ | 3.8 | 25 | 0.1 |

So, the number of dissociation steps is:

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---:|
| Dissociation steps | 2 |

A quick chemistry note: oxalic acid’s **second deprotonation** falls in the more familiar weak-acid range, while the reported **first pKa is very low**, indicating the fully protonated form is strongly acidic under these conditions. Source: **NIST SRD-46**.
