**Prompt:** Look up the pKa values for oxalic acid. How many dissociation steps are there?

Oxalic acid in SRD-46 is **ethanedioic acid (ligand_8872)** with ligand definition **H2L**, so it has **2 dissociation steps**:

| Step | Dissociation |
|---|---|
| 1 | H2L ⇌ HL⁻ + H⁺ |
| 2 | HL⁻ ⇌ L²⁻ + H⁺ |

The cataloged pKa brackets for this ligand are:

| Ligand | pKa1 | pKa2 | Dissociation steps |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Ethanedioic acid (Oxalic acid) | -1.2 | 3.8 | 2 |

SRD-46 also shows exactly **two protonation/deprotonation equilibria** for this H⁺/oxalic acid system:
- **[HL] + [H] ⇌ [H2L]**
- **[L] + [H] ⇌ [HL]**

So the answer is: **oxalic acid is diprotic and has 2 dissociation steps**.  
*Source: NIST SRD-46.*
