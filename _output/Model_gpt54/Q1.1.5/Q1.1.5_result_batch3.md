**Explanation**  
Oxalic acid (ethanedioic acid) is scientifically a **diprotic** acid, so it dissociates in **two steps**: H₂C₂O₄ ⇌ HC₂O₄⁻ ⇌ C₂O₄²⁻. Typical aqueous values near 25 °C are about **pKa₁ ≈ 1.2** and **pKa₂ ≈ 4.2**, showing the first proton is much more acidic than the second. The reported pair **3.80 and −1.20** appears inconsistent with standard oxalic-acid behavior.
