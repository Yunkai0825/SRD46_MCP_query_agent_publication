Using SRD-46 matched aqueous data at **25 °C** and **ionic strength 0.1 M**, glycine and alanine are very similar in acidity/basicity.

| Ligand | pKa (carboxyl-related step) | pKa (amino/ammonium-related step) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Glycine | 2.33 | 9.57 |
| Alanine | 2.33 | 9.71 |

| Comparison | ΔpKa (alanine − glycine) |
|---|---:|
| Carboxyl step | 0.00 |
| Amino/ammonium step | 0.14 |

**Conclusion:** the pKa values are **not significantly different overall**. The carboxyl pKa is identical in the matched SRD-46 records, and the amino-group pKa differs by only **0.14 units**, which is small and only slightly above typical experimental scatter for pKa measurements.

A reasonable chemical interpretation is that the **methyl group in alanine** makes the amino group just a bit more basic than in glycine, but the effect is modest. So for most practical aqueous speciation discussions, glycine and alanine behave **very similarly**, with alanine only slightly shifted toward the protonated amine.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 / IUPAC Stability Constants Database, matched pKa entries `vlm_93627`, `vlm_93606`, `vlm_94078`, `vlm_94058`; citations linked in SRD-46 include Bjerrum (1923) and later compilations.
