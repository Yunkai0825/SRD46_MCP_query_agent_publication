Using matched SRD-46 entries at **25 °C** and **ionic strength 0.1 M**, glycine and alanine are **very similar**, with only a small difference in the higher pKa.

| Ligand | Lower pKa | Higher pKa | Conditions |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Glycine | 2.33 | 9.57 | 25 °C, 0.1 M |
| Alanine | 2.33 | 9.71 | 25 °C, 0.1 M |

| Comparison | ΔpKa (Alanine − Glycine) | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Lower pKa | 0.00 | Essentially identical |
| Higher pKa | 0.14 | Small difference; alanine is slightly higher |

So: **no, they are not significantly different in a broad chemical sense**. Alanine’s amino-group deprotonation is only modestly less acidic than glycine’s, consistent with the methyl group in alanine being slightly electron-donating.

## Explanation

Scientifically, a 0.14 pKa-unit shift is small and not a major difference for most purposes.
