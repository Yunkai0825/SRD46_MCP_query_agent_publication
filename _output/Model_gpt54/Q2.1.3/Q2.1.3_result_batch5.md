| Ligand | pKa (HL → H2L) | pKa (L → HL) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Glycine | 2.33 | 9.57 |
| Alanine | 2.33 | 9.71 |

Difference:

| Comparison | ΔpKa |
|---|---:|
| Carboxyl deprotonation term (reported as HL → H2L) | 0.00 |
| Ammonium deprotonation term (reported as L → HL) | 0.14 |

**Conclusion:** glycine and alanine are **very similar**, not dramatically different. The carboxyl-group pKa is identical in the matched SRD-46 entries, and the amino-group pKa differs by only **0.14 units**, which is small and around the scale where ionic strength/method differences often matter. So I would say they are **not significantly different in a practical sense**, though alanine is **slightly more basic** at the amino group.

The small increase for alanine is chemically reasonable: its methyl substituent is weakly electron-donating, which slightly stabilizes the protonated amine relative to glycine.
