SRD-46 indicates that **EDTA forms the more stable 1:1 complex with Fe³⁺** than **NTA**.

| Ligand | Fe³⁺ complex | log K |
|---|---|---:|
| EDTA | Fe³⁺–EDTA | 25.1 |
| NTA | Fe³⁺–NTA | 15.9 |

**Conclusion:** **EDTA** is markedly stronger, by about **9.2 log units**.

A difference that large means Fe³⁺ binds EDTA far more tightly than NTA under comparable definition of the overall 1:1 formation constant. In coordination chemistry terms, that is consistent with EDTA’s higher denticity and stronger chelate effect.

*Source: NIST SRD-46.*
