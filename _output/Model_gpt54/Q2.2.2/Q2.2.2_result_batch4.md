### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
The subagent fully answered the question. It identified **H⁺** as having the most distinct ligands studied in SRD-46 and also provided the chemically useful ranking excluding protonation.

**Explanation**  
In SRD-46, counting **distinct ligand identities** shows a strong concentration of data around a few common ions: **H⁺ (4081)** far exceeds all others, with **Cu²⁺ (1951)** the top conventional metal ion, followed by **Ni²⁺, Zn²⁺, and Co²⁺**. The main caveat is that **H⁺** reflects protonation equilibria rather than metal coordination chemistry, so scientists often focus on the “excluding H⁺” ranking when comparing metal–ligand study breadth.
