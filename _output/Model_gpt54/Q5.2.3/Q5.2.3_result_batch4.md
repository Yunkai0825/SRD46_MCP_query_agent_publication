### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
There is a gap: the subagent did not provide stability constants because the chemical system was unspecified. It correctly noted that constants cannot be given from temperature and pressure alone.

**Explanation**  
Stability constants are equilibrium constants for a particular metal–ligand reaction, so values at 200 °C and 50 bar depend entirely on which ion, ligand, stoichiometry, and often ionic strength are involved. Scientifically, high-temperature/high-pressure data can exist for some systems, but coverage is often sparse, so even after specifying the system, values may be limited or unavailable.
