# Q2.1.4 -- Result (batch 1)

**Section:** 2.1 -- Comparison & Ranking

**Prompt:** Compare the stability constants of Cu²⁺ with chloride ion vs bromide ion. Which halide forms a stronger copper complex?

**Tool calls:** 10  |  **Time:** 109s planning + 60s execution + 10s verdict = 178s total

---

Using the **1:1 complex** definition **β₁ = [ML]/([M][L])** for **Cu²⁺ + X⁻ ⇌ CuX⁺**, SRD-46 shows that **chloride forms the stronger Cu(II) complex than bromide** under comparable aqueous conditions at **25 °C**.

| Ligand | β definition | logK at 25 °C, I = 0 M | logK at 25 °C, I = 2 M | logK at 25 °C, I = 3 M | logK at 25 °C, I = 4 M |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Chloride ion | β₁ = [ML]/[M][L] | 0.20 | -0.10 | 0.00 | 0.11 |
| Bromide ion | β₁ = [ML]/[M][L] | -0.04 | -0.49 | -0.40 | -0.22 |

So the direct matched-condition comparison is:

| Condition | logK(CuCl⁺) | logK(CuBr⁺) | Stronger complex |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 25 °C, I = 0 M | 0.20 | -0.04 | Chloride |
| 25 °C, I = 2 M | -0.10 | -0.49 | Chloride |
| 25 °C, I = 3 M | 0.00 | -0.40 | Chloride |
| 25 °C, I = 4 M | 0.11 | -0.22 | Chloride |

**Conclusion:** **Cl⁻ binds Cu²⁺ more strongly than Br⁻** for the 1:1 CuX⁺ complex in the SRD-46 data.

A small chemistry note: both constants are fairly small, so these are **weak Cu(II)–halide complexes** in water. The trend **Cl⁻ > Br⁻** is consistent with Cu²⁺ being a relatively hard/borderline Lewis acid, which generally favors the less polarizable halide more than bromide.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 equilibrium data for Cu²⁺ with chloride ion (ligand_10163) and bromide ion (ligand_10168), β₁ = [ML]/[M][L].*
