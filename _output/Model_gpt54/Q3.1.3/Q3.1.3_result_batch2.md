### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**  
There are gaps. The answer gives one SRD-46 citation and several Zn–histidine constants, but it does not establish whether this is your colleague’s ~2005 paper or identify any 2005-era citation.

**Explanation**  
The retrieved SRD-46 data indicate Zn²⁺ binds histidine strongly, with stepwise formation of ZnL (logK ~6.3–7.1) and especially ZnL₂ (logβ₂ ~11.7–12.7), while protonated complexes ZnHL and ZnHL₂ are also significant. Stability varies with ionic strength and temperature, so constants from different conditions are not directly interchangeable; the cited 1999 dataset at 25 °C, I = 0.1 M reports ZnL 6.51 and ZnL₂ 12.04.
