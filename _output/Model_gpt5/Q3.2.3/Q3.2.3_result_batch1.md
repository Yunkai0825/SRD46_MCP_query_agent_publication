### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
Gap present — the subagent provided a rough pH (~4–5) based on oxalic acid pKa2 but did not use the Ca–oxalate stability/solubility constants the question asked to incorporate.

Explanation
Oxalic acid is diprotic; the oxalate dianion (C2O4²−) becomes appreciable near pH ≈ pKa2 and increases ~10× per pH unit, so precipitation tends to begin in that region. Calcium oxalate forms when [Ca²⁺][C2O4²−] exceeds Ksp and can be modulated by aqueous complexation and ionic strength. Therefore, the precise onset pH depends on Ksp and [Ca²⁺]; without those constants, pH ~4–5 is only qualitative.
