Here’s a compact set of constants at 25 °C from SRD‑46 that control CaC2O4(s) formation:

| Quantity | Value (log10 K) | Medium (I, M) | Notes | SRD‑46 ref |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| pKa1 (H2C2O4 ⇌ HC2O4− + H+) | 1.2 | 0.1 | Oxalic acid, first dissociation | ligand_8872 (pKa set) |
| pKa2 (HC2O4− ⇌ C2O4^2− + H+) | 3.8 | 0.1 | Oxalic acid, second dissociation | ligand_8872 (pKa set) |
| Ca^2+ + C2O4^2− ⇌ CaC2O4(aq) | 3.19 | 0 | Aqueous 1:1 complex | vlm_151599, beta_def_812 |
| CaC2O4·H2O(s) ⇌ Ca^2+ + C2O4^2− + H2O | −8.75 | 0 | Monohydrate dissolution (Ksp ≈ 1.8×10^−9) | vlm_151608, beta_def_327 |
| CaC2O4·3H2O(s) ⇌ Ca^2+ + C2O4^2− + 3H2O | −8.32 | 0 | Trihydrate dissolution | vlm_151610, beta_def_307 |

Rule-of-thumb from these data:
- Precipitation requires the free-ion product [Ca^2+][C2O4^2−] ≳ Ksp. Because oxalate becomes the dianion mainly above its second pKa (pKa2 ≈ 3.8), the C2O4^2− fraction increases steeply across pH 3–4.
- For equimolar totals C_T of Ca and oxalate (and ignoring activity corrections), precipitation is impossible if C_T < √Ksp ≈ 4×10^−5 M (~40 μM). Above this, the “onset pH” satisfies α2(pH) ≈ Ksp / C_T^2, where α2 is the fraction of total oxalate present as C2O4^2−.
  - Example: at C_T = 0.1 mM, Ksp/C_T^2 ≈ 0.18, which corresponds to pH ≈ 3.2–3.3; at C_T = 1 mM it’s ≈ pH ~1–2; at very dilute levels (≤10 μM) precipitation cannot occur regardless of pH.

Bottom line (typical dilute aqueous conditions): As pH rises past the second pKa, calcium oxalate precipitation becomes increasingly favored; for sub-millimolar to millimolar solutions it is typically significant by pH ≈ 4 (±0.5), with the exact threshold shifting lower at higher concentrations and higher at lower concentrations. The Ca–oxalate aqueous complex (log K ≈ 3.19) moderately buffers free ions but does not eliminate this pH trend at low supersaturation.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critical Stability Constants of Metal Complexes), entries for oxalic acid (ligand_8872) and Ca^2+–oxalate (beta_def_812, beta_def_307, beta_def_327).

Explanation
