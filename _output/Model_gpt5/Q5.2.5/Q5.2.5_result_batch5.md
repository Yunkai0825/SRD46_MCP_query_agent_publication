Explanation
The Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ standard reduction potential is +0.337 V versus the standard hydrogen electrode at 25 °C with unit activity (≈1 M) for Cu²⁺ and pure Cu(s). Its positive value indicates Cu²⁺ is thermodynamically easier to reduce than H⁺ under standard conditions, explaining why copper metal is not oxidized by non-oxidizing acids. Reported values may vary slightly (~+0.34 V) with temperature and ionic strength.
