# Q5.2.5 -- Result (batch 3)

**Section:** 5.2 -- Negative / Edge-Case

**Prompt:** What is the electrode potential of Cu²⁺/Cu⁰?

**Tool calls:** 16  |  **Time:** 143s planning + 258s execution + 9s verdict = 410s total

---

| Redox couple | E° (V vs SHE, 25 °C) | Conditions |
|---|---:|---|
| Cu2+(aq) + 2e− → Cu(s) | +0.337 | a(Cu2+) = 1 (infinite dilution, I → 0), p = 1 bar; Cu(s) activity = 1 |

Source: NIST SRD‑46 literature notes (Pourbaix/Cebelcor reports; e.g., Cebelcor Rapp. Tech. series, 1955–58; internal refs such as 55ZD [lit_1159]).

Notes:
- This is the true standard potential for Cu2+/Cu(s) in aqueous solution at 25 °C vs the standard hydrogen electrode (SHE).
- A positive E° indicates metallic copper is relatively noble; in non‑standard conditions (e.g., different [Cu2+] or complexing media), the observed potential shifts according to the Nernst equation.

---

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
The subagent fully answered the question.

Explanation
SRD‑46 reports the standard potential E°(Cu2+/Cu) = +0.337 V vs SHE at 25 °C under true standard-state conditions (aCu2+ = 1, I → 0, p = 1 bar, Cu(s) activity = 1). The positive value indicates copper is relatively noble. In real solutions, the observed potential shifts with Cu2+ activity, ionic strength, complexation (e.g., chloride, ammonia), and temperature per the Nernst equation.
