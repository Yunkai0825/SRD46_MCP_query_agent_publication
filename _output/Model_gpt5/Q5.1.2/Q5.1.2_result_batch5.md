### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
No; the subagent did not fully answer the question, providing no binding constant values and instead requesting more specifics.

Explanation
For DTPA, “binding constant” usually means the overall formation constant (log beta) for a specific metal-ligand stoichiometry, which varies strongly with the metal and with temperature, ionic strength, and pH. DTPA complexes are much more stable with trivalent ions (e.g., lanthanides, Fe3+, Al3+) than with common divalent ions (Ca2+, Mg2+, Zn2+), and apparent (conditional) constants drop at lower pH due to proton competition. SRD-46 reports metal-specific log beta values under defined conditions, so the metal and conditions must be specified.
