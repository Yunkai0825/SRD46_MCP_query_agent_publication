Explanation
DTPA forms very stable 1:1 aqueous complexes (β1, reported as logβ1) with many metals; SRD‑46 lists numerous values near 25 °C and I ≈ 0.1 M. Stability increases markedly with metal charge: trivalent/tetravalent ions (e.g., Fe3+, Al3+, lanthanides, Th4+) show very large logβ1, while divalent ions (e.g., Ca2+, Mg2+) are much lower; transition divalents (Cu2+, Ni2+, Zn2+) are intermediate to high. Apparent constants depend on pH and ionic strength due to ligand protonation and metal hydrolysis.
