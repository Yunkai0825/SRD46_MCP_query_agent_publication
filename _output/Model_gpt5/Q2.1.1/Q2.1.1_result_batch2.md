**Prompt:** Between EDTA and NTA, which ligand forms the more stable complex with Fe³⁺? Show the log K values side by side.

Here are matched 1:1 formation constants (log β1 for [ML]/[M][L]) for Fe3+ with EDTA and NTA at 25 °C in aqueous solution:

| Condition (25 °C; I, M) | Fe3+–EDTA (log β1) | Fe3+–NTA (log β1) |
|---|---|---|
| I = 0.1 | 25.10 (VLM: vlm_108639) | 15.90 (VLM: vlm_105553) |
| I = 1.0 | 23.80 (VLM: vlm_108640) | 14.78 (VLM: vlm_105554) |

Answer: EDTA forms the more stable Fe3+ complex. At matched conditions, EDTA’s β1 is ~9 log units higher than NTA (≈10^9-fold stronger), and the gap persists across ionic strengths.

Notes:
- These are β1 values for the 1:1 complex (beta_def_812) at 25 °C; ionic strength as shown.
- The larger stability for EDTA is consistent with its higher denticity and stronger chelate effect relative to NTA.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD‑46).
