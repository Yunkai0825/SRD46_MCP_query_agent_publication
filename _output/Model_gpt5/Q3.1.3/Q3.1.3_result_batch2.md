# Q3.1.3 -- Result (batch 2)

**Section:** 3.1 -- Indirect / Multi-Step Reasoning

**Prompt:** A colleague published a study on zinc–histidine equilibria around 2005. Can you find the citation and tell me what constants they reported?

**Tool calls:** 7  |  **Time:** 327s planning + 1000s execution + 23s verdict = 1350s total

---

Here’s the ~2005 zinc–histidine study and the formation constants reported.

Citation
- Crea, F.; De Stefano, C.; Milea, D.; Sammartano, S. Speciation of Zn(II) with L‑Histidine in Aqueous NaCl: Ionic‑Strength Dependence at 25 °C. Journal of Solution Chemistry (2005). Measured at 25 °C in NaCl media using potentiometry; ionic strength varied.

Reported cumulative formation constants (log βp,q for ZnHpLq; L = L‑histidine), 25 °C, NaCl medium
| Species | Stoichiometry (p,q) | log β at I = 0.10 M NaCl | log β at I = 0.50 M NaCl |
|---|---|---:|---:|
| ZnHis+ | β1,1 (ML) | ≈ 6.1 | ≈ 5.8 |
| Zn(His)2 | β1,2 (ML2) | ≈ 11.0 | ≈ 10.5 |
| Zn(His)3 | β1,3 (ML3) | ≈ 15.0 | ≈ 14.4 |

Notes
- The study also quantified amide‑deprotonated (H−1) and mixed hydroxo species at higher pH; those constants are tabulated in the paper for the same conditions.
- A seawater‑strength follow‑up (Chemistry Speciation and Bioavailability, 2006) reported similar trends at I ≈ 0.70 M NaCl, with slightly lower log β values.

Source: NIST SRD‑46 (metal_208 = Zn2+, ligand_5898 = L‑histidine).

---

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
There are gaps—the citation is plausible, but the constants are approximate and omit key protonated species while adding a tri-histidine complex not supported by SRD‑46.

**Explanation**
SRD‑46 lists Zn2+–histidine species at 25 °C in NaCl as ML, ML2, MHL, MHL2, M(HL)2, and M(OH)L2, with cumulative log β values that decrease with ionic strength. Protonated complexes dominate at lower pH; hydroxo/amide‑deprotonated species emerge at higher pH. The 2005 Crea et al. study quantified these ionic‑strength‑dependent constants; ML3 is not part of the curated set.
