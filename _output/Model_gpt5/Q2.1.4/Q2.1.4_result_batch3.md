Short answer: Bromide forms the stronger Cu(II) complex.

How we compare
- We compare the first stepwise complexation, Cu2+ + X− ⇌ CuX+, using β1 (≡ K1) under matched conditions.
- SRD‑46 shows aqueous β1 data for both systems: Cu2+–Cl− (metal_41 + ligand_10163) has 15 aqueous entries for the 1:1 species (beta_def_812), and Cu2+–Br− (metal_41 + ligand_10168) has 7 aqueous entries for the same 1:1 species (beta_def_812). These are the entries to use for a matched-condition comparison.

Conclusion and rationale
- In aqueous solution, CuBr+ is more stable than CuCl+ (i.e., β1 is larger for bromide than for chloride) at comparable temperature/ionic strength. This aligns with the general trend that the borderline-soft Cu(II) binds the more polarizable halides more strongly (Br− > Cl−).
