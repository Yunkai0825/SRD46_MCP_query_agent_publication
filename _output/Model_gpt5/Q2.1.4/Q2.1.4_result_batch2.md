### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
No gaps. The subagent directly compares Cu2+–Cl− and Cu2+–Br− stability constants at matched conditions and correctly concludes chloride forms the stronger complex.

Explanation
In water at 25 °C, the stepwise 1:1 formation constants (log β1) are small, indicating weak binding: chloride shows log β1 ≈ −0.2 to +0.2, while bromide is lower (≈ −0.64 to −0.04) across ionic strengths 0–4 M, including I→0 values. Thus, Cu2+ binds slightly more strongly to chloride than bromide. This pattern is chemically consistent with Cu(II), a borderline acid, preferring the harder halide (Cl−) over the softer Br−.
