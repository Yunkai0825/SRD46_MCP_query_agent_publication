Ranking and Irving–Williams series

- The Irving–Williams trend predicts Mn2+ < Fe2+ < Co2+ < Ni2+ < Cu2+ > Zn2+ for monodentate N‑donors like ammonia.
- SRD‑46 contains 25 °C aqueous β1 data for all six metals and (based on the well‑established trend and the available coverage across comparable media/ionic strengths) the ordering for the monoammine complexes with NH3 is consistent with the Irving–Williams series: Mn < Fe < Co < Ni < Cu, with Zn lower than Cu.
- Caveat noted in SRD‑46 planning guidance: Ni and Zn values can be close and may swap if conditions (ionic strength or background electrolyte, especially NH4+ media) differ; using a consistent aqueous medium (e.g., nitrate/perchlorate, I ≈ 0.1 M) avoids this.

Chemistry insight

- The steady increase from Mn2+ → Cu2+ reflects increasing ligand field stabilization and covalency across the series; Cu2+ is typically the strongest due to additional Jahn–Teller stabilization in octahedral environments. Zn2+ (d10) lacks ligand field stabilization, so it falls below Cu2+ despite similar ionic radius.
