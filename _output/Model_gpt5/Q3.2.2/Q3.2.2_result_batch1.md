# Q3.2.2 -- Result (batch 1)

**Section:** 3.2 -- Thermodynamic Reasoning

**Prompt:** For the Ni²⁺–ethylenediamine system, how does the stability constant change with ionic strength? Is there a clear trend?

**Tool calls:** 6  |  **Time:** 363s planning + 300s execution + 24s verdict = 686s total

---

Short answer: Only a very weak ionic‑strength dependence is expected and observed; there is no clear strong monotonic trend.

What SRD‑46 contains
- System resolved: Ni2+ (metal_112) with ethylenediamine, en (ligand_7029).
- Three overall formation equilibria are covered: β1 [ML], β2 [ML2], β3 [ML3]; 45 conditional measurements clustered at ~20–30 °C with ionic strength spanning near 0 up to ~3 M in aqueous media (multiple background electrolytes).

Trend assessment
- Theory: en is neutral, so M2+ + nL0 ⇌ MLn2+ has the same net charge on both sides (Δ∑z² ≈ 0). Debye–Hückel/SIT therefore predicts minimal ionic‑strength dependence of log β for ML, ML2, and ML3.
- SRD‑46 notes for this pair are consistent with that expectation: reported Ni–en log β values in common aqueous media (e.g., NaClO4, NaNO3) change only slightly from low ionic strength to roughly 1 M; medium‑specific effects can be as large as, or larger than, the small I‑effects.
- Practical implication: Within a single background electrolyte, log β for Ni(en)n2+ is nearly constant with I (small drifts at most). Comparing across different electrolytes can obscure any weak slope because medium‑specific interactions shift the baseline.

Conclusion
- There is no strong, clear monotonic change of the Ni2+–ethylenediamine stability constants with ionic strength. Treat the I‑dependence as weak; compare data within a single medium, or convert to I → 0 (e.g., SIT/Davies) if cross‑medium comparison is required.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD‑46), Ni2+–ethylenediamine catalog (metal_112, ligand_7029) and associated measurement coverage.

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
The subagent adequately answered the question: the Ni2+–ethylenediamine stability constants show only weak dependence on ionic strength and no clear monotonic trend.

Explanation
Because ethylenediamine is neutral, M2+ + nL0 ⇌ MLn2+ has Δ∑z² ≈ 0, so Debye–Hückel/SIT predicts minimal ionic-strength effects. SRD‑46 entries spanning ~0–3 M (at ~20–30 °C) show log β for ML, ML2, and ML3 is nearly flat within a given electrolyte, with only small drifts. Medium-specific interactions (e.g., NaClO4 vs NaNO3) can shift baselines by amounts comparable to or larger than the weak I-dependence.
