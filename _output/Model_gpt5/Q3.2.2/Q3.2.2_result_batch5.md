Short answer: The Ni2+–ethylenediamine system shows only a weak dependence of the (apparent) stability constants on ionic strength, and a single, strong monotonic trend is not evident. In SRD‑46 there is extensive coverage for Ni2+ (metal_112) with ethylenediamine (ligand_7029), and across studies at 25 °C in water the reported log K (or log β) values within a given supporting electrolyte typically vary only modestly as ionic strength increases. The small sensitivity arises because en is neutral and the reactions Ni2+ + en ⇌ [Ni(en)]2+, [Ni(en)]2+ + en ⇌ [Ni(en)2]2+, etc., have no net change in charge; therefore, the primary Debye–Hückel ionic‑strength term largely cancels, leaving only small, medium‑dependent residual effects.

What this means in practice:
- Within a single medium (e.g., NaClO4 or KNO3) at ~25 °C, the conditional constants for Ni–en complexes tend to be nearly flat with I, showing only slight shifts that can differ by medium (and are often comparable to experimental scatter).
- Apparent decreases with I may be seen in some electrolytes, but the effect is small relative to systems where the reaction changes net charge.

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
Partial. The subagent gives a correct qualitative conclusion (weak, medium-dependent dependence; no clear universal trend) but does not provide quantitative log K/log β versus ionic strength data to substantiate the trend.

Explanation
For Ni2+–ethylenediamine, formation steps involve no net change in charge (e.g., Ni2+ + en ⇌ [Ni(en)]2+), so Debye–Hückel ionic-strength effects largely cancel. Consequently, conditional stability constants vary only slightly with ionic strength within a given supporting electrolyte at 25 °C. Residual, medium-specific activity and specific-ion effects can cause small shifts (sometimes slight decreases), typically comparable to experimental scatter, so no single monotonic trend is universally observed.
