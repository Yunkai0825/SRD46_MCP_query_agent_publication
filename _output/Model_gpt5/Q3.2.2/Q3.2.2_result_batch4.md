Here are the 25 °C concentration-based cumulative formation constants (logβ) for Ni2+ + en ⇌ Ni(en)2+ (β1) and Ni2+ + 2 en ⇌ Ni(en)2+2 (β2) as a function of ionic strength (I), from a single Ni2+–ethylenediamine series in SRD‑46:

| Ionic strength, I (M) | logβ1 (Ni(en)2+) | logβ2 (Ni(en)2+2) |
|---|---:|---:|
| 0.0 | 7.32 | 13.50 |
| 0.1 | 7.30 | 13.44 |
| 0.5 | 7.45 | 13.70 |
| 1.0 | 7.56 | 13.95 |
| 2.0 | 7.90 | −14.77  |
| 3.0 | 7.87 | 14.53 |

Notes:
- All entries are at ~25 °C and aqueous; the summary here does not display the background electrolyte, but the constants come from one internal “ref_eq_map” series in SRD‑46.
- The β2 value at I = 2.0 M appears anomalous (negative logβ reported). Treat this as a likely outlier or transcription/quality issue pending inspection of the original citation.

Answer to your question:
- Yes—within this dataset there is a clear upward trend of stability with ionic strength for both β1 and β2 at 25 °C. For β1, logβ increases modestly from ~7.3 at I ≈ 0–0.1 M to ~7.9 at 2–3 M (≈ +0.5–0.6 log units). For β2, excluding the anomalous 2.0 M entry, logβ rises from ~13.5 at I ≈ 0–0.1 M to ~14.5 at 3 M (≈ +1.0 log unit).
- The increase is roughly monotonic up to 1 M for both β1 and β2; above 1 M the trend persists but is more gradual. Because en is neutral and the overall charge is the same on both sides of the equilibria, simple Debye–Hückel charge-balance arguments would predict weak I-dependence; the observed positive slope likely reflects medium/specific-ion effects and residual activity-coefficient differences for 2+ species.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (IUPAC Stability Constants), Ni2+–ethylenediamine entries at 25 °C.

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
Largely complete—within the reported 25 °C series, β1 and β2 increase with ionic strength, aside from one anomalous β2 point at 2.0 M.

**Explanation**
Concentration-based logβ1 rises from ~7.3 at I≈0–0.1 M to ~7.9 at 2–3 M, and logβ2 from ~13.5 to ~14.5 by 3 M; increases are roughly monotonic up to 1 M and then gradual. Because ethylenediamine is neutral and charges balance, a small I-dependence is expected; the observed positive slope likely reflects activity-coefficient and medium/specific-ion effects. Trends may vary with electrolyte; the 2.0 M β2 value appears spurious.
