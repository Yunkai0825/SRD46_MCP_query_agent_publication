Interpretation
- For the 1:1 complex (ML), the apparent formation constant at 25 °C varies only modestly (Δlogβ ≲ 0.6) over I = 0–3 M, which indicates no strong monotonic ionic‑strength dependence in the aggregated SRD‑46 data.
- For ML₂ and ML₃, the database aggregates show very large scatter (including implausible negatives when interpreted as overall β), which likely reflects mixing of different constant definitions (stepwise vs overall), differing media, and cross‑study variability. From these pooled values, a clear trend with ionic strength cannot be established.

Chemistry insight
- The ML, ML₂, and ML₃ reactions each carry a +2 cation on both sides (neutral en), so net ionic charge change is near zero; accordingly, activity‑coefficient effects (and thus ionic‑strength dependence) are expected to be modest for the 1:1 complex. Any residual I‑dependence often comes from background electrolyte and ligand protonation corrections rather than strong electrostatic effects.

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
The subagent fully answered: Ni(en)²⁺ shows only modest variation with ionic strength and no clear monotonic trend; for ML₂/ML₃, trends cannot be resolved from heterogeneous data.

Explanation
At 25 °C, logβ for Ni(en)²⁺ spans ~7.3–7.9 from I ≈ 0–3 M, indicating weak ionic‑strength dependence. This aligns with near‑zero net charge change, so activity‑coefficient effects are small. Reported ML₂/ML₃ constants vary widely across media/definitions, precluding a consistent I‑trend; residual shifts likely reflect electrolyte and protonation corrections rather than strong electrostatics.
