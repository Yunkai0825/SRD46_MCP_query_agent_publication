### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
Partial. The subagent offers a plausible prediction (Asp > Glu) but does not establish which amino acids truly lack Zn²⁺ stability data in SRD‑46, leaving the “not yet studied” condition unverified.

**Explanation**
Zn²⁺ is a borderline Lewis acid that binds strongest to thiolate and imidazole donors (Cys, His). Excluding those, dicarboxylates (Asp, Glu) can form O,O-chelates once their low‑pKa carboxylates deprotonate; Asp may edge Glu via a 5‑membered ring and slightly lower side‑chain pKa. Nonetheless, absolute β values depend on pH/ionic strength, and carboxylate complexes are generally weaker than S/N-bound analogs.
