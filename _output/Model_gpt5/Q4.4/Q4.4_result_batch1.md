**Explanation**
Zn(II) favors softer donors; SRD‑46 shows stronger complexes for cysteine (thiolate) and histidine (imidazole) and modest ones for N,O-only amino acids (e.g., glycine logK≈4.96). Among amino acids without documented Zn(II) data, methionine’s thioether S should outperform N,O-only and phenolate-dependent ligands near pH 6–8. Key caveat: thioether S is weaker than thiolate, and stability depends on pH and ionic strength.
