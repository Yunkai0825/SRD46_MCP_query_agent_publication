- Our Zn²⁺ system catalog snapshot includes strong donors already studied: histidine (imidazole N; ligand_5898) and cysteine (thiolate S; ligand_5856), each showing multiple MLₙ species and hydroxo equilibria, consistent with high stability for soft/borderline donors.
- Glycine and alanine (simple amino acids) are also covered and form ML, ML₂, ML₃ species with Zn²⁺, illustrating the common N,O chelation motif.
- Among remaining canonical amino acids, aspartate/glutamate uniquely have a second carboxylate with low pKa (3.71 for Asp; 4.15 for Glu), ensuring anionic donors at neutral pH and enabling bidentate/tridentate coordination with the backbone amine. In contrast, tyrosine’s phenolic donor requires pH ≥10; serine/threonine are weak neutral donors; methionine’s thioether is softer but neutral and generally weaker than cysteine’s thiolate; lysine/arginine side chains are cationic and poor donors at neutral pH.

Chemistry insight:
- Zn²⁺ is a borderline hard/soft Lewis acid that displays strong affinity for anionic donors and chelates. With histidine (N) and cysteine (S) already covered, the next best readily accessible donor set among natural amino acids is the di‑carboxylate of aspartate, which can chelate via O,O (and optionally N) at physiological pH. Glutamate should be similar but slightly less favorable at lower pH because its side‑chain pKa is higher.

Conclusion:
- Most probable top new binder: L‑Aspartic acid (ligand_5802) ≥ L‑Glutamic acid (ligand_5804).
