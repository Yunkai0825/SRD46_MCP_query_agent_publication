Prediction: thiohistidine (ligand_5860) is the best candidate not yet studied with Zn2+ to form the most stable zinc–amino‑acid complex.

Why this pick
- Strong donor set and chelation: Thiohistidine offers a mixed N,S chelate (imidazole N plus thiolate S) in addition to the backbone donors. Zn2+ (a borderline-soft Lewis acid) binds especially strongly to thiolates and well to imidazole N; combining these in one ligand typically boosts stability over N,N- (His) or O,O- (Asp/Glu) chelation.
- Favorable pKa alignment: SRD‑46 lists the thiohistidine side‑chain thiol pKa ≈ 8.59, making S− accessible near neutral to mildly basic pH; Zn2+ commonly lowers thiol pKa further upon coordination. Imidazole donors are already available around pH 6–8.
- Benchmarks from measured Zn–amino‑acid constants: Thiolate donors (Cys, penicillamine) show much higher Zn(II) log β than neutral/imidazole-only or carboxylate-only donors; thus an N,S ligand like thiohistidine should be at least as strong as these and stronger than phenolate, thioether, or amine-only cases.

Putting this together
- SRD‑46 shows Zn(II) complexes with thiolate‑bearing amino acids (cysteine, penicillamine) have much larger log β than those with only backbone donors (glycine/alanine) or imidazole alone (histidine).
- Thiohistidine combines the two strongest donor types observed for Zn(II) in amino‑acid contexts (thiolate + imidazole), enabling a robust N,S chelate; its thiol pKa (≈8.6) and imidazole availability favor coordination near neutral to mildly basic pH.
- Other unstudied amino acids either require higher pH to access strong donors (tyrosine phenolate, pKa ≈10.1) or present weaker/neutral donors (thioethers, simple amines), so they are unlikely to surpass an N,S chelate.

If restricted to canonical amino acids only: among those without Zn(II) data in this snapshot, tyrosine is the most plausible candidate to give the strongest Zn complex, but primarily at alkaline pH where phenolate is formed; it would still be weaker than the thiolate‑based complexes above.

**Verdict**

**Explanation**
SRD‑46 shows Zn²⁺ stability constants are highest with thiolate donors (cysteine, penicillamine) and strong with imidazole, but weaker with phenolate/carboxylate. Thiohistidine unites thiolate and imidazole donors with pKa values enabling deprotonation near neutral pH, maximizing chelation. Stability is pH‑dependent; phenolate (tyrosine) requires alkaline conditions and generally binds weaker than thiolate.
