### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
Yes. It reports that 85.04% of stability-constant measurements were conducted at 25 °C (operationalized as 24.8–25.2 °C), with all measurements having recorded temperatures.

Explanation
Of 79,063 stability-constant measurements, 67,237 fall within 25.0 ± 0.2 °C, indicating a strong concentration at standard ambient temperature, which facilitates cross-comparison of constants. The remaining ~15% were measured at other temperatures, which may introduce temperature-dependent variation. The ±0.2 °C tolerance reasonably captures entries reported as “25 °C,” but exact-equality counts could be slightly different.
