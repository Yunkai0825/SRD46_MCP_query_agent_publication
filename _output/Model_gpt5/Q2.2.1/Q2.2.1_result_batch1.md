### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
No gaps: the subagent provided a clear percentage with counts and stated the temperature window used.

Explanation
Out of 79,063 stability-constant measurements, 67,237 were reported at 25.0 °C (using a 24.9–25.1 °C window), yielding 85.04%. This reflects the common practice of measuring and reporting formation constants at 25 °C, especially in aqueous systems. A caveat is that the percentage depends on the chosen tolerance; entries slightly off 25.0 °C or recorded differently (e.g., broader “room temperature,” 298 K) would not be included in the numerator.
