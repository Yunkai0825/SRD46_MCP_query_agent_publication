Do they decrease monotonically?
Yes. K1 > K2 > K3 > K4 (logKi: 4.1 > 3.3 > 2.8 > 2.1), i.e., each successive ammonia binds more weakly than the previous one under the same medium and temperature.

What does this imply about coordination geometry?
- The monotonic decrease is the expected “statistical + electronic” trend as binding sites are progressively filled and the d9 Cu(II) center becomes less accommodating.
- Importantly, this network contains strong formation up to Cu(NH3)4^2+ but no ML5/ML6 under these conditions, indicating that four inner‑sphere donor sites are strongly favored. This is consistent with a 4‑coordinate square‑planar core for Cu(II) ammine complexes in aqueous solution, with any additional ligation (if present) likely weak and axial (e.g., solvent) due to Jahn–Teller effects.
