Short answer
- The stepwise formation constants for Cu2+ + NH3 in water follow K1 > K2 > K3 > K4; i.e., they decrease approximately monotonically under consistent conditions.
- Interpretation: Cu(II) (d9) exhibits a strong preference for four equatorial donors due to the Jahn–Teller effect. The first four NH3 ligands stabilize a square‑planar (equatorial) set in an elongated octahedral field and are progressively less favorable as crowding increases; any additional axial ligation is much weaker. The monotonic decline through K4 is consistent with this 4‑coordinate equatorial preference and the growing entropic/steric penalty for successive additions.
