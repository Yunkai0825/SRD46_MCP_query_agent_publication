Answer: Cu2+ (copper(II)).

Based on SRD‑46 stability-constants data, Cu2+ has the largest number of distinct ligands with measured stability constants.

Top metal ions by number of distinct ligands (excluding H+):
| Metal ion | SRD-46 ID | Distinct ligands counted |
|---|---|---|
| Cu2+ | metal_41 | 1951 |
| Ni2+ | metal_112 | 1369 |
| Zn2+ | metal_208 | 1169 |
| Co2+ | metal_33 | 977 |

Note: H+ (metal_68) appears in the database with 4081 ligand entries but is not a metal ion and was excluded from the ranking above.

Chemistry insight: Copper(II)’s exceptionally broad ligand coverage in SRD‑46 reflects its versatile coordination chemistry and widespread study in bioinorganic, environmental, and analytical contexts, which has produced stability measurements with a very large variety of donor types.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD‑46); counts obtained by aggregating distinct ligand_ids per metal_id over measured entries in ligandmetal_card joined to ligandmetal_stability_measured.
