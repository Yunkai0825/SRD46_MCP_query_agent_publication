Short answer: Yes—EDTA is a classic multidentate (hexadentate) ligand that is highly deprotonated near neutral pH, which strongly favors chelation of many metal ions in water.

What SRD‑46 shows about EDTA’s acid–base profile (25 °C, I ≈ 0.1 M, water)
The pKa ladder below indicates that around pH 7 EDTA is mostly 3– to 4– deprotonated (HL3–/L4–), providing multiple anionic donor sites for strong metal binding.

Interpretation
- Structure (from SRD‑46 SMILES for ligand_6277) shows two amines and four carboxylates, consistent with hexadentate coordination.
- The pKa pattern means that at physiological pH EDTA bears multiple negative charges and can wrap around metal ions, which is the hallmark of an effective chelator.
