Here are matched-condition aqueous pKa values for glycine and alanine (25 °C, I = 0.10 M):

| Ligand | pKa1 (COOH deprotonation) | pKa2 (NH3+ deprotonation) | Conditions |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Glycine | 2.33 | 9.57 | Water, 25 °C, I = 0.10 M |
| Alanine (L) | 2.33 | 9.71 | Water, 25 °C, I = 0.10 M |

Answer: The carboxyl pKa1 is identical (2.33) for both. The ammonium pKa2 differs slightly (alanine higher by 0.14), a small shift consistent with the methyl group's weak electron-donating effect. In practice, this difference is minor and generally within typical experimental variability; the two amino acids have very similar acid–base behavior under these conditions.

Source: NIST SRD‑46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes) pKa data for ligand_5760 (glycine) and ligand_5761 (alanine).
