### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
No. The answer suggests plausible candidates but provides no quantitative Ca2+ vs Cu2+ stability comparison and includes ligands (e.g., tartrate, pyrophosphate) that often bind Cu2+ strongly.

**Explanation**
Hard oxygen‑donor anions (carbonate, sulfate, fluoride) generally favor Ca2+ and form weak Cu2+ complexes near neutral–basic pH, making them better Ca2+ masks. Polycarboxylates (oxalate, citrate, tartrate) and polyphosphates frequently chelate Cu2+ strongly or precipitate both metals, reducing selectivity. Speciation is pH‑ and ionic‑strength‑dependent and may involve precipitation (e.g., CaCO3, CaF2, Ca3(PO4)2), so conditional stability constants must be compared under the intended conditions.
