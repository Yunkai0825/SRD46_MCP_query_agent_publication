Interpretation and reasoned estimates (qualitative, aqueous conditions)
- The absence of SRD‑46 stability entries for Fe(II) and Fe(III) with DMF, MeCN, THF, and EG (and close neutral analogs) indicates that, in water near ambient conditions, any inner‑sphere complexation is too weak to have been compiled as measurable formation constants. This contrasts with clear SRD‑46 coverage of Fe(II) complexes with stronger/chelating ligands (e.g., ammonia, ethylenediamine, 2,2′‑bipyridyl, 1,10‑phenanthroline), showing that when complexation is appreciable, constants are indeed reported.
- Qualitative trend for Fe(II) among the requested donors is expected to be EG > DMF ≳ MeCN >> THF in water: EG can chelate as a 1,2‑diol (albeit weakly when neutral), amides and nitriles are modest N/O donors, and THF (an ether) is the weakest. For Fe(III), hydrolysis and preference for anionic hard O‑donors dominate; neutral donors like these are outcompeted by water and OH−, so conditional stability is negligible under typical aqueous pH.
- Important caveat: SRD‑46 primarily curates aqueous formation constants. Solvation/coordination in neat nonaqueous media (e.g., FeXn(MeCN)m in acetonitrile, solvated Fe(ClO4)2 in DMF/THF) are generally outside the aqueous stability‑constant framework and are not represented here. Thus, lack of aqueous β values does not preclude inner‑sphere solvation in those pure solvents; it means SRD‑46 does not tabulate corresponding equilibrium constants for those media.

Bottom line
- SRD‑46 contains no aqueous stability constants for DMF–Fe(II/III), MeCN–Fe(II/III), THF–Fe(II/III), or EG–Fe(II/III). Given the database’s comprehensive coverage of Fe complexes that do form appreciably, the practical inference for water at ~25 °C and μ ≲ 0.1–1 M is that these neutral donors bind Fe(II/III) only very weakly (effectively negligible compared to aquo/hydrolyzed species). EG may chelate slightly more than the others when neutral, but remains minor unless deprotonated to alkoxide at high pH (then it is no longer “ethylene glycol” as a neutral ligand).
