Reasoned estimates for the requested neutral donors (aqueous)

- In water at 25 °C and modest ionic strength, neutral donors such as DMF (amide O), acetonitrile (nitrile N), THF (ether O), and ethylene glycol (neutral diol) bind Fe(II)/Fe(III) much more weakly than deprotonated O‑donors. SRD‑46’s absence of measurable ML constants for these specific neutral ligands, despite extensive Fe coverage, is consistent with very weak complexation that does not compete with hydration and, for Fe(III), hydrolysis.
- Therefore, for aqueous speciation modeling under typical conditions (nitrate/perchlorate media, ~25 °C), it is reasonable to treat complexation of Fe(II)/Fe(III) by DMF, acetonitrile, THF, or neutral ethylene glycol as negligible relative to hydrolysis and to anionic ligand complexation.
- Among the four neutral ligands, ethylene glycol could in principle chelate through two neutral alcohol donors, but in water the chelate advantage is offset by weak alcohol donor strength; it remains far weaker than the anionic glycolate/diglycolate shown above.

Notes and caveats

- SRD‑46 predominantly catalogs aqueous data. Many well‑known Fe complexes with acetonitrile, DMF, or THF occur in those solvents (nonaqueous) and lie outside SRD‑46’s aqueous scope.
- Nitrile/amide/ether name searches in SRD‑46 chiefly return anionic systems (e.g., cyanide, thiocyanate, hydroxamates, salicylamide‑phenolate, carboxylates) that are not appropriate analogs for neutral donors in water.
- For Fe(III), hydrolysis and competing anions (e.g., Cl−) strongly influence solution chemistry; constants for weak neutral donors are often too small to quantify under aqueous conditions.
