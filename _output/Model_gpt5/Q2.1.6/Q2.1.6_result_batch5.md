**Prompt:** Rank Cd²⁺, Pb²⁺, and Hg²⁺ by their log K with thiocyanic acid. Which soft metal has the highest affinity?

Here are stepwise ML formation constants (β1 = K1) for SCN− at 25 °C from NIST SRD‑46 (reaction: M2+ + SCN− ⇌ M(SCN)+; β_def_812). Ionic strength differs slightly by metal, so I is shown for each value.

| Metal | log K1 (25 °C) | Ionic strength (M) | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Hg2+ | 9.64 | 0.0 | Also 9.08 at I = 1.0 |
| Cd2+ | 1.93 | 0.0 | 1.68 at I = 0.1; 1.35 at I = 0.5 |
| Pb2+ | 0.49 | 1.0 | 0.54 at I = 2; 0.78 at I = 3 |

Ranking by affinity (highest to lowest): Hg2+ » Cd2+ > Pb2+.

Answer: Mercury(II) has by far the highest affinity for thiocyanate. The order is robust to ionic strength differences—the Hg(II) K1 is ~8–9 orders of magnitude larger than Cd(II) and ~9–10 larger than Pb(II). This aligns with HSAB: SCN− coordinates through sulfur to soft metals, and Hg(II) is the softest of the three.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD‑46), thiocyanic acid/thiocyanate systems at 25 °C (β_def_812).
