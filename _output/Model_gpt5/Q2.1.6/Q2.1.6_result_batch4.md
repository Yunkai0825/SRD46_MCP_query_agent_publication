### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
There are gaps: only Cd2+ had a numeric log K; Pb2+ and Hg2+ values were missing, so the ranking isn’t quantitatively supported, although Hg2+ as highest is chemically plausible.

**Explanation**
Thiocyanate is a soft S‑donor; by HSAB, Hg2+ (very soft) binds most strongly, followed by Cd2+, then Pb2+. Retrieved data show Cd–SCN log K1 ≈ 1.7 near 25 °C, while Hg2+ forms very strong ML and higher complexes (and even Hg(SCN)2(s)), consistent with Hg2+ >> Cd2+ ≳ Pb2+. Affinities vary with ionic strength and S- vs N‑binding, so matched conditions are essential for precise ranking.
