Here are the SRD‑46 values for oxalic acid (Ethanedioic acid; ligand_8872) in water. Oxalic acid is diprotic (two dissociation steps), with the sequence H2L → HL → L.

| Step | Equilibrium (acid dissociation) | pKa |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | H2L ⇌ HL + H+ | −1.2 |
| 2 | HL ⇌ L + H+ | 3.8 |

Notes
- Dissociation steps: 2 (diprotic).
- These pKa values come from the SRD‑46 ligand card’s species “bracket” transitions (H2L → HL at pH ≈ −1.2; HL → L at pH ≈ 3.8). SRD‑46 contains multiple equilibrium maps for this system (primarily near room temperature and low ionic strength), but the ligand card reports these canonical bracket points.

Source: NIST SRD‑46 (metal_68 = H+, ligand_8872 = oxalic acid).

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
Partial. The agent correctly states oxalic acid is diprotic (two dissociation steps) but gives an implausible first pKa (−1.2); accepted aqueous values near 25 °C are pKa1 ≈ 1.2–1.3 and pKa2 ≈ 4.2–4.3.

**Explanation**
Oxalic acid (H2C2O4) dissociates in two steps: H2A ⇌ HA− (pKa1 ~1.2) and HA− ⇌ A2− (pKa2 ~4.2). These equilibria vary with ionic strength and temperature, but the first dissociation is not strong enough to have a negative pKa in water. Thus, there are two dissociations with a moderately strong first acidity and a weaker second.
