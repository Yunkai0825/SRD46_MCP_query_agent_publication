### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
There is a gap: the subagent correctly states two dissociation steps, but the reported pKa1 = −1.2 is inconsistent with oxalic acid’s known acidity.

Explanation
Oxalic acid is diprotic (H2C2O4 ⇌ HC2O4− ⇌ C2O42−), so it has two dissociation steps. In water near 25 °C, stepwise pKa values are about pKa1 ≈ +1.2 and pKa2 ≈ 4.0–4.3; apparent values shift with ionic strength (e.g., pKa2 ≈ 3.8 at I ≈ 0.1 M). A negative pKa1 would imply a far stronger first acidity than oxalic acid actually exhibits.
