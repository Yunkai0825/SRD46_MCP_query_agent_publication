Oxalic acid (ethanedioic acid; H2L) is diprotic, so it has two dissociation steps:
- Step 1: H2L ⇌ HL + H+
- Step 2: HL ⇌ L + H+

Representative pKa values retrieved for ligand_8872 (oxalic acid) under reported conditions:

|-------------------|------------------------------|------|--------|--------------------|---------------|
| 1 (pKa1)          | H2L ⇌ HL + H+                | -1.2 | 25     | 0.1                | vlm_151532    |
| 2 (pKa2)          | HL ⇌ L + H+                  | 3.8  | 25     | 0.1                | vlm_151494    |

Notes
- The system catalog for oxalic acid (H2L) confirms 2 acidic protons and thus 2 dissociation steps.
- Conditions (temperature and ionic strength) influence reported pKa; these entries are at 25 °C and I = 0.1 M. Solvent was not shown in the returned summary.
- The very low (negative) pKa1 indicates a strong first deprotonation; the second deprotonation is weaker near pH ~3.8 under these conditions.

Source: NIST SRD-46 (Ligand ID: ligand_8872; entries vlm_151532 and vlm_151494).

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
Partial. The agent correctly states oxalic acid is diprotic (two dissociation steps), but the reported pKa1 = −1.2 is inconsistent with well-established aqueous values.

Explanation
Oxalic acid has two acidic protons and dissociates in two steps. In water near 25 °C, widely accepted values are pKa1 ≈ 1.2 and pKa2 ≈ 4.2–4.3; modest ionic strength can shift them slightly lower but not to negative pKa1. A negative first pKa would imply much stronger acidity than observed for oxalic acid in aqueous solution, suggesting a misinterpretation of constants or nonstandard conditions.
