Big‑picture takeaways
- Hg(II) forms exceptionally strong complexes with soft anions: CN− >> I− > Br− > Cl−; thiosulfate and EDTA‑type chelators also bind strongly and can outcompete halides under matched conditions.
- Ionic strength matters: increasing I pushes equilibria toward higher‑halide anions (e.g., HgCl3−, HgCl42−), and modestly lowers chelate logβ1.
- Hg(I) exists predominantly as the dimer Hg2^2+ in water; halide chemistry is dominated by very low solubility of Hg2X2(s), not by well‑defined aqueous coordination series.
