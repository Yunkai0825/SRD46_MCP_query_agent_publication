Chemistry perspective and trends
- Hg(II) shows a clear halide affinity trend: iodide complexes are significantly stronger than chloride at 25 °C (e.g., ML logK 12.87 for I− vs ~6.7–7.3 for Cl−), in line with Hg(II) behaving as a soft Lewis acid. The negative ΔH values and large positive ΔS values for Hg(II)–chloride steps are consistent with exothermic formation and substantial desolvation/ion-pairing contributions.
- Organomercury cations bind halides more weakly than Hg(II); e.g., EtHg+–Cl− ML logK 5.32 is lower than Hg(II)–Cl− ML. MeHg+ and EtHg+ show extensive complexation with thiolate-type ligands under mild conditions, reflecting the strong Hg–S affinity relevant to biochemical binding (cysteine/glutathione).
