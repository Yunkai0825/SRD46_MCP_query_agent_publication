Notes
- Species definitions are explicitly reported as M + L ⇌ ML and M + 2L ⇌ ML2, with L = glycinate; the ligand protonation bracket includes (9.57, L, +∞).
- Constants vary modestly with temperature and ionic strength: at 25 °C, logβ1 ≈ 8.1–8.6 and logβ2 ≈ 15.0–15.7; at 37 °C and I ≈ 0.15 M, they are slightly lower (logβ1 ≈ 7.99, logβ2 ≈ 14.69).

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
Partially complete: it reports β1 and β2 for Cu2+–glycinate at selected temperatures and ionic strengths but omits other reported species and some conditions present in SRD‑46.

Explanation
SRD‑46 indicates Cu(II) forms strong 1:1 and 1:2 glycinate complexes, with logβ1 ≈ 8–9 and logβ2 ≈ 15–16 near 25 °C; stability decreases with increasing temperature and shows modest ionic‑strength dependence. Reported β values are overall formation constants for Cu2+ + L ⇌ CuL and Cu2+ + 2L ⇌ CuL2 with L = glycinate. Speciation depends on pH (ligand protonation) and ionic strength.
