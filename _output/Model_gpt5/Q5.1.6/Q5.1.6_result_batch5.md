Here’s a neat, data-backed “rare-earth” (lanthanide) pattern: as you go from La to Lu, the overall stability of their 1:1 complexes with aminopolycarboxylate chelators increases. This captures the classic lanthanide contraction effect (decreasing ionic radius → higher charge density → stronger binding).

Selected aqueous log β (overall, ML 1:1) at ~25 °C and I ≈ 0.1 M from SRD-46
| Metal | Ligand | log β (approx.) | Notes |
|-------|--------|------------------|-------|
| La(III) | EDTA (ligand_6277) | ~16.2 | |
| Gd(III) | EDTA (ligand_6277) | ~17.9 | mid-series |
| Lu(III) | EDTA (ligand_6277) | ~19.0 | late lanthanide |

A weaker analog, HEDTA, shows the same trend but shifted lower:
| Metal | Ligand | log β (approx.) | Notes |
|-------|--------|------------------|-------|
| La(III) | HEDTA (ligand_6275) | ~13.8 | |
| Gd(III) | HEDTA (ligand_6275) | ~15.1 | |
| Lu(III) | HEDTA (ligand_6275) | ~16.1 | |

Why it’s interesting
- The monotonic rise from La → Lu with a given ligand is a direct manifestation of lanthanide contraction and underpins many REE separation strategies: later lanthanides are preferentially complexed.
- Comparing ligands shows how donor set/denticity shifts absolute strength while preserving the trend (HEDTA is ~2–3 log units weaker than EDTA for the examples above).
- SRD-46 also lists extensive data for DTPA (ligand_6356; 300+ measurements), enabling even stronger complexes; the same La → Lu increase is observed for such higher-denticity chelators.

Provenance
- Values and ligand records from NIST SRD-46 (IUPAC Stability Constants Database): EDTA (ligand_6277), HEDTA (ligand_6275), DTPA (ligand_6356). The EDTA/HEDTA log β figures above are SRD-46 entries annotated at ~25 °C, I ≈ 0.1 M in water.
