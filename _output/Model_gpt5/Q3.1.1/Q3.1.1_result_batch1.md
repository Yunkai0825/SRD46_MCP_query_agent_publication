### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
There are gaps — it doesn’t rank Pb²⁺ affinities; only EDTA is quantified. Clinical use is noted qualitatively.

**Explanation**
SRD‑46 shows Pb–EDTA is very strong (ML logβ ≈ 18 at 25 °C, I ≈ 0.10 M), dominating near neutral pH. Without matched constants for DTPA, EGTA, DOTA, and thiol chelators (DMSA, DMPS, BAL), a highest‑affinity list isn’t possible; thiol complexes (often ML₂/ML₃) are pH/ionic‑strength dependent. Clinically, CaNa₂‑EDTA and DMSA are used; DMPS is regional; BAL is rare.
