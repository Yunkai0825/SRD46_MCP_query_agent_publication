Difference: Δlog K = log β1(en) − log β1(NH3) = 7.32 − 2.72 = 4.60, i.e., ethylenediamine binds ≈ 10^4.6 ≈ 4 × 10^4 times more strongly to Ni2+ than ammonia under comparable aqueous, room‑temperature, low‑ionic‑strength conditions.

Insight: The much larger β1 for en reflects the chelate effect—bidentate en forms a stable five‑membered ring with Ni2+, enhancing entropy and often enthalpy relative to monodentate NH3.

Source: NIST SRD‑46 (IUPAC Stability Constants), reference equilibrium networks ref_eq_net_28239 (Ni2+–NH3) and ref_eq_net_9597 (Ni2+–en).

### Verdict & Explanation

Verdict
No major gaps; the subagent identified ethylenediamine as binding more strongly to Ni2+ and quantified ΔlogK ≈ 4.6.

Explanation
At ~25 °C in water and low ionic strength, log β1 ≈ 2.72 for Ni2+–NH3 and ≈ 7.32 for Ni2+–en, implying ~10^4.6-fold stronger binding for en—consistent with the chelate effect from bidentate en forming a five‑membered ring. Stability constants depend on ionic strength, pH (ligand protonation), and step (βn), but the β1 comparison robustly shows en >> NH3 under comparable conditions.
