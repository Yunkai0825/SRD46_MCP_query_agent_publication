### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict**
No gaps. Ethylenediamine binds more strongly to Ni²⁺; the difference in log K1 is about 4.5–5 units under 25 °C, low-ionic-strength aqueous conditions.

**Explanation**
SRD‑46 reports log β1 ≈ 2.3 for Ni²⁺–NH3 at I ≈ 0.1 M and ≈ 6.9 for Ni²⁺–ethylenediamine at low ionic strength (25 °C), giving Δlog K ≈ 4.6. This reflects the chelate effect: bidentate en forms a more stable five-membered chelate ring, increasing stability by ~10^4.6. Ionic strength shifts absolute values modestly but does not alter the ~4–5 log-unit difference.
