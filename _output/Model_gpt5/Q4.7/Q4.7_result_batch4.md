Interpretation and prediction (grounded in SRD‑46 coverage and crystal‑field theory)
- Qualitative comparison: Where SRD‑46 shows both oxidation states (e.g., TACN, en, dien, TREN, cyclam, EDTA, NTA), Co3+ complexes with amine donors are reported and noted to be more stable than Co2+ analogues (explicitly stated for TACN; consistent with broader coordination chemistry). Co3+ (d6) gains strong LFSE in low‑spin octahedral fields with hard N donors; Co2+ (d7) is commonly high‑spin and more labile.
- Predicted behavior for a new polydentate amine ligand:
  • With Co3+ (metal_34): Expect substantially stronger binding and high cumulative stability (large logβ values), favoring low‑spin octahedral coordination and kinetic inertness. The Co3+–amine advantage should grow with denticity, preorganization (macrocycles), and rigid chelate frameworks.  
  • With Co2+ (metal_33): Expect weaker binding relative to Co3+ under comparable conditions, typically high‑spin and more labile complexes; increasing denticity/macrocyclic preorganization will help but will still trail Co3+ stability.
- Practical factors that modulate the Co3+–Co2+ gap: higher ligand denticity and macrocyclic rigidity, tertiary vs secondary amines, and minimized protonation (use the fully deprotonated amine donor set when comparing).
