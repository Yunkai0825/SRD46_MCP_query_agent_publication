Qualitative comparison and prediction (crystal‑field theory; no numeric values claimed)
- Co3+ (d6) in octahedral amine fields is typically low‑spin with larger ligand‑field stabilization and stronger electrostatic attraction than Co2+ (d7, often high‑spin). Together with the chelate effect, this leads to much stronger and more kinetically inert amine chelates for Co3+ than for Co2+ under comparable aqueous conditions.
- Therefore, for ligands that exist with both oxidation states, the stability constants for Co3+ complexes are expected to be substantially higher than those for Co2+ when stoichiometry, temperature, solvent, and ionic strength are matched.
- Prediction for a new polydentate amine ligand:
  - With Co3+: very strong binding and pronounced kinetic inertness (octahedral low‑spin amine complexes), likely forming well‑defined six‑coordinate chelates. In practice, such ligands can even promote air oxidation of Co2+ to Co3+ during complexation.
  - With Co2+: moderate binding strength and much greater lability; complexes form readily but are less inert and more sensitive to competing ligands and ionic strength.
