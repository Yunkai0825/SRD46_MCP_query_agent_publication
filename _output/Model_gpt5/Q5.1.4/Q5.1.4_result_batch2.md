Notes and context
- Media/conditions: Nearly all Zn(II) stability data here are in water and cluster at 25 °C and I ≈ 0.1 M, with substantial coverage up to I = 1–3 M for anion complexes.
- Ligand families: Alongside simple inorganic anions, SRD‑46 contains many amino acids (glycine, histidine, cysteine), N‑donors (ethylenediamine, imidazole, ammonia), aromatic chelators (1,10‑phenanthroline), and phosphonates (e.g., NTP). EDTA and related aminopolycarboxylates are present; representative entries include hydrolysis‑coupled reactions, and protonation ladders for EDTA derivatives are available at I = 0.1 M.
- Trends: Chloride complexation is weak and increases with ionic strength, while bisulfate is substantially stronger at low I and weakens with I. Bicarbonate shows appreciable cumulative stability, especially for ML2 at higher ionic strength. Among bioligands, thio‑donor substitution (thiohistidine) enhances Zn(II) binding relative to histidine.

Explanation
SRD‑46 holds ~4,100 aqueous Zn2+ stability measurements, mostly at 25 °C and I≈0.1 M, dominated by equilibrium constants with some enthalpy/entropy data. Chloride binding is weak and increases with ionic strength; bisulfate is stronger at low I but weakens with I; bicarbonate shows moderate stability with ML2 strengthened at higher I. Bioligand binding varies: histidine logK≈6.5, thiohistidine ≈9.2; glycine forms ML (≈4.9) and higher (ML2/ML3). Coverage spans amino acids, N-donors, phosphonates, and aminopolycarboxylates (EDTA).
