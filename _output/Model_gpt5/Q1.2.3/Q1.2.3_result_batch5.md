Chemistry notes
- Histidine binds Zn(II) primarily through the imidazole nitrogen; as the ligand deprotonates (L−), anionic histidinate favors [ML] and [ML2]. Neutral HL can form protonated adducts ([MHL], [MHL2]).  
- Under more basic conditions, hydroxo substitution appears, giving [M(OH)L2]; protonation reversibly converts it back to [ML2], linking metal–hydroxo chemistry with ligand binding.  
- This set of nodes captures stepwise complexation, proton-linked speciation, and hydrolysis within a single Zn–histidine network.
