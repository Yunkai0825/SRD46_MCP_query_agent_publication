## Chemistry Insights

1. **Chelate effect dominates:** EDTA's log K₁ ≈ 15.5 dwarfs simple carboxylates because it wraps six donor atoms (2N + 4O) around the large La³⁺ ion, which favors coordination numbers of 8–9. Each additional chelate ring adds roughly 2–3 log units of stability.

2. **Dicarboxylates > monocarboxylates:** Among simple acids, malonic acid (log K₁ ≈ 3.2) significantly outperforms acetic acid (log K₁ ≈ 1.8) because it forms a stable five- or six-membered chelate ring, whereas acetate can only bind in a monodentate or bidentate-bridging fashion.

3. **Hydroxy groups help modestly:** Glycolic and lactic acids (α-hydroxycarboxylates) show slightly higher log K₁ than acetic acid due to the additional hydroxyl donor, but the gain is small because La³⁺–OH bonding is relatively weak compared to La³⁺–COO⁻ interactions.

> **Source:** NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46) — Critical Stability Constants of Metal Complexes. Exact values vary with ionic strength and temperature; the database should be consulted for precise conditions of individual measurements.
