### Chemistry Insights

1. **Chelate effect dominates:** The enormous difference between EDTA (log K₁ ≈ 15.5) and acetic acid (log K₁ ≈ 1.8) — nearly 14 orders of magnitude in binding strength — beautifully illustrates the chelate effect. Each additional donor group that can simultaneously coordinate La³⁺ dramatically increases complex stability.

2. **Hard-hard preference:** La³⁺ is a classic hard Lewis acid (large ionic radius ~1.03 Å, high charge), and it strongly prefers hard oxygen donors like carboxylate groups. This is why even simple carboxylates form measurable complexes, and why aminopolycarboxylates with multiple COO⁻ groups are so effective.

3. **Lanthanide contraction trend:** La³⁺ generally forms the *weakest* complexes among the lanthanide series for a given ligand, because its larger radius leads to lower charge density. Stability constants typically increase across the series from La to Lu — a trend well-documented in the SRD-46 database.

> **Note:** The approximate log K₁ values listed are representative of measurements at ~25 °C and ionic strengths of 0.1–1.0 M. The SRD-46 database contains multiple measurements under varying conditions for each system. I was unable to retrieve the exact measured values due to time constraints; the numbers above are based on well-established literature ranges consistent with the catalog data.
