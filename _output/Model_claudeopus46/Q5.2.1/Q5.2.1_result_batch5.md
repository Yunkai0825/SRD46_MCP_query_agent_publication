### Why the gap?

This absence is not surprising from a chemistry perspective:

1. **Solvent scope:** SRD-46 focuses primarily on **aqueous-phase** equilibrium constants. Most uranyl–crown ether interaction studies have been conducted in **nonaqueous or mixed solvents** (e.g., methanol, acetonitrile, or liquid–liquid extraction systems), which fall outside the database's primary scope.

2. **Weak aqueous binding:** The uranyl ion (UO₂²⁺) is a hard, linear dioxo cation that preferentially coordinates equatorial oxygen donors in its coordination plane. Crown ethers, being cyclic polyethers with relatively rigid cavities, are not well-suited to the equatorial coordination geometry of uranyl in aqueous solution, leading to weak or negligible complex formation in water.

3. **Extraction chemistry focus:** Much of the published uranyl–crown ether work relates to **synergistic solvent extraction** rather than homogeneous aqueous equilibria, so those conditional constants are typically not included in SRD-46.

**Source:** NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).
