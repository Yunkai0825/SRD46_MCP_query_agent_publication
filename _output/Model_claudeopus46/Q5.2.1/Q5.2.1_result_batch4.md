### Chemistry Insight

While crown ethers have extensive stability data with alkali and alkaline earth metals (which is where most of their ~200+ VLM entries come from), their interaction with the uranyl ion UO₂²⁺ is not well-characterized in terms of classical aqueous stability constants. This is likely because:

1. **Geometric mismatch**: The linear O=U=O²⁺ ion coordinates ligands in the equatorial plane, making it a poor fit for the circular cavity of crown ethers designed for spherical cations.
2. **Solvent extraction context**: Most uranyl–crown ether studies in the literature focus on liquid–liquid extraction or non-aqueous systems, which typically report distribution coefficients rather than thermodynamic formation constants suitable for SRD-46.
3. **Weak binding in water**: Crown ethers generally bind poorly to highly charged, hard metal ions like UO₂²⁺ in aqueous solution, so measurable stability constants may simply be too small to determine reliably.

If you're interested in uranyl complexation, SRD-46 has extensive data for UO₂²⁺ with ligands such as fluoride, carbonate, oxalate, EDTA, sulfate, and other oxygen/nitrogen donors. For crown ether interactions with uranyl, the primary literature on solvent extraction or computational studies may be more informative.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critical Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
