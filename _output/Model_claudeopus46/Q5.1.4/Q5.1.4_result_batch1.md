## Equilibrium Species Types

The database covers a wide variety of Zn²⁺ species, including:

- **Simple stepwise complexes:** ML, ML₂, ML₃, ML₄
- **Protonated complexes:** MHL, MH₂L, MH₃L, MH₄L, MHL₂, M(HL)₂
- **Hydroxo complexes:** M(OH)L, M(OH)L₂, M(OH)₂L
- **Polynuclear species:** M₂L, M₂(H₋₁L)₂, M₃HL₄, M₃L₄, M₃(OH)L₄, M₄L₄
- **Solid-phase solubility products:** ZnL₂(s) in multiple polymorphs (α, β₁, β₂, γ, δ, ε, amorphous) and ZnO(s)

Experimental conditions span **5–45 °C** and ionic strengths from **~0 to 5 M**, with a variety of supporting electrolytes.

## Chemistry Insights

The dominance of **amino acids** (16% of all Zn²⁺ entries) reflects zinc's critical role in metalloenzymes and its strong affinity for nitrogen and sulfur donors — consistent with its borderline hard/soft character. The extensive data on **cysteine, histidine, and penicillamine** directly supports modeling of zinc-finger proteins and metallothioneins. The rich coverage of **macrocyclic ligands** (aza- and aza-oxa-macrocycles together ~7%) highlights interest in Zn²⁺ sensor design and selective chelation therapy.
