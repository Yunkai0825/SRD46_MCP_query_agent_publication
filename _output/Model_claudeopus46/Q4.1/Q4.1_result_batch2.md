### Key Considerations

1. **Solvent competition:** In aqueous solution, water itself is a strong competitor for these weak neutral donors. The effective formation constants are therefore very small (log K₁ typically < 2–3), and many are too small to measure reliably — which explains their absence from SRD-46.

2. **Fe(III) vs Fe(II) trends:** Fe³⁺ is a hard Lewis acid (HSAB theory) and preferentially binds hard O-donors (DMF, ethylene glycol) more strongly than Fe²⁺. For the softer nitrile donor of acetonitrile, Fe²⁺ (borderline) may actually bind slightly better.

3. **Non-aqueous relevance:** These ligands are far more important as coordinating solvents in non-aqueous electrochemistry and catalysis. In pure DMF or MeCN as solvent, the "solvation number" (typically 6 for octahedral Fe) replaces the concept of a discrete formation constant — the metal is fully solvated by the ligand/solvent.

4. **Ethylene glycol** is the most promising candidate for measurable aqueous complexation due to its chelating ability, but even here, the neutral diol is a weak donor compared to its deprotonated glycolate form.
