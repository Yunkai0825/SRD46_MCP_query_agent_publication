### Chemistry Insight

The Pb²⁺–Cl⁻ system is one of the most extensively studied metal–halide equilibria, which explains the extraordinary number of linked references. The modest log β₁ ≈ 1.07 (at *I* = 0.1 M, 25 °C) reflects the relatively weak, predominantly electrostatic interaction between the large, soft Pb²⁺ cation and the hard Cl⁻ anion. The value increases with ionic strength (reaching ~1.55 at *I* = 0), consistent with Debye–Hückel activity-coefficient corrections. This system is of major importance in environmental geochemistry and lead speciation in natural waters.
