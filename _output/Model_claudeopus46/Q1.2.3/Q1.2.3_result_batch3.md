### Chemistry Insight

This network beautifully illustrates the **amphoteric** nature of zinc. At low pH, free Zn²⁺ dominates; at moderate pH, sparingly soluble Zn(OH)₂ precipitates in multiple polymorphic forms; and at high pH, the soluble **zincate ion** Zn(OH)₄²⁻ re-dissolves the solid. The polynuclear species — the dinuclear Zn₂OH³⁺ and the tetranuclear Zn₄(OH)₄⁴⁺ (a cubane-like cluster) — are fascinating intermediates that form at elevated zinc concentrations during hydrolysis. The existence of at least five distinct Zn(OH)₂ solid polymorphs (amorphous, β₁, β₂, δ, ε, γ) plus ZnO reflects the remarkably rich solid-state chemistry of this system, which is critically important in corrosion science, galvanizing technology, and environmental zinc speciation modeling.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 — Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes*
