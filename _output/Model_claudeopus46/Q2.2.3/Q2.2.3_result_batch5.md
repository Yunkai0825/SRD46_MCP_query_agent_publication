### Chemistry Insights

The leaders in this list make perfect chemical sense. **Nitrilotris(methylenephosphonic acid) (NTP)** tops the list with 30 entries — it has 6 dissociable protons (three phosphonate groups plus a tertiary amine), and its industrial importance in water treatment drives extensive study across varied conditions. **DTPA** (26 entries) has 5 carboxylic acid protons plus 3 amine nitrogens (up to 8 protonation steps), making it one of the most proton-rich chelators in the database. The polyamine ligands like **pentaethylenehexamine** (6 amine groups = 6 distinct pKa steps) also appear prominently, as each amine represents a separate protonation equilibrium. Note that some ligands with fewer dissociable protons (e.g., succinic acid with only 2 pKa steps) still have high entry counts because they have been extensively studied under many different experimental conditions.
