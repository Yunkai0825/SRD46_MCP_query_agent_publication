### Chemistry Insight

DTPA's exceptionally large number of pKa values reflects its molecular structure: it contains **three nitrogen atoms** and **five carboxylate groups**, giving it eight protonatable sites. This makes DTPA one of the most versatile chelating agents in coordination chemistry — it is widely used in MRI contrast agents (e.g., Gd-DTPA/Magnevist) and in nuclear medicine for actinide decorporation, precisely because its many donor groups allow it to wrap tightly around metal ions. The spread of pKa values across a wide pH range (from very acidic carboxylate protons to moderately basic amine protons) is characteristic of aminopolycarboxylate ligands and is essential for understanding their metal-binding behavior at physiological pH.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
