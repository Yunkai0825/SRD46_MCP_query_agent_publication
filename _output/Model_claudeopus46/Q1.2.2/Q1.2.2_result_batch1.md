# Cd²⁺–Chloride Stability Constants in NIST SRD-46

## Independent Literature Sources

**68 independent literature references** have reported stability constants (or related thermodynamic quantities) for the Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system, spanning publications from **1930 to 1993**. This makes it one of the most extensively studied metal–halide systems in aqueous solution chemistry.

## Conditions Summary

- **Temperature:** All measurements at **25 °C**
- **Ionic strength:** 0 (extrapolated to infinite dilution) up to **4 mol/L**
- **Medium:** Aqueous solution; the I = 0 values represent thermodynamic constants extrapolated to zero ionic strength

## Chemistry Insights

The systematic decrease in log K₁ from 1.98 (I = 0) to 1.35 (I = 0.5–1 M) illustrates the strong ionic-strength dependence typical of ion-association equilibria — higher background electrolyte concentrations compress the activity coefficients and reduce the apparent formation constant. The positive ΔS values that increase from CdCl⁺ → CdCl₃⁻ (30 → 70 J/mol·K) are consistent with progressive release of coordinated water molecules as chloride replaces aqua ligands, a hallmark of inner-sphere complexation. The near-zero ΔH values for the first two complexes confirm that Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ binding is **entropy-driven** — a classic signature of desolvation-dominated complex formation.
