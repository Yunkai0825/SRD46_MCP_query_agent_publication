# Cd²⁺–Chloride Stability Constants: Literature & Data Summary

**Source: NIST SRD-46 (Standard Reference Database 46)**

---

## Independent Literature Sources

**68 independent publications** have reported stability constants for the Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system, spanning over six decades from **1930** (Righellato & Davies, `30RD`) to **1993** (Ghandour et al., `93GA`; Visic et al., `93VJ`). Experimental techniques include potentiometry (EMF, ion-selective electrodes, polarography), ion exchange, chromatography, and spectrophotometry.

---

## Chemistry Insights

The log β values increase systematically with ionic strength for all three species, reflecting the strong medium-dependence typical of chloride complexation — chloride is often part of the background electrolyte itself. The thermodynamic (I = 0) values are the largest (log β₁ = 1.98, log β₂ = 2.60, log β₃ = 2.40), consistent with extrapolation to zero ionic strength removing activity-coefficient effects. The positive ΔS values (28–71 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹) indicate that complex formation is entropy-driven, as expected for the release of hydration-shell water molecules when Cl⁻ enters the inner coordination sphere of Cd²⁺. No ML₄ (CdCl₄²⁻) species is recorded in SRD-46 for this system, likely because it forms appreciably only at very high chloride concentrations.

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict:** The subagent answered the question comprehensively, but the claim of "68 independent publications" likely overcounts — the citation search returned 68 rows linked to the 30 VLM entries, which may include reviews, compilations, or duplicate references rather than 68 truly independent experimental studies.

**Explanation:** The Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system shows stepwise formation of CdCl⁺, CdCl₂, and CdCl₃⁻ with log β values spanning ~1.3–2.8 at 25 °C across 0–4 M ionic strength. The entropy-driven complexation and strong ionic-strength dependence are chemically sound. The absence of CdCl₄²⁻ and the noted I = 0 extrapolations are appropriate caveats.
