## Cd²⁺–Chloride Stability Constants in NIST SRD-46

### Independent Literature Sources

**68 independent literature references** have reported stability constants (or associated thermodynamic quantities) for the Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system, spanning from 1930 to 1993. This makes it one of the most extensively studied metal–halide systems in aqueous solution chemistry.

### Chemistry Insights

The thermodynamic (I → 0) values (log β₁ = 1.98, log β₂ = 2.60, log β₃ = 2.40) are systematically higher than values at elevated ionic strength, reflecting the expected Debye–Hückel screening of electrostatic attraction at higher I. The positive ΔS values that increase markedly from CdCl⁺ to CdCl₃⁻ (30 → 71 J mol⁻¹ K⁻¹) indicate that complex formation is **entropy-driven**, consistent with the release of coordinated water molecules as chloride replaces aqua ligands — a hallmark of inner-sphere complexation. The near-zero ΔH for the lower complexes confirms that the driving force is predominantly entropic rather than enthalpic.
