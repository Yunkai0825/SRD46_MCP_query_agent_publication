# Cd²⁺–Chloride Stability Constants: Literature & Data Summary

**Source: NIST SRD-46**

## Independent Literature Sources

**68 independent literature citations** have reported stability constants (log β), thermodynamic quantities (ΔH, ΔS), or both for the Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system. These span publications from **1930 to 1993**, covering a wide range of experimental methods (potentiometry, polarography, ion exchange, spectroscopy, calorimetry).

## Chemistry Insight

The log β values increase systematically with ionic strength for all three species — a well-known medium effect for chloro-cadmium complexes. The thermodynamic data reveal that complexation is **entropy-driven** (large positive ΔS values increasing from ML to ML₃), consistent with the release of hydration-shell water molecules as chloride replaces coordinated water around Cd²⁺. The near-zero or small ΔH values confirm that the Cd–Cl bond enthalpies roughly compensate for dehydration costs, making the free energy gain almost entirely entropic — a hallmark of inner-sphere substitution in labile d¹⁰ metal ions.
