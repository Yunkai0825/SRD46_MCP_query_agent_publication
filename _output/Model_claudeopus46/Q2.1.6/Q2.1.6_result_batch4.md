## Ranking Cd²⁺, Pb²⁺, and Hg²⁺ by log K₁ with Thiocyanic Acid (SCN⁻)

Using the 1:1 complex formation equilibrium **[M] + [L] ⇌ [ML]** (beta_def_812) at **25 °C** in aqueous solution, the NIST SRD-46 database gives:

| Metal | log K₁ | T (°C) | I (M) | VLM ID |
|-------|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| **Hg²⁺** | **9.64** | 25 | 0 | vlm_172457 |
| **Cd²⁺** | **1.93** | 25 | 0 | vlm_172411 |
| **Pb²⁺** | **0.49 – 1.08** | 25 | 1–4 | vlm_172483–172486 |

> Note: No log K value at I = 0 was available for Pb²⁺ in SRD-46; the closest entries range from 0.49 (I = 1 M) to 1.08 (I = 4 M). At comparable ionic strength (I = 1 M), Cd²⁺ gives log K₁ = 1.31 vs. Pb²⁺ = 0.49, confirming the ranking.

### Ranking (highest → lowest affinity):

$$\text{Hg}^{2+} \gg \text{Cd}^{2+} > \text{Pb}^{2+}$$

### **Hg²⁺ has by far the highest affinity**, with log K₁ = 9.64 — nearly **8 orders of magnitude** greater than Cd²⁺.

### Chemistry Insight

This result is a textbook illustration of **HSAB (Hard-Soft Acid-Base) theory**. Thiocyanate (SCN⁻) is a soft/borderline base that coordinates through its sulfur atom to soft metals. Among these three d-block metals, Hg²⁺ is the *softest* acid (large, polarizable, d¹⁰ configuration), which explains its extraordinarily strong affinity for the soft sulfur donor. Cd²⁺ is borderline-soft and Pb²⁺ is borderline, consistent with their progressively weaker binding. The large exothermic ΔH for Hg²⁺–SCN⁻ (−49 kJ/mol) compared to Cd²⁺ (−13 kJ/mol) and Pb²⁺ (−1.3 kJ/mol) further underscores the dominant covalent character of the Hg–S bond.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
