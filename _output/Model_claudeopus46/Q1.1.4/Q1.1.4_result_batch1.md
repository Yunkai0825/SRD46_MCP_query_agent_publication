### Chemistry Insights

The Ag⁺–NH₃ system is a textbook example of linear diamine coordination. The cumulative log β₂ ≈ 7.2–8.2 (depending on ionic medium) reflects the well-known stability of the **[Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺** complex, which is the basis for **Tollens' reagent** used in aldehyde detection and silver-mirror reactions. The spread in reported values across the ionic strength range 0–5 M highlights the significant effect of the supporting electrolyte (e.g., NH₄NO₃, NaClO₄, KNO₃) on apparent stability — a reminder that "the" stability constant is always medium-dependent. The negative ΔH and ΔS values confirm the reaction is **enthalpically driven** with an entropic penalty from ordering solvent and ligand molecules around the metal centre.
