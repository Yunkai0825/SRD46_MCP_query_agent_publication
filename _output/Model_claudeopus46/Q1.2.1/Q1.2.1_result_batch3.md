### Chemistry Insight

A log K of 18.78 makes Cu²⁺–EDTA one of the most stable divalent-metal EDTA complexes, consistent with Cu²⁺ sitting at the peak of the Irving–Williams series. The hexadentate chelation by EDTA — coordinating through two tertiary amine nitrogens and four carboxylate oxygens — creates an exceptionally stable octahedral complex. This remarkable stability underpins the widespread use of EDTA as a titrant for Cu²⁺ in analytical chemistry and as a chelation therapy agent.
