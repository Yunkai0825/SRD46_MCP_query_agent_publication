### Chemistry context

The absence of data is not surprising for several reasons:

1. **Aspirin hydrolyzes readily** in aqueous solution to salicylate and acetate, making it difficult to measure true metal–acetylsalicylate equilibria under the conditions typically used for stability constant determination.
2. **Pu(IV) is notoriously difficult** to study in solution — it undergoes strong hydrolysis at even mildly acidic pH and tends to form colloidal polymers, severely limiting the pH/concentration window for reliable measurements.
3. The combination of these two challenges likely explains why no critically evaluated constants have been compiled for this system.
