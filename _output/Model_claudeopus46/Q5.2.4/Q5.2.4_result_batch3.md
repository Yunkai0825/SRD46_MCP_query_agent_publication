### Why is this absent?

Aspirin (2-acetoxybenzoic acid) is hydrolytically labile — its acetyl ester group readily cleaves in aqueous solution, especially under the acidic conditions required to keep Pu(IV) in solution. This makes it a poor candidate for equilibrium studies with highly charged, strongly hydrolysing cations like Pu⁴⁺. Most Pu(IV) coordination chemistry in SRD-46 focuses on stronger chelators (e.g., EDTA, hydroxamates, catecholates) that can effectively compete with Pu⁴⁺ hydrolysis.
