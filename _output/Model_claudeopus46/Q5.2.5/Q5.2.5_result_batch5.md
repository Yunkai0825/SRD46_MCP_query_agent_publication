**The standard electrode potential of Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ is not available in the NIST SRD-46 database.**

SRD-46 is dedicated to **stability constants** (log β, log K) and **pKₐ values** for metal–ligand equilibria in solution. It does not store standard electrode (reduction) potentials. A search of the database's citation records, stability measurements, and table schemas confirmed that no E° data are present.

### For reference (from standard electrochemistry sources):

| Half-reaction | E° (V vs. SHE) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Cu²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻ → Cu(s) | **+0.342** | CRC Handbook / NIST SRD-69 |

For authoritative electrode potential data, consult:
- **NIST SRD-69** (Standard Reference Data for electrode potentials)
- **CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics**
- **Bard, Parsons & Jordan** — *Standard Potentials in Aqueous Solution* (IUPAC, 1985)

### Chemistry Insight
The positive E° of +0.34 V places copper below hydrogen in the activity series, meaning Cu metal does **not** dissolve in non-oxidizing acids (e.g., dilute HCl). This property, combined with copper's excellent electrical conductivity, underpins its widespread use in wiring and electronics — it remains stable under most ambient aqueous conditions.
