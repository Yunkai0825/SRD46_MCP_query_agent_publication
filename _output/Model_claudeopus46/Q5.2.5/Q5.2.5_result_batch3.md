## Standard Electrode Potential of Cu²⁺/Cu⁰

The standard reduction potential for the half-reaction:

> **Cu²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻ → Cu(s)**

is:

| Half-Reaction | E° (V vs. SHE) | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu⁰ | **+0.34 V** (commonly cited as +0.337 to +0.340 V) | 25 °C, unit activity (a(Cu²⁺) = 1), 1 bar |

> ⚠️ **Note:** This value is **not from NIST SRD-46**. SRD-46 is a stability constants database — it stores equilibrium constants (log β, log K) for metal–ligand complexation reactions in solution, not electrode potentials. For authoritative electrode potential data, consult sources such as the **IUPAC Tables of Standard Electrode Potentials** or the **CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics**.

### Chemistry insight
The positive E° means Cu²⁺ is thermodynamically favorable to reduce to metallic copper relative to H₂/H⁺. This is why copper metal does **not** dissolve in non-oxidizing acids (e.g., dilute HCl). For non-standard conditions, the **Nernst equation** applies:

$$E = E° + \frac{RT}{nF}\ln a(\text{Cu}^{2+})$$
