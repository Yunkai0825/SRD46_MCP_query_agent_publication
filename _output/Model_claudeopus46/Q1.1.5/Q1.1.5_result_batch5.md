## pKa Values for Oxalic Acid (Ethanedioic Acid)

Oxalic acid (H₂C₂O₄) is a **diprotic acid** with **two dissociation steps**:

1. **H₂L ⇌ HL⁻ + H⁺** (first dissociation)
2. **HL⁻ ⇌ L²⁻ + H⁺** (second dissociation)

The SRD-46 database reports the following pKa values at **25 °C** and **I = 0.1 M**:

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | VLM ID |
|------|-------------|-----|--------|
| 1 | H₂L → HL⁻ + H⁺ | **−1.2** | vlm_151532 |
| 2 | HL⁻ → L²⁻ + H⁺ | **3.8** | vlm_151494 |

### Chemistry Insight

The very low (negative) pKa₁ indicates that the first proton of oxalic acid dissociates essentially completely in aqueous solution — oxalic acid is a moderately strong acid in its first step. The second dissociation (pKa₂ = 3.8) is typical of a weak acid. The large gap of ~5 units between pKa₁ and pKa₂ arises because removing the second proton from the already negatively charged hydrogen oxalate ion (HC₂O₄⁻) is electrostatically much more difficult. This two-step dissociation makes oxalate an excellent chelating ligand for metal ions, as confirmed by the 20 different metal–oxalate systems catalogued in SRD-46.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
