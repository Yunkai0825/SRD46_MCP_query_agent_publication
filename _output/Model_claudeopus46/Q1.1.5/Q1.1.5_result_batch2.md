## pKa Values for Oxalic Acid (Ethanedioic acid)

Oxalic acid is a **diprotic acid** with **2 dissociation steps**:

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | T (°C) | I (mol/L) |
|------|-------------|-----|--------|-----------|
| 1st (Ka₁) | H₂L → HL⁻ + H⁺ | **−1.2** | 25 | 0.1 |
| 2nd (Ka₂) | HL⁻ → L²⁻ + H⁺ | **3.8** | 25 | 0.1 |

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 (ligand_8872, vlm_151532 & vlm_151494)

### Chemistry Insight

The very low (negative) pKa₁ means the first proton of oxalic acid is almost completely dissociated in aqueous solution — it behaves as a strong acid for its first dissociation. The second dissociation (pKa₂ ≈ 3.8) is typical of a moderately weak carboxylic acid. The large gap of ~5 pKa units between the two steps reflects the electrostatic difficulty of removing a second proton from an already negatively charged species, compounded by the close proximity of the two carboxylate groups on this small dicarboxylic acid.
