## Co(II) + 2,2'-Bipyridyl — SRD-46 Data Summary

**Yes, data exists.** The NIST SRD-46 database contains **18 independent measurements** for the Co²⁺ / 2,2'-bipyridyl (bipy) system, covering three successive complex species:

### Log K (log β) Values

| Species | Equilibrium | log β | T (°C) | I (mol/L) |
|---------|-------------|-------|--------|-----------|
| [CoL]²⁺ | Co²⁺ + L ⇌ CoL²⁺ | 5.80 | 25 | 0.1 |
| [CoL]²⁺ | Co²⁺ + L ⇌ CoL²⁺ | 5.81 | 25 | 1.0 |
| [CoL₂]²⁺ | Co²⁺ + 2L ⇌ CoL₂²⁺ | 11.3 | 25 | 0.1 |
| [CoL₂]²⁺ | Co²⁺ + 2L ⇌ CoL₂²⁺ | 11.3 | 25 | 1.0 |
| [CoL₃]²⁺ | Co²⁺ + 3L ⇌ CoL₃²⁺ | 16.0 | 25 | 0.1 |
| [CoL₃]²⁺ | Co²⁺ + 3L ⇌ CoL₃²⁺ | 16.2 | 25 | 1.0 |

### Measurement Breakdown

| Constant Type | Count |

### Log K Range

The **overall log β values span from 5.80 to 16.2**, corresponding to stepwise constants of approximately:
- **K₁ ≈ 5.8** (Co²⁺ + bipy)
- **K₂ ≈ 5.5** (CoL²⁺ + bipy; from log β₂ − log β₁ ≈ 11.3 − 5.8)
- **K₃ ≈ 4.8** (CoL₂²⁺ + bipy; from log β₃ − log β₂ ≈ 16.1 − 11.3)

### Chemistry Insight

The steady decrease in stepwise log K values (5.8 → 5.5 → 4.8) is typical for successive coordination of bidentate N,N-donor ligands to a d⁷ Co²⁺ center — each additional bipy faces increasing steric crowding around the octahedral metal. The enthalpy values (ΔH increasingly negative with more ligands) confirm that complexation is enthalpically driven, while the positive ΔS contributions reflect displacement of coordinated water molecules. The near-identical log β values at I = 0.1 M and I = 1.0 M indicate that ionic strength has only a modest effect on these uncharged-ligand equilibria.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*

### Verdict & Explanation

**Verdict:** The subagent answered the question thoroughly, but the claim of 18 "independent measurements" is slightly misleading — 6 are log K values, 6 are ΔH, and 6 are ΔS, so there are really 6 independent equilibrium constant determinations across 3 species.

**Explanation:** Co²⁺ forms the expected tris-bipy octahedral series with log β₁–β₃ ranging 5.8–16.2. The decreasing stepwise log K (≈5.8, 5.5, 4.8) reflects typical statistical and steric effects for successive bidentate ligand binding. Minimal ionic-strength dependence is consistent with a neutral ligand reacting with a divalent cation.
