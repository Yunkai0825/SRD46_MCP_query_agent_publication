## pKa Values of Citric Acid (NIST SRD-46)

Citric acid (2-hydroxypropane-1,2,3-tricarboxylic acid, **ligand_9058**) is a triprotic acid (H₃L). The database reports three measured pKa values at **25 °C** and **I = 0.1 M**:

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | T (°C) | I (mol/L) | VLM ID |
|------|-------------|-----|--------|-----------|--------|
| pKa₁ | H₃L ⇌ H₂L⁻ + H⁺ | **2.90** | 25 | 0.1 | vlm_157473 |
| pKa₂ | H₂L⁻ ⇌ HL²⁻ + H⁺ | **4.35** | 25 | 0.1 | vlm_157459 |
| pKa₃ | HL²⁻ ⇌ L³⁻ + H⁺ | **5.65** | 25 | 0.1 | vlm_157439 |

### Chemistry Insight

The three pKa values correspond to the successive deprotonation of citric acid's three carboxyl groups. The stepwise increase (pKa₁ < pKa₂ < pKa₃) reflects the growing electrostatic difficulty of removing a proton from an increasingly negatively charged species. These values make citric acid an effective buffer in the pH 2–7 range and explain its widespread use in biological buffers, food chemistry, and as a chelating agent for metal ions in analytical and industrial applications.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
