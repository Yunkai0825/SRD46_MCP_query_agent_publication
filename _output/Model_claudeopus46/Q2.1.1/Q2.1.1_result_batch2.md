## Fe³⁺ Stability: EDTA vs NTA — Side-by-Side Comparison

The overall formation constant **log K₁** for the reaction **M + L ⇌ ML** (beta_def_812) is compared below at matched conditions:

| Condition | EDTA (log K₁) | NTA (log K₁) | Δ log K |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|---------|

| 25 °C, I = 1.0 M | **23.8** | **14.78** | **+9.0** |

*Source: NIST SRD-46*

### Verdict

**EDTA forms a dramatically more stable complex with Fe³⁺ than NTA**, with log K₁ values roughly **9 orders of magnitude** larger under identical conditions.

### Chemical Insight

This large difference is expected from the denticity of the two ligands. **EDTA is hexadentate** (4 carboxylate + 2 amine donors), while **NTA is tetradentate** (3 carboxylate + 1 amine donor). The two additional donor atoms in EDTA provide extra chelate rings around Fe³⁺, massively boosting thermodynamic stability via the **chelate effect**. This is why EDTA is the preferred chelator in analytical chemistry (e.g., complexometric titrations of Fe³⁺), water treatment, and clinical iron-overload therapies, whereas NTA, though a respectable chelator, simply cannot match EDTA's grip on this hard, highly charged metal ion.
