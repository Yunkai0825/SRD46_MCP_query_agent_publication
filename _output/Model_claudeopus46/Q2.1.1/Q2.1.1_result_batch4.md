**EDTA wins decisively.** It forms a vastly more stable 1:1 complex with Fe³⁺ than NTA does, with a difference of about **9 log units** under identical conditions.

### Why EDTA is so much stronger

The ~9 log-unit advantage of EDTA over NTA reflects the **chelate effect** and **denticity difference**. EDTA is a hexadentate ligand (2 N + 4 carboxylate O donors) that wraps completely around Fe³⁺, whereas NTA is only tetradentate (1 N + 3 carboxylate O donors). The two additional donor groups in EDTA provide extra coordination bonds and a much larger favorable entropy change (ΔS = 443 vs 350 J/mol·K), which is the dominant thermodynamic driving force for both complexes. This enormous stability difference is why EDTA is the preferred chelator in analytical chemistry (e.g., complexometric titrations of Fe³⁺) and in industrial applications for iron sequestration.
