### Chemistry Insights

1. **Fe³⁺ binds most strongly** (log K ≈ 28), which is why DTPA is widely used as an industrial iron sequestrant in pulp bleaching and water treatment. The enormous ~12 log-unit gap between Fe³⁺ and Fe²⁺ reflects the much higher charge density of the ferric ion.

2. **Lanthanides cluster tightly** around log K ≈ 21–23, making DTPA the backbone of the classic MRI contrast agent **Gd-DTPA (Magnevist®)**. The high thermodynamic stability ensures the toxic Gd³⁺ ion stays chelated *in vivo*.

3. DTPA generally exceeds EDTA by **2–3 log K units** for most metals, thanks to its additional fifth carboxylate arm providing an extra coordination site — a textbook example of the chelate effect amplified by denticity.
