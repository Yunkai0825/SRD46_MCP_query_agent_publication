### Key Insights

**DTPA is an exceptionally powerful chelator** — its eight donor atoms (3 N + 5 carboxylate O) form highly stable complexes, generally ~1–2 log units stronger than EDTA for the same metal. The strongest binding is with Bi³⁺ (log β = 33.6), followed by Fe³⁺ (28.6), reflecting the preference of this octadentate ligand for high-charge, hard metal ions.

**Clinically**, the Gd³⁺-DTPA complex (Magnevist®) is one of the most widely used MRI contrast agents, and Ca-DTPA/Zn-DTPA are FDA-approved for decorporation of transuranic elements (Pu, Am, Cm) — their enormous stability constants ensure the chelator can strip these metals from biological tissue.

**The Irving-Williams trend** is clearly visible among the divalent ions: Cu²⁺ > Ni²⁺ > Co²⁺ ≈ Zn²⁺ > Fe²⁺ > Mn²⁺ > Ca²⁺ > Mg²⁺, consistent with crystal-field stabilization effects.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
