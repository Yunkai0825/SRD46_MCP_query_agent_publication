Below are the 1:1 formation constants for the equilibrium **M + L ⇌ ML** from the NIST SRD-46 database. DTPA has stability data with **44 different metals** in the database — an impressively broad dataset.

## Key Chemistry Insights

1. **DTPA is an octadentate chelator** (3 nitrogen + 5 carboxylate oxygen donors), which explains its extraordinarily high log K values — generally **2–3 log units higher than EDTA** for the same metals.

2. **The Gd³⁺–DTPA complex (log K₁ = 20.85)** is the basis of the MRI contrast agent **Magnevist® (Gd-DTPA)**, one of the most widely used gadolinium-based contrast agents in clinical medicine. The high thermodynamic stability is essential for safe in vivo use.

3. **Selectivity pattern:** DTPA strongly favors hard, highly charged cations (Fe³⁺ at 28.0, Bi³⁺ at 33.0, Hf⁴⁺ at 29.5) over softer divalent ions. Among the **lanthanides**, log K increases steadily from La³⁺ (19.48) to Lu³⁺ (22.44), reflecting the **lanthanide contraction** — smaller ions fit the DTPA binding cavity more snugly, enhancing electrostatic and donor–acceptor interactions.

4. **Alkaline earth metals** (Ba²⁺ → Ca²⁺, log K ≈ 8.6–10.7) are bound much more weakly than transition metals or lanthanides, which is why DTPA can selectively chelate toxic or diagnostic metals even in the presence of physiological Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
