## Chemistry Insights

The **recommended values at 25 °C, I = 0** are log β₁ = **8.56** and log β₂ = **15.70**, making the stepwise log K₂ ≈ 7.14 — only slightly lower than log K₁, which is characteristic of Cu(II) amino acid complexes where the second glycinate binds in a *trans* square-planar arrangement with minimal steric penalty. The strongly negative ΔH values confirm that complex formation is enthalpy-driven (ligand-field stabilization of the d⁹ Cu²⁺ ion), while the positive ΔS contributions reflect desolvation of both the metal aqua ion and the glycinate anion upon chelation. The modest decrease in log K with increasing ionic strength (at 25 °C) follows the expected Debye–Hückel trend for reactions that reduce the net charge of the species in solution.
