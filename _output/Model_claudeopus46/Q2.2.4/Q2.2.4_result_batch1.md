## Ligand Classes in SRD-46

The database contains **60 distinct ligand classes** (including the "Ligands not selected" catch-all category for unclassified ligands).

### Top 5 Ligand Classes by Number of Ligands

| Rank | Ligand Class | Ligand Count | % of Total |
|------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Ligands not selected | 1,339 | 23.3% |
| 2 | Amino Acids | 297 | 5.2% |
| 3 | Aliphatic amines | 250 | 4.4% |
| 4 | Pyridines (azines) | 248 | 4.3% |
| 5 | Carboxylic acids | 182 | 3.2% |

> **Note:** "Ligands not selected" is a catch-all for ligands that haven't been assigned to a specific class. If we exclude it, the 5th spot goes to **Aza-macrocycles** (165 ligands, 2.9%).

### Chemistry Insight

The dominance of **amino acids** and **aliphatic amines** reflects the importance of nitrogen-donor ligands in coordination chemistry — the lone pair on nitrogen is an excellent σ-donor for metal binding. **Pyridines** rank similarly high thanks to their aromatic N-donor character and widespread use in catalysis and supramolecular chemistry. The strong representation of **carboxylic acids** highlights the role of hard oxygen donors, particularly for binding oxophilic metals like lanthanides and early transition metals.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Standard Reference Database 46 — Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
