## Ligand Classes in NIST SRD-46

The database contains **60 distinct ligand classes** across 5,750 ligand entries.

### Top 5 Classes by Number of Ligands

| Rank | Ligand Class | Ligand Count | % of Total |
|------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | Ligands not selected | 1,339 | 23.3% |
| 2 | Amino Acids | 297 | 5.2% |
| 3 | Aliphatic amines | 250 | 4.4% |
| 4 | Pyridines (azines) | 248 | 4.3% |
| 5 | Carboxylic acids | 182 | 3.2% |

> **Note:** The largest category, "Ligands not selected," is a catch-all for ligands that haven't been assigned to a specific chemical class. If we exclude that bin, the **top 5 named classes** are **Amino Acids** (297), **Aliphatic amines** (250), **Pyridines/azines** (248), **Carboxylic acids** (182), and **Aza-macrocycles** (165).

### Chemistry Insight

The dominance of amino acids and aliphatic amines reflects the historical importance of biologically relevant ligands in coordination chemistry — amino acids are ubiquitous metal binders in biological systems, while simple amines serve as fundamental N-donor ligands. Pyridine derivatives rank highly because the aromatic nitrogen is an excellent σ-donor, making pyridines workhorses in both analytical and industrial coordination chemistry.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
