### Chemistry Insight

The Gajda et al. (2005) study provides a thorough speciation picture for the Zn²⁺–histidine system. The log K₁ values of 6.5–6.6 and log β₂ values of 12.1–12.6 are consistent with histidine acting as a strong tridentate chelator (via the amino group, carboxylate, and imidazole nitrogen) for Zn²⁺ — a biologically critical interaction, since histidine residues are among the most common zinc-binding motifs in metalloproteins such as carbonic anhydrase and zinc-finger domains. The protonation constants for the complexes (log K ≈ 4.9–5.9) reflect protonation of the imidazole nitrogen in the coordinated ligand, which is notably more acidic than in free histidine (pKₐ ≈ 6.0), consistent with electron density being drawn toward the metal centre upon coordination.
