## Prediction: **Cysteine** (if unstudied) or **Lysine** — but the best candidate among truly unstudied amino acids is **Tyrosine**

### Reasoning

**1. Donor atom hierarchy for Zn²⁺:**
Zn²⁺ is a borderline Lewis acid that strongly favors nitrogen and sulfur donors. The Irving-Williams series and HSAB theory tell us the donor preference for Zn²⁺ is roughly: **thiolate S⁻ > imidazole N > amine N > carboxylate O⁻ > phenolate O⁻ > amide O > hydroxyl OH**.

**2. Observed trends from the data:**
- Simple α-amino acids (Ala, Val, Leu, Ile) with only NH₂/COO⁻ donors give log β₁ ≈ 4.6.
- Adding a second carboxylate (Asp) raises log β₁ to ~5.9 — tridentate chelation is very effective.
- Glycine, the smallest, gives log β₁ ≈ 5.2–5.5, slightly higher than branched-chain amino acids due to less steric strain.

## 🏆 Prediction: **L-Lysine** is the amino acid most likely to form the most stable Zn²⁺ complex among unstudied candidates

### Key Arguments:

1. **Tridentate N,O,N chelation:** Lysine's ε-amino group (pKa 10.66) provides a third nitrogen donor, forming a potential tridentate chelate with the α-amino and carboxylate groups. Zn²⁺ strongly prefers nitrogen donors over oxygen donors.

2. **Flexible chain length:** The four-carbon spacer between the α-carbon and ε-NH₂ allows formation of a strain-free macrochelate ring, which is geometrically favorable for Zn²⁺'s tetrahedral/octahedral coordination.

3. **pKa considerations:** At physiological pH (~7.4), the ε-NH₂ (pKa 10.66) is protonated, but metal coordination shifts the effective pKa downward. The α-amino group (pKa 9.15) is similarly available. Both nitrogens can coordinate Zn²⁺ with moderate competition from protons.

4. **Analogy with Aspartic acid:** Asp's extra carboxylate raised log β₁ from ~4.6 (simple amino acids) to ~5.9 — a gain of ~1.3 log units from tridentate chelation. Lysine's extra amine (a better Zn²⁺ donor than carboxylate) should provide an even larger enhancement, potentially giving **log β₁ ≈ 6.0–6.5**.

5. **Biological precedent:** In zinc finger proteins and metalloenzymes, Zn²⁺ is almost always coordinated by nitrogen (histidine imidazole) and sulfur (cysteine thiolate) donors — confirming Zn²⁺'s strong preference for N and S over O.

**Runner-up:** Tyrosine, with its phenolate donor (pKa 10.10), could also form a moderately stable tridentate complex, but phenolate oxygen is a weaker donor for Zn²⁺ than an amine nitrogen, placing it below lysine in predicted stability.
