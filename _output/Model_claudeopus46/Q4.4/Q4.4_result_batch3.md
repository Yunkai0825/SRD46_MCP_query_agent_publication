## Analysis and Prediction

### What Makes a Strong Zn²⁺–Amino Acid Complex?

Zn²⁺ is a borderline Lewis acid (HSAB theory) that binds well to both nitrogen and sulfur donors. The stability data confirms the expected trend:

1. **Cysteine** (thiolate donor) gives the **highest** log β values with Zn²⁺ — up to ~9.8 for ML and ~18.2 for ML₂. The thiolate S⁻ (pKa 8.18) is an excellent soft donor that matches Zn²⁺'s borderline character, and it provides a third coordination site beyond the standard {NH₂, COO⁻} chelate.

2. **Histidine** (imidazole N donor, pKa 6.05) is the next strongest binder — the imidazole nitrogen is deprotonated and available for coordination at physiological pH, creating a tridentate {NH₂, COO⁻, N_im} chelate.

3. **Simple amino acids** (glycine, alanine, etc.) with only {NH₂, COO⁻} donors give the weakest complexes (log β₁ ≈ 5).

### Key Principle: Additional side-chain donors with pKa values accessible near neutral pH dramatically enhance Zn²⁺ stability.

### Which Standard Amino Acid Lacking Zn²⁺ Data Would Form the Strongest Complex?

Among the 20 standard amino acids, the ones most likely to **lack** comprehensive Zn²⁺ stability data in SRD-46 (based on lower total VLM counts and the absence from the catalog) while possessing strong donor potential are:

**🏆 Prediction: Tyrosine (ligand_5819)**

**Reasoning:**

| Factor | Tyrosine | Comparison |
|---|---|---|
| Donor set | NH₂ + COO⁻ + **phenolate O⁻** | Tridentate, like Cys and His |
| Side-chain pKa | 10.10 (phenol) | Accessible at mildly basic pH |
| Donor atom | O⁻ (hard donor) | Zn²⁺ binds O well (borderline acid) |
| Chelate ring | 5-membered (amino acid) + 6-membered (phenolate) | Favorable geometry |
| Analogy | o-Tyrosine (ligand_5817, pKa 8.67 for phenol) has 71 VLM entries — likely studied | Lower phenol pKa = even better |

Tyrosine's phenolate oxygen provides a **third donor atom** beyond the standard glycinate-type {NH₂, COO⁻} chelate. While the phenol pKa of 10.1 means the phenolate is mostly protonated at pH 7, Zn²⁺ coordination itself lowers the effective pKa (metal-promoted deprotonation), making the tridentate mode accessible. This is analogous to how Zn²⁺ promotes thiol deprotonation in cysteine.

The predicted log β₁ for Zn²⁺–tyrosine would be **intermediate between histidine (~6.5–7) and simple amino acids (~5)**, likely around **log β₁ ≈ 6–7**, because:
- The phenolate O⁻ is a harder donor than imidazole N, and Zn²⁺ has moderate affinity for hard O-donors
- The higher pKa (10.1 vs. 6.05 for histidine) means less favorable deprotonation at neutral pH
- But the tridentate chelation still provides a significant stability boost over bidentate amino acids

### Runner-up: Lysine

Lysine has a side-chain ε-amino group (pKa 9.15) that could provide an additional N-donor. However, the long flexible chain (4 CH₂ groups) creates an unfavorable large chelate ring (8-membered), reducing the chelate effect. Its predicted stability would be only slightly above glycine.
