### Key Takeaway

**Ethylenediamine binds Ni²⁺ roughly 10⁴·⁶ ≈ 40 000 times more strongly than ammonia** in terms of the first stepwise formation constant. This dramatic enhancement is a textbook illustration of the **chelate effect**: ethylenediamine, a bidentate ligand with two –NH₂ donor groups, forms a five-membered chelate ring upon coordination, gaining a large entropic advantage over two separate ammonia molecules binding sequentially. The thermodynamic data in SRD-46 confirm this — the ΔH for en binding is near zero at low ionic strength while ΔS is strongly positive, whereas ammonia binding is enthalpy-driven (ΔH ≈ −15 kJ/mol) with a much smaller entropy contribution.
