### Key Result

**Ethylenediamine binds Ni²⁺ far more strongly than ammonia.** At 25 °C and zero ionic strength, the difference is:

> **Δ log K₁ = 7.32 − 2.72 = 4.60 log units**

This means the Ni(en)²⁺ complex is roughly **40,000 times** more stable than Ni(NH₃)²⁺ (10⁴·⁶ ≈ 4 × 10⁴).

### Chemistry Insight

This dramatic enhancement is a textbook illustration of the **chelate effect**. Ethylenediamine, a bidentate ligand with two –NH₂ donor groups connected by an ethylene bridge, forms a five-membered chelate ring upon coordination to Ni²⁺. Although each individual Ni–N bond is comparable in strength to the Ni–NH₃ bond, the chelate ring closure provides a large favorable entropy contribution (releasing fewer particles from solution per donor atom coordinated), boosting the overall stability constant by ~4.6 log units. This is why chelating ligands like en are preferred in applications such as metal ion sequestration and electroplating baths.
