### Chemical Insight

**Ethylenediamine binds Ni²⁺ roughly 10⁴·⁶ ≈ 40,000 times more strongly** than ammonia in the first complexation step. This dramatic enhancement is a textbook illustration of the **chelate effect**: ethylenediamine is a bidentate ligand that forms a five-membered chelate ring with Ni²⁺, coordinating through both nitrogen atoms simultaneously. The entropic advantage of displacing two monodentate water molecules with a single chelating ligand, combined with the favorable ring geometry, accounts for the ~4.6 log-unit gain in stability. This is why ethylenediamine complexes are widely used in analytical chemistry and metal-ion separations where high complex stability is required.
