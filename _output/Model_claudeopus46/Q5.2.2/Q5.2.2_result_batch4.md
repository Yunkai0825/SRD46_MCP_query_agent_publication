## Metal–Protein Binding in SRD-46

**SRD-46 does not contain stability constant data for intact proteins** such as transferrin, serum albumin, or ferritin. The database is designed for **small-molecule and short-peptide ligand–metal equilibria**, so macromolecular binding data falls outside its scope.

### What SRD-46 *does* have: Polypeptide Ligands

The database includes **98 polypeptide-class ligands** — short synthetic or natural peptides (typically 2–6 residues). These serve as excellent **model systems** for understanding metal–protein binding-site chemistry. A sample of well-studied polypeptides:

| Ligand | Ligand ID | Metal Systems (VLM count) |
|--------|-----------|--------------------------|
| Tetraglycine (Gly₄) | ligand_6595 | 46 |
| Fibrinopeptide A fragment | ligand_6692 | 12 |
| L-Ala₄ | ligand_6597 | 10 |
| Gly-Pro-Gly-Gly | ligand_6610 | 7 |
| Gly-Sar-Gly-Gly | ligand_6606 | 7 |
| L-Ala-L-Pro-L-Ala-L-Ala | ligand_6614 | 7 |

These peptides coordinate metals primarily through their **terminal amine**, **deprotonated amide nitrogens**, and **carboxylate** groups — the same donor-atom types relevant to metal–protein interactions.

### Relevance to Transferrin Chemistry

While transferrin itself is absent, SRD-46 has extensive data for iron and other biologically relevant metals bound to small ligands that model protein binding sites:

- **Individual amino acids** (glycine, histidine, cysteine, tyrosine — the key donor groups in transferrin's binding cleft)
- **Siderophore-like chelators** (citrate, EDTA, desferrioxamine) commonly used in iron–transferrin competition studies
- **Short peptides** (the 98 polypeptides above) that approximate the coordination environment of protein metal-binding sites

### Recommendation

For actual protein-level binding constants (e.g., Fe³⁺–transferrin log K ≈ 20–22), you would need specialized databases or primary literature. However, SRD-46's amino acid, peptide, and chelator data can provide valuable thermodynamic context for understanding and modeling metal–protein interactions at the molecular level.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46*
