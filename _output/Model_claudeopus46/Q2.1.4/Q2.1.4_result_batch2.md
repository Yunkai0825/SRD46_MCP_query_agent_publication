### Chemical Insight

This result follows the general trend for borderline-hard Cu²⁺: it preferentially binds the harder, smaller chloride over the softer, larger bromide — consistent with Pearson's Hard-Soft Acid-Base (HSAB) principle. The very small magnitudes of these log K values (near zero) reflect the inherently weak nature of halide complexation with divalent copper in aqueous solution, where water molecules compete effectively as ligands. Interestingly, the entropy contribution is actually *more favorable* for bromide (ΔS = 34.3 vs. 29.3 J/mol·K), but this is offset by a less favorable enthalpy balance, resulting in the net thermodynamic preference for chloride.
