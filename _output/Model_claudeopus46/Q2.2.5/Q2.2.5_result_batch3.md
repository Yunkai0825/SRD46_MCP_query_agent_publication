## Distribution of Constant Types in SRD-46 Stability Measurements

The NIST SRD-46 database contains **79,063** stability constant measurements, distributed across three constant types:

| Constant Type | Count | Percentage |
|---------------|---------|------------|
| **K** (stepwise equilibrium constant) | 60,540 | 76.57% |
| **H** (protonation/hydrolysis constant) | 9,693 | 12.26% |
| **S** (overall stability constant, β) | 8,830 | 11.17% |

### Key Observations

- **K-type constants** dominate the database, accounting for over three-quarters of all entries. These represent stepwise equilibrium constants for successive ligand binding (e.g., ML, ML₂, ML₃…).
- **H-type constants** (≈12%) capture protonation or hydrolysis equilibria, which are essential for understanding speciation in aqueous systems at varying pH.
- **S-type constants** (≈11%) represent cumulative (overall) stability constants (β values), which describe the formation of a complex directly from the free metal and ligands in a single expression.

The heavy weighting toward stepwise constants reflects the experimental tradition in coordination chemistry, where successive formation constants are typically measured and reported individually rather than as cumulative products.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
