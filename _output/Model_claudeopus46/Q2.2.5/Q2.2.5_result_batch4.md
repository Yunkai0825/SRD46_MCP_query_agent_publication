## Distribution of Constant Types in SRD-46 Stability Measurements

The NIST SRD-46 database contains **79,063** stability constant measurements, distributed across three constant types:

| Constant Type | Description | Count | Percentage |
|:---:|---|---:|---:|
| **K** | Stepwise stability constant (Kₙ) | 60,540 | 76.57% |
| **H** | Hydrolysis constant | 9,693 | 12.26% |
| **S** | Overall stability constant (βₙ) | 8,830 | 11.17% |

### Key Observations

- **Stepwise constants (K)** dominate the database, accounting for over three-quarters of all measurements. This reflects the common experimental practice of reporting equilibrium constants for successive ligand-binding steps (M + L ⇌ ML, ML + L ⇌ ML₂, etc.).
- **Hydrolysis constants (H)** make up ~12% of entries, capturing the important chemistry of metal-ion hydrolysis in aqueous solution — a critical factor in environmental and biological speciation.
- **Overall (cumulative) stability constants (S/β)** represent ~11% of the data. These describe the global formation reaction (M + nL ⇌ MLₙ) and are especially useful for thermodynamic modeling and speciation calculations.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
