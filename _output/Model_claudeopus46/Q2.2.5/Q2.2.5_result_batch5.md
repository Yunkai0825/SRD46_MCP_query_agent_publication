## Distribution of Constant Types in SRD-46 Stability Measurements

The NIST SRD-46 database contains **79,063** stability constant measurements, distributed across three constant types:

| Constant Type | Description | Count | Percentage |
|:---:|---|---:|---:|
| **K** | Stepwise stability constant (log *K*) | 60,540 | 76.57% |
| **H** | Overall (cumulative) stability constant (log *β*) | 9,693 | 12.26% |
| **S** | Solubility / other special constants | 8,830 | 11.17% |

### Key Observations

- **Stepwise constants (K)** dominate the database, accounting for over three-quarters of all entries. This reflects the common experimental practice of reporting equilibrium constants for successive ligand-binding steps (e.g., ML, ML₂, ML₃…).
- **Cumulative constants (H / β)** make up about 12% of entries. These represent the overall formation constant from the free metal and free ligand directly to the MLₙ species.
- **S-type constants** (~11%) cover solubility products and other specialized equilibrium definitions.

This distribution is typical of coordination chemistry databases, where stepwise constants are the most frequently measured and reported quantities.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
