## Chemistry Insights

1. **Aminopolycarboxylates dominate thermodynamic affinity.** EDTA and DTPA form the most stable 1:1 complexes with Pb²⁺ (log K ≈ 18–19) among clinically relevant agents. Their hexadentate/octadentate coordination saturates Pb²⁺'s coordination sphere, yielding very high formation constants.

2. **Thiol-based chelators trade thermodynamic strength for pharmacological advantages.** DMSA (succimer) and BAL have lower log K values for Pb²⁺ but are preferred clinically because they can be given orally (DMSA) or intramuscularly (BAL), cross cell membranes more readily, and are less likely to redistribute lead to the brain — a critical safety consideration in pediatric patients.

3. **The highest-affinity ligand overall — PhDTA (log K = 25.46) — is not used clinically** due to poor water solubility and toxicity concerns. This illustrates that thermodynamic stability alone does not determine clinical utility; selectivity over essential metals (Ca²⁺, Zn²⁺, Fe³⁺), pharmacokinetics, and safety profile are equally important in chelation therapy design.

⚠️ *This is a scientific data summary from NIST SRD-46, not medical advice. Chelation therapy protocols should be designed by qualified toxicologists/physicians.*
