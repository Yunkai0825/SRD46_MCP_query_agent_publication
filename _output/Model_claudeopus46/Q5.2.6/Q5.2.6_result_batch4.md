## Result

**No stability constants measured in supercritical CO₂ are available in the NIST SRD-46 database.**

The database contains **79,063** stability-constant measurements, and **100% of them** were measured in water (H₂O) as the solvent. There are no entries for supercritical CO₂ — or any non-aqueous solvent, for that matter.

### Why?

NIST SRD-46 (the *Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes* database) is focused exclusively on **aqueous-phase** equilibrium data. Supercritical CO₂ (scCO₂) is used in some specialized extraction and coordination chemistry studies, but those data are not compiled in this particular reference database. For metal-complex equilibria in scCO₂, you would need to consult primary literature sources (e.g., studies on uranyl–TBP or lanthanide–β-diketonate complexes in scCO₂).

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46*
