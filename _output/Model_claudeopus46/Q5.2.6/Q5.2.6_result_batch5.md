## Result

**No stability constants measured in supercritical CO₂ were found in the NIST SRD-46 database.**

The database contains **79,063** stability-constant measurements, and **100%** of them were recorded in **water (H₂O)** as the solvent. There are no entries for supercritical CO₂ (or any other non-aqueous solvent) in this dataset.

### Why?

NIST SRD-46 (the **NIST Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes** database) is specifically focused on **aqueous-phase** equilibrium data. It was designed to compile and critically evaluate metal–ligand stability constants determined in aqueous solution. Measurements in exotic media such as supercritical CO₂, ionic liquids, or organic solvents fall outside its scope.

If you need stability data in supercritical CO₂, you might consider:
- **Primary literature** searches (e.g., via SciFinder or Web of Science) — some studies have examined metal–chelate complexes in scCO₂ for applications like supercritical fluid extraction.
- **IUPAC Stability Constants Database (SC-Database)** — which has broader solvent coverage in some areas.

*Source: NIST SRD-46*
