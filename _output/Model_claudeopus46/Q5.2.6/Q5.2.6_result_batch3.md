## Result

**No stability constants measured in supercritical CO₂ are available in the NIST SRD-46 database.**

The database contains **79,063** stability constant measurements, and **100% of them** were measured in water (H₂O) as the solvent. There are no entries for supercritical carbon dioxide (scCO₂) or any other non-aqueous solvent.

### Why?

This is not surprising — the NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes) is specifically focused on **aqueous-phase** metal–ligand equilibria. Supercritical CO₂ is a very different medium:

- **scCO₂** is a non-polar solvent used primarily in extraction and green chemistry applications. While metal-ligand complexation in scCO₂ has been studied (e.g., for nuclear waste processing with organophosphorus ligands or β-diketones), these data are typically found in specialized literature rather than in classical aqueous stability constant compilations.
- Measuring thermodynamic stability constants in scCO₂ is experimentally challenging due to the high pressures involved and the lack of a well-defined ionic strength framework analogous to aqueous solutions.

If you're interested in metal complexation in scCO₂, I'd recommend searching journals like *The Journal of Supercritical Fluids* or *Industrial & Engineering Chemistry Research* rather than aqueous equilibrium databases.

*Source: NIST SRD-46*
