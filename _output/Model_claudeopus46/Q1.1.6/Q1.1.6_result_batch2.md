This is a sizable collection, and as one might expect, virtually all of them (100%) contain a **carboxyl group**, which is the hallmark of amino acids. A majority (57%) feature a **primary amine**, consistent with standard α-amino acids, while about a third include **aromatic rings** (e.g., phenylalanine, tryptophan derivatives) and roughly a quarter have **hydroxyl groups** (e.g., serine, threonine derivatives). The class also includes modified and non-standard amino acids with thioether, thiol, amide, and other functional groups.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
