**Chemistry insight:** Cu²⁺'s dominance is unsurprising — it is one of the most versatile and widely studied metal ions in coordination chemistry. Its d⁹ electronic configuration allows it to form stable complexes with an enormous variety of N-, O-, and S-donor ligands, and it is central to bioinorganic chemistry, catalysis, and environmental science. The Irving–Williams series also predicts Cu²⁺ to form the most stable divalent first-row transition metal complexes, which has historically motivated extensive study of its equilibria.
