### Chemistry Insights

1. **Entropy-driven complexation:** The positive ΔS values for all three species (rising from ~+29 to ~+67 J/mol·K as the complex grows) indicate that Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ binding is predominantly **entropy-driven**, consistent with the release of ordered hydration-shell water upon complexation — a hallmark of "soft" metal–halide interactions.

2. **Ionic-strength dependence:** log β₁ rises from ~1.35 at I = 0.5 M to ~1.98 at I = 0 (extrapolated), reflecting the activity-coefficient corrections expected for a 2+/1− → 1+ charge neutralisation reaction. The trend is well-captured by the SIT or Davies equations.

3. **Environmental relevance:** In seawater (I ≈ 0.7 M, [Cl⁻] ≈ 0.55 M), CdCl⁺ and CdCl₂ are the dominant Cd species, dramatically increasing Cd bioavailability and mobility relative to freshwater — a key concern in marine toxicology and geochemistry.
