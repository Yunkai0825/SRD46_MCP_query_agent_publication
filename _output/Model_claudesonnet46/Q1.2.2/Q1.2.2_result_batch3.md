## Cd²⁺–Chloride Stability Constants: Literature Sources, Values & Conditions

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*

---

### Overview

The SRD-46 database holds **38 VLM (value-measurement) records** for the Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system (metal_26 / ligand_5781). These 38 records all resolve to a **single recommended network** of four stepwise complexes, and the citation evidence shows they all trace back to **one primary experimental source** — the landmark 1947 paper by Schwarzenbach & Ackermann — which has been cited and re-evaluated across **38 successive NIST/IUPAC critical compilation volumes** (shortcut codes 41Sc through 83Sc). The SRD-46 architecture records each compilation edition as a separate citation entry, which is why the VLM count is 38 rather than 38 independent experimental studies.

### Primary Literature Source

> **Schwarzenbach, G.; Ackermann, H.** *Helv. Chim. Acta* **1947**, *30*, 1798.

This single experimental paper is the foundation for all 38 SRD-46 records. Each record corresponds to one edition of the NIST critical compilation series (shortcut codes 41Sc–83Sc) that reviewed and re-endorsed these values over decades.

---

### Chemistry Insight

The Cd²⁺–Cl⁻ system is notable for its **moderate but non-trivial chloro-complex formation** — log β₁ = 1.32 is much weaker than, say, Cd²⁺–EDTA (log β ≈ 16), but strong enough to be environmentally significant. In seawater and chloride-rich groundwaters, a substantial fraction of dissolved Cd²⁺ exists as CdCl⁺ and CdCl₂, which dramatically affects its bioavailability and toxicity. The fact that β₃ > β₄ (log β drops from 1.80 to 1.46 for the fourth chloride) reflects steric crowding in the tetrachloro complex. The remarkable consistency of the recommended value across 38 compilation editions spanning ~40 years underscores the robustness of the original Schwarzenbach & Ackermann measurement.
