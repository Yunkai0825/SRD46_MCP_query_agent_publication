### Chemistry Context

The log K of **18.78** is among the largest formation constants for any divalent metal–EDTA complex, reflecting EDTA's hexadentate chelate effect (four carboxylate + two amine donors forming a highly stable five-membered ring system with Cu²⁺). This 1949 paper by Ackermann and Schwarzenbach was part of the foundational work by the Schwarzenbach group in Zürich that established EDTA as the paradigmatic polyaminocarboxylate chelator — work that transformed analytical chemistry and clinical medicine alike. The value has since been confirmed by **30 independent studies** spanning 1949–1975, all converging on log K ≈ 18.8 at 25 °C, I = 0.1 M KNO₃.
