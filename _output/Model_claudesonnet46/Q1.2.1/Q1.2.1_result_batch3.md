### Chemistry Context

The log K = 18.78 for the 1:1 Cu²⁺–EDTA complex is one of the highest among divalent transition metals, reflecting the exceptional chelate effect of EDTA's four carboxylate and two amine donor groups forming a highly stable octahedral cage around Cu²⁺. Schwarzenbach's 1949 paper was a landmark in coordination chemistry — it was part of the foundational series by Schwarzenbach's group in Zürich that established EDTA as the quintessential analytical chelating agent and introduced the concept of conditional stability constants. The value has been reproduced by dozens of subsequent studies (49 citations in SRD-46 alone), confirming its reliability.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
