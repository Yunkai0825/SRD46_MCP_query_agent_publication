## Chemistry Highlights

**1. Trivalent metals dominate:** Fe³⁺, Bi³⁺, and Th⁴⁺ show the highest affinities (log β > 25), reflecting DTPA's eight donor atoms (3 N + 5 O carboxylates) perfectly complementing high-charge-density metal ions.

**2. Lanthanide selectivity:** DTPA binds lanthanides (Sm³⁺, Eu³⁺, Nd³⁺, Yb³⁺) with log β ≈ 21–23, which is why **Gd-DTPA (Magnevist®)** was the first approved MRI contrast agent — strong enough to be safe, yet thermodynamically stable in vivo.

**3. Alkaline earth metals bind weakly:** Ca²⁺ (log β ≈ 10.8) and Mg²⁺ (log β ≈ 9.3) bind ~10 orders of magnitude more weakly than Fe³⁺, which is why DTPA can selectively chelate heavy/toxic metals even in biological fluids rich in Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes), ligand_6356.
