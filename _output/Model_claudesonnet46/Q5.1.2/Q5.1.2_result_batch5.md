### Chemistry Highlights

- **Enormous range:** DTPA log β values span ~12 orders of magnitude (Mn²⁺ = 15.6 to Fe³⁺ = 27.5), driven by differences in metal charge density and coordination geometry.
- **Lanthanide contraction:** Lanthanide log β values increase steadily from La³⁺ (19.5) to Lu³⁺ (22.4) as the ionic radius shrinks across the series, producing a tighter fit in DTPA's cavity. This is the thermodynamic basis for Gd³⁺–DTPA (Magnevist®) as an MRI contrast agent — log β ≈ 21.4 is large enough to prevent Gd³⁺ release under physiological conditions.
- **Fe³⁺ vs Fe²⁺:** The 11-unit difference between Fe³⁺ (27.5) and Fe²⁺ (16.5) illustrates how oxidation state dominates chelation strength, and explains DTPA's use in iron chelation therapy and nuclear medicine for trivalent radiometals such as ⁹⁰Y and ¹¹¹In.
