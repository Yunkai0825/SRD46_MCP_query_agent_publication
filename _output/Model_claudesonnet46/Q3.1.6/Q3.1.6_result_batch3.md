### Chemistry Insights

1. **The chelate effect drives the ranking.** The aminopolycarboxylate ligands (DTPA > EDTA > NTA) follow a clear trend: more donor atoms → more chelate rings → higher thermodynamic stability. DTPA's eight donor atoms are ideally matched to the large La³⁺ ion (ionic radius ~1.03 Å for 9-coordinate), which is why DTPA-gadolinium and DTPA-lanthanide chelates are the basis of clinical MRI contrast agents.

2. **Nitrogen donors matter.** Even though this question focuses on carboxylic acids, the nitrogen atoms in DTPA, EDTA, and NTA contribute significantly to stability through additional coordination bonds. Pure carboxylate ligands (citrate, oxalate) are far weaker because they lack these nitrogen donors.

3. **Citrate and oxalate remain geochemically important.** Despite their lower log K values, citric and oxalic acids are ubiquitous in soils and biological fluids, making them key players in natural lanthanide mobilization, plant uptake, and rare-earth element cycling in the environment.
