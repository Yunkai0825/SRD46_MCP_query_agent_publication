### Chemistry Insights

1. **Strong, stepwise complexation:** The stepwise log K values (≈ 5.8, 5.5, 4.7 estimated from cumulative differences) are remarkably consistent across ionic strengths, indicating that ionic medium has little effect on this system — typical for a neutral ligand like 2,2'-bipyridyl.

2. **Chelate effect:** The high overall stability (log β₃ ≈ 16) reflects the strong chelate effect of the bidentate bipyridyl ligand, which forms five-membered chelate rings with Co²⁺. This is why Co(bipy)₃²⁺ is a classic coordination chemistry benchmark.

3. **Thermodynamic completeness:** The database is unusually complete for this system — it includes not just log β but also ΔH and ΔS, allowing full Gibbs energy decomposition (ΔG = ΔH − TΔS) for all three complexation steps.
