### Chemistry Insights

- **Stepwise log K values** can be derived: K₁ ≈ 5.8, K₂ ≈ 5.5, K₃ ≈ 4.7–4.9 — a modest but consistent decrease with each successive ligand addition, consistent with statistical and steric factors in octahedral Co(II) complexes.
- The **large negative ΔH values** (−34 to −89 kJ/mol cumulative) confirm that complex formation is strongly enthalpy-driven, typical of the chelate effect with rigid aromatic N-donors like 2,2'-bipy.
- The data are remarkably **insensitive to ionic strength** (I = 0.1 vs. 1.0 M), with log β differing by ≤0.2 log units — suggesting the charge-neutral nature of the overall reaction minimizes activity coefficient effects.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
