### 🏆 Winner: EDTA — by ~9.2 log units

**EDTA forms a dramatically more stable complex with Fe³⁺ than NTA.** The difference of ~9 orders of magnitude in the formation constant reflects EDTA's hexadentate coordination (4 carboxylate + 2 amine donors) versus NTA's tetradentate coordination (3 carboxylate + 1 amine), giving EDTA a far superior chelate effect.

**Thermodynamic insight:** At I = 0.1 M, 25 °C, the Fe³⁺–EDTA system also shows ΔH = −11.3 kJ/mol and ΔS = +442.7 J/mol·K, while Fe³⁺–NTA shows ΔH = +13.4 kJ/mol and ΔS = +349.8 J/mol·K. The EDTA complex is both enthalpically and entropically favored — a rare combination that underscores why EDTA is so widely used in iron chelation therapy, analytical chemistry, and industrial water treatment.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Standard Reference Database 46)*
