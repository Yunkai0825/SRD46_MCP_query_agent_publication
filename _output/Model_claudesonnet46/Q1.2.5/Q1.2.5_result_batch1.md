### Chemistry Context

The value **log β₁ = 1.59** for PbCl⁺ at 25 °C and zero ionic strength is a well-established benchmark. Lead(II) forms relatively weak chloride complexes compared to, say, Hg²⁺ or Cd²⁺, because Pb²⁺ is a borderline soft acid and Cl⁻ is a borderline soft base — the interaction is real but modest. In seawater and other chloride-rich environments, this modest affinity is nonetheless environmentally significant: a meaningful fraction of dissolved Pb²⁺ is speciated as PbCl⁺ and PbCl₂(aq), reducing the concentration of the free ion and affecting bioavailability and toxicity.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes Database)*
