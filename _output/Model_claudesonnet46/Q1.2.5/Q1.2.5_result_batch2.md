### Chemistry Insight

The log β₁ = 1.62 for PbCl⁺ at 25 °C and zero ionic strength reflects a moderately weak 1:1 complex. Pb²⁺ is a borderline-to-soft Lewis acid that binds Cl⁻ (a soft donor) more strongly than hard metals like Ca²⁺ or Mg²⁺, but far less strongly than Hg²⁺ (log β₁ ≈ 7). This moderate affinity is environmentally significant: in seawater or chloride-rich groundwater, a substantial fraction of dissolved lead exists as PbCl⁺ and higher chloro-complexes (PbCl₂, PbCl₃⁻), reducing free Pb²⁺ activity and influencing both bioavailability and remediation strategies. Both citations trace back to L. G. Sillén's landmark *Stability Constants* compilations — the foundational references for inorganic complex equilibria.
