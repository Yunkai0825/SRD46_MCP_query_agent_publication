## 🏆 Winner: **Hg²⁺** — by an enormous margin

**Hg²⁺ has the highest affinity for thiocyanate**, with log β₄ = **21.8** — roughly **18 log units** above Cd²⁺ (3.6) and **20 log units** above Pb²⁺ (1.0). This is a textbook demonstration of the **HSAB (Hard-Soft Acid-Base) principle**:

- **Hg²⁺** is the archetypal **soft acid**: large ionic radius, high polarizability, filled d¹⁰ shell, and low charge density. It forms exceptionally strong bonds with **soft donor atoms** like **sulfur**.
- **SCN⁻ is ambidentate** — it can bind through N (harder end) or S (softer end). Soft metals like Hg²⁺ bind preferentially through **sulfur**, gaining extra stabilization from covalent/back-bonding interactions.
- **Cd²⁺** is borderline-soft and shows moderate affinity; it also forms a tetrakis complex but with far lower stability.
- **Pb²⁺** is borderline-hard, binds weakly, and only reaches the bis-complex in aqueous solution.

This ordering (Hg²⁺ ≫ Cd²⁺ > Pb²⁺) explains why sulfur-containing ligands are used in mercury detoxification (e.g., DMSA, DMPS), and why thiocyanate-based colorimetric sensors are highly selective for Hg²⁺ over other heavy metals.
