### 🏆 Winner: Hg²⁺ — by an enormous margin

**Hg²⁺** has the highest affinity for thiocyanate, with log K₁ = **9.64** — more than **7.7 log units** above Cd²⁺ and nearly **9 log units** above Pb²⁺. This is a stunning difference: Hg²⁺ binds SCN⁻ ~50 million times more strongly than Cd²⁺ on a free-energy basis.

### Chemistry Insight

This ranking is a textbook illustration of **HSAB (Hard-Soft Acid-Base) theory**:
- **Hg²⁺** is the *softest* of the three — highly polarizable, with a large, diffuse electron cloud — and SCN⁻ is a *soft base* that coordinates through its sulfur end. The Hg–S bond has significant covalent character, driving the enormous log K.
- **Cd²⁺** is intermediate in softness (borderline), giving a modest but measurable affinity.
- **Pb²⁺** is the *hardest* of the three and shows the weakest interaction with this soft ligand.

The thermodynamic data reinforce this: Hg²⁺ + SCN⁻ has ΔH ≈ −49 to −50 kJ/mol (strongly exothermic), while Cd²⁺ shows only ΔH ≈ −9 to −13 kJ/mol — the Hg–SCN bond is intrinsically much stronger.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
