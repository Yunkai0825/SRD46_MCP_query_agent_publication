**Hg²⁺** has the highest affinity for thiocyanate, with log K₁ ≈ **17.47** — roughly **16 orders of magnitude** stronger than Pb²⁺ (log K₁ ≈ 0.78) and **~16 log units** above Cd²⁺ (log K₁ ≈ 1.40).

### Chemistry Insight

This ranking is a textbook illustration of **Pearson's Hard-Soft Acid-Base (HSAB) theory**. SCN⁻ is a **soft base** that coordinates preferentially through its sulfur atom to soft acids. Among the three metals:

- **Hg²⁺** is the **softest** of the three (highly polarizable, large ionic radius relative to charge, low electronegativity difference), giving it an extraordinary affinity for the soft sulfur donor of SCN⁻. The log K₁ ≈ 17.47 is one of the largest first-step thiocyanate constants in the entire database.
- **Cd²⁺** is a **borderline-to-soft** acid, showing moderate affinity (log K₁ ≈ 1.40).
- **Pb²⁺** is a **borderline** acid with significant hard character, giving the weakest interaction (log K₁ ≈ 0.78).

This same softness ordering (Hg²⁺ ≫ Cd²⁺ > Pb²⁺) is also observed with other soft ligands such as iodide, sulfide, and thiosulfate — and is the chemical basis for mercury's extreme toxicity, as Hg²⁺ avidly displaces essential metals from sulfur-containing biological sites (e.g., cysteine residues in enzymes).
