### Why Proteins Are Excluded by Design

SRD-46 is a **critically evaluated thermodynamic database** for aqueous solution equilibria of small, discrete coordination compounds. Proteins are excluded because:

1. **No single log β is definable** — proteins have multiple, heterogeneous binding sites that cannot be collapsed into one equilibrium constant
2. **Cooperativity & allostery** — binding is not a simple stepwise equilibrium amenable to the database's formalism
3. **Measurement heterogeneity** — protein Kd values come from ITC, fluorescence, and equilibrium dialysis, not the standardized potentiometric titrations used in SRD-46
4. **Macromolecular scope** — SRD-46 is explicitly scoped to small inorganic and organic ligands

---

### Chemistry Insight 🔬

Transferrin binds Fe³⁺ with K ~ 10²⁰ M⁻¹ using **two Tyr (catecholate-like donors), one His (imidazole), one Asp (carboxylate), and one synergistic carbonate**. Every one of these donor-atom types is represented in SRD-46 through small-molecule analogs — catechols, imidazoles, amino acids, and carboxylates. Studying the stability constants of Fe³⁺ with catechol (log β ~ 20 for tris-catecholate complexes) or histidine in SRD-46 gives powerful mechanistic insight into why transferrin achieves such extraordinary iron affinity. In this sense, SRD-46 is an excellent **molecular toolkit** for understanding protein–metal interactions at the individual residue level, even though it does not list the proteins themselves.
