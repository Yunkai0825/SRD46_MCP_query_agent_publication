### 💡 Chemistry Insight

The hydroxamic acid ligands in SRD-46 (acetohydroxamic acid, threonine- and serinehydroxamic acids, glycylglycylglycinehydroxamic acid) are direct **structural units of natural siderophores** like desferrioxamine B — the iron-scavenging molecule produced by bacteria that competes with transferrin for Fe³⁺. The Fe³⁺–catecholate data (Tiron) similarly models the binding mode used by enterobactin, the strongest known Fe³⁺ chelator (log β ~ 49). SRD-46's small-molecule constants thus provide the thermodynamic foundation for understanding *why* transferrin (log K ~ 10²²) can hold iron so tightly — it mimics a tris-catecholate coordination environment.
