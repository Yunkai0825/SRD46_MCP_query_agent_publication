### 📌 Interpretation

**SRD-46 does not include uranium(VI)/UO₂²⁺ as a metal entity**, and therefore no stability constants for uranyl–crown ether complexes are catalogued in this database. The crown ether data in SRD-46 is focused on alkali metals (Na⁺, K⁺, Cs⁺, Rb⁺), alkaline earths (Ca²⁺, Sr²⁺, Ba²⁺, Ra²⁺), and a few transition/heavy metals (Ag⁺, Hg²⁺, Pb²⁺, Tl⁺).

### 🧪 Chemistry Context

From the broader literature (outside SRD-46), uranyl (UO₂²⁺) does form complexes with crown ethers, particularly in non-aqueous or mixed-solvent media. For example:
- **18-Crown-6** has been reported to weakly complex UO₂²⁺ in organic solvents (e.g., acetonitrile, propylene carbonate), with log β₁ values typically in the range of **1–3**, but aqueous stability constants are very limited due to competition from hydration and hydrolysis.
- Crown ethers are more commonly used with uranyl in **solvent extraction** contexts rather than aqueous equilibrium studies.
