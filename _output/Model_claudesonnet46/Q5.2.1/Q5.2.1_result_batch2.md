### Chemistry Context

The uranyl ion (UO₂²⁺) is a linear dioxo cation with a large equatorial coordination belt (~2.4 Å ionic radius in the equatorial plane). 18-Crown-6, with its six oxygen donors arranged in a ring of ~2.6–2.8 Å cavity diameter, can accommodate UO₂²⁺ in the equatorial plane. Crown ether complexation of uranyl is typically studied in non-aqueous or mixed solvents (e.g., methanol, acetonitrile, propylene carbonate), where log β₁ values in the range of ~2–5 have been reported in the literature. The relatively modest stability compared to transition metals reflects the hard-acid character of UO₂²⁺ and the neutral (non-charged) donor nature of crown ether oxygens.
