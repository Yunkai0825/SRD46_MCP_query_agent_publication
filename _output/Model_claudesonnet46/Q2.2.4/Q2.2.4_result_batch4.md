### Chemistry Insights

- **Amino acids** dominate because they are biologically ubiquitous chelators — their α-amino and carboxylate groups form stable 5-membered chelate rings with virtually every metal ion, making them a natural focus of coordination chemistry research.
- **Aliphatic amines** and **pyridines** together reflect the enormous diversity of N-donor ligand chemistry; pyridines are particularly well-studied because their aromatic rigidity and tunable basicity make them ideal for systematic structure–stability studies.
- The prevalence of **aza-macrocycles** (rank 5) highlights the importance of macrocyclic ligands in medical imaging (e.g., Gd-DOTA for MRI) and heavy-metal remediation, where the macrocyclic effect dramatically enhances thermodynamic stability.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 `ligand_card` table, 5,750 total ligands.*
