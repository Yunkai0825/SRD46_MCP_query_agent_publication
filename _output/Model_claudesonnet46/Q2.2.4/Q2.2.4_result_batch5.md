### Chemistry Insights

- **Amino acids** dominate because they are biologically ubiquitous, well-studied chelators with both amine and carboxylate donor groups — making them ideal model ligands for metal-binding studies.
- **Pyridines and aliphatic amines** reflect the enormous interest in N-donor ligands for transition-metal coordination chemistry, from catalysis to bioinorganic modeling.
- **Aza-macrocycles** (e.g., cyclam, cyclen derivatives) are heavily represented due to their importance in medical imaging (MRI contrast agents) and radiopharmaceuticals, where their kinetic inertness is prized.
