### Chemistry Context

Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid) is chemically unstable in aqueous solution — it rapidly hydrolyzes to **salicylate** and acetate. Any real Pu(IV)–aspirin interaction would likely be mediated by the salicylate hydrolysis product, which is a known chelating ligand (phenolate + carboxylate). However, even salicylate–Pu(IV) data are absent from SRD-46. Pu(IV) is also strongly prone to hydrolysis itself, making clean complex formation studies in aqueous media challenging.

> **Conclusion:** The NIST SRD-46 database contains **no stability constant** for Pu(IV) with aspirin or acetylsalicylate. This likely reflects a genuine gap in the published thermodynamic literature rather than a database omission.
