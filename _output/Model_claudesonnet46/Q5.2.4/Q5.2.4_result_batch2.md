### Chemistry Context

The absence of data is not surprising for two reasons:
1. **Aspirin hydrolyzes** readily to salicylate (2-hydroxybenzoate) in aqueous solution, especially at the near-neutral pH and elevated temperatures relevant to Pu(IV) coordination chemistry — so any real experiment would likely measure Pu(IV)–**salicylate** rather than Pu(IV)–aspirin.
2. Pu(IV) is a hard Lewis acid that strongly prefers oxygen donors; salicylate (with both carboxylate and phenolate groups) is a much better-characterized ligand for actinides.

> **Recommendation:** If you are interested in the closest analog, search for **Pu(IV) + salicylic acid** or **Pu(IV) + 2-hydroxybenzoate**, which are more likely to have published stability constants.
