### Chemistry Insights

1. **Aminopolycarboxylates dominate** the top of the affinity ranking for Pb²⁺. EDTA and DTPA are the workhorses because Pb²⁺ (large, soft-borderline ion) forms stable octadentate or hexadentate complexes with these ligands' mixed N/O donor sets.

2. **CDTA outperforms EDTA** for many metals including Pb²⁺ due to the rigid cyclohexane backbone pre-organizing the chelate geometry — but it is not used clinically because of poor bioavailability and toxicity concerns.

3. **Thiol-based agents (DMSA, BAL, DMPS)** exploit Pb²⁺'s soft-metal character (class B / borderline Lewis acid) and are especially valuable when Pb²⁺ has displaced zinc or calcium from sulfhydryl-containing enzymes. DMSA's oral bioavailability makes it the preferred agent for pediatric lead poisoning.

### Recommendation for Protocol Design

For a chelation therapy protocol:
- **Severe/symptomatic poisoning:** CaNa₂EDTA (IV) ± BAL (IM) for encephalopathy
- **Moderate poisoning (BLL 25–70 µg/dL):** Oral DMSA (succimer) — best safety/efficacy balance
- **Research/experimental:** CDTA and DTPA show higher thermodynamic affinity but lack clinical approval for Pb specifically

*Source: NIST SRD-46 Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes Database, metal_125 (Pb²⁺), 470 ligands surveyed.*
