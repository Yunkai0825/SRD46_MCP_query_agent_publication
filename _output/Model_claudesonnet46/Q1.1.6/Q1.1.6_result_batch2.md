### Chemistry Insight

The 297 amino acid ligands in SRD-46 extend far beyond the 20 canonical proteinogenic amino acids. The class encompasses stereoisomers (D/L/allo forms of isoleucine, threonine, etc.), N-alkylated glycines (sarcosine, N-ethylglycine, N-butylglycine), phosphono-amino acids (glyphosate, phosphoserine, phosphothreonine), sulfur-containing variants (cysteine, penicillamine, methionine, homocysteine, cystine), and heterocyclic analogs (histidine derivatives, mimosine, triazole carboxylic acids, tryptophan analogs). The universal carboxylate group (100% of ligands) combined with a donor amine makes these excellent bidentate chelators for virtually all metal ions. Glycine leads the dataset with 428 stability constant measurements — a reflection of its simplicity, biological ubiquity, and role as a benchmark ligand in coordination chemistry research.
