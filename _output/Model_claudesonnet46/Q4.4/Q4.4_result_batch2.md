## Step 3: Prediction Framework — LFER and Coordination Chemistry

### Key Principles for Zn²⁺ Binding

Zn²⁺ is a **borderline Lewis acid** (Irving–Williams series). Its binding affinity follows these rules:

1. **Soft donors win**: –SH (thiolate) >> imidazole-N > –NH₂ > –COO⁻ > –OH
2. **LFER (Brønsted correlation)**: Higher amine pKa → stronger Zn–N bond (log K ∝ pKa of donor)
3. **Chelate effect**: Multidentate ligands forming 5-membered rings are strongly favored
4. **Tridentate > bidentate**: Each additional donor adds ~1–2 log units to β

## Prediction: **L-Lysine** Would Form the Most Stable Zn²⁺ Complex

### Mechanistic Reasoning

```
Structure of Zn²⁺–Lysine tridentate complex:

        COO⁻
         |
    Zn²⁺—NH₂(α)—CH—CH₂—CH₂—CH₂—CH₂—NH₂(ε)
         |___________________________________|
              two 5-membered chelate rings
```

| Factor | Value | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| α-NH₂ pKa | 9.18 | Strong N-donor (comparable to glycine) |
| ε-NH₂ pKa | **10.53** | Exceptionally basic — strongest amine among all 20 amino acids |
| Coordination mode | Tridentate (α-NH₂, ε-NH₂, α-COO⁻) | Two fused 5-membered chelate rings |
| Analogy | Diethylenetriamine (dien) + Zn²⁺: log β₁ ≈ 8.9 | Lysine mimics a dien-like N,N,O donor set |
| Predicted log β₁ | **~7.5–8.0** | Between glycine (~5.2) and cysteine (~9.86) |

The **ε-amino group of lysine** (pKa = 10.53) is the most basic primary amine among all 20 standard amino acids. The Brønsted LFER for Zn²⁺ complexes predicts that each unit increase in amine pKa raises log K by ~0.5–0.7 units. Combined with the **tridentate chelate effect** (two fused 5-membered rings, analogous to diethylenetriamine), lysine's predicted log β₁ of ~7.5–8.0 would place it **second only to cysteine** among amino acid–Zn²⁺ complexes.

### Why Not Methionine or Tryptophan?

- **Methionine**: The thioether sulfur is a much weaker donor than thiolate (cysteine's –S⁻). Thioethers are soft but neutral, contributing only modestly to stability.
- **Tryptophan**: The indole nitrogen has its lone pair tied up in aromaticity (pKa ~17 in free indole), making it a very poor σ-donor to Zn²⁺.
- **Proline**: The secondary amine (pKa ~10.60) cannot form the classic bidentate glycinate chelate because the nitrogen geometry is constrained by the pyrrolidine ring.

## Summary

> **L-Lysine** is predicted to form the most stable Zn²⁺ complex among amino acids not yet thoroughly studied in SRD-46. Its uniquely high ε-amino pKa (10.53), potential tridentate coordination mode, and formation of two fused 5-membered chelate rings (analogous to diethylenetriamine) point to a predicted **log β₁ ≈ 7.5–8.0** — well above simple α-amino acids (~5.0) and approaching cysteine's exceptional value of 9.86 (which benefits from the soft thiolate donor). This prediction is grounded in the linear free-energy relationship between amine basicity and Zn²⁺ affinity, and the well-established chelate effect quantified across hundreds of SRD-46 measurements.
