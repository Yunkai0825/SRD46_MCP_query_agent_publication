### Chemistry Context

Supercritical CO₂ (T > 31.1 °C, P > 73.8 bar) is a fascinating non-polar, tuneable solvent used in metal extraction (e.g., uranium, lanthanides with fluorinated β-diketonate ligands like TTA or HFA), but the equilibrium constants measured in scCO₂ are typically **distribution ratios** or **extraction equilibria** rather than classical stepwise formation constants — which is why they fall outside the scope of SRD-46, a database focused on aqueous solution thermodynamics.

For scCO₂ stability/extraction data, specialized resources such as the **IUPAC Solubility Data Series** or literature from groups like Wai, Laintz, and Darr would be the appropriate starting point.
