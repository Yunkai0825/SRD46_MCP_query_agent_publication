## Electrode Potential of Cu²⁺/Cu⁰

The NIST SRD-46 database specialises in **aqueous metal–ligand stability constants** (formation constants, β values, and pKa data) — it does **not** contain electrode potential / reduction potential data.

For the Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ half-reaction, the standard value comes from well-established electrochemical references:

| Half-reaction | E° (V vs. SHE) | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Cu²⁺ + 2 e⁻ → Cu⁰ | **+0.342 V** | 25 °C, unit activity (standard state) |

### Key points

- **Source:** IUPAC / NIST WebBook / CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics.
- The positive E° means Cu²⁺ is a **moderately good oxidising agent** and copper metal is relatively **noble** (it does not dissolve in non-oxidising acids like HCl under standard conditions).
- For comparison, the Cu⁺/Cu⁰ couple is +0.521 V, and Cu²⁺/Cu⁺ is +0.153 V — consistent with the overall +0.342 V for the two-electron process.

### Where to find authoritative electrode potential data

| Resource | URL |
|---|---|
| NIST WebBook (Thermochemical) | https://webbook.nist.gov |
| NIST SRD-20 (CODATA Key Values) | https://www.nist.gov/srd/nist20 |
| CRC Handbook, Table of Electrochemical Series | Print / online library access |

> **Note:** SRD-46 (this database) is the right tool if you want to know how ligands *shift* the apparent reduction potential of copper via complex formation (using the Nernst equation + stability constants). Feel free to ask about that!
