### Why Cu²⁺?

Cu²⁺ dominates coordination chemistry for several compelling reasons:
1. **Irving–Williams series**: Cu²⁺ sits at the top of the stability series (Mn < Fe < Co < Ni < Cu > Zn), forming the most stable complexes with most ligands — making it the most-studied benchmark ion.
2. **Biological relevance**: Copper is essential in enzymes (ceruloplasmin, superoxide dismutase, cytochrome c oxidase), driving enormous research interest.
3. **Versatile coordination geometry**: Cu²⁺ adopts square-planar, octahedral, and distorted geometries (Jahn–Teller effect), enabling it to bind an extraordinary diversity of ligand types.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Standard Reference Database 46)*
