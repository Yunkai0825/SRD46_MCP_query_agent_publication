### The Relationship

The overall stability constant β₂ is related to the stepwise constants by:

$$\log\beta_2 = \log K_1 + \log K_2$$

where:
- **K₁** = β₁ = [ML]/[M][L] → `beta_def_812`
- **K₂** = stepwise second step: ML + L ⇌ ML₂, so log K₂ = log β₂ − log K₁
- **β₂** = [ML₂]/[M][L]² → `beta_def_840`

### Chemistry Insights

1. **Perfect internal consistency.** The SRD-46 database stores β₁ and β₂ as independent measurements, yet their difference exactly reproduces K₂ at every condition — confirming the thermodynamic self-consistency of the dataset. The relation log β₂ = log K₁ + log K₂ holds to two decimal places across all nine condition sets.

2. **K₂ < K₁ (statistical + steric effect).** At 25 °C, I → 0: log K₁ = 8.56 vs. log K₂ = 7.14, a difference of ~1.4 log units. This is expected: the first glycine binds to a bare Cu²⁺ with four open coordination sites, while the second must displace water from an already-occupied complex. A purely statistical factor of 4 sites → 2 sites predicts ~0.6 log units difference; the extra ~0.8 units reflects steric and electrostatic repulsion between the two glycinate ligands.

3. **Temperature and ionic-strength trends.** log β₂ decreases from 16.30 (10 °C) to 15.30 (40 °C), indicating the complexation is **exothermic** (confirmed by ΔH = −54.4 kJ/mol at 25 °C). At 25 °C, log β₂ shows a shallow minimum near I = 0.5 M and rises again at higher ionic strength, consistent with activity-coefficient effects on the highly charged Cu²⁺ ion.
