## 🏆 Verdict: **Chloride forms a stronger Cu²⁺ complex than bromide**

At every comparable ionic strength and at 25 °C, **CuCl⁺ has a consistently higher log K₁ than CuBr⁺** — by roughly **+0.24 to +0.44 log units** (a factor of ~1.7–2.8× in K). At infinite dilution (I → 0), log K₁(CuCl⁺) ≈ **+0.20** vs log K₁(CuBr⁺) ≈ **−0.04**.

### Chemistry Insight

This result is somewhat counterintuitive from a simple "soft acid–soft base" perspective — one might expect Cu²⁺ (a borderline metal) to prefer the larger, more polarizable Br⁻ over Cl⁻. However, Cu²⁺ in aqueous solution behaves more like a **hard acid** in its halide interactions, and the stronger hydration of Br⁻ (relative to its weaker Cu²⁺ binding) tips the balance toward Cl⁻. Both complexes are **weak** (log K₁ < 1), reflecting that Cu²⁺ strongly prefers N- and O-donor ligands (e.g., amines, amino acids) over halides in aqueous solution. The positive ΔH values (8–12 kJ/mol for Cl⁻; 12.6 kJ/mol for Br⁻) confirm these are **entropically driven** associations, not enthalpically favorable bonds.
