## Cu²⁺ Halide Complex Stability: Chloride vs Bromide

Here is a complete comparison of log K₁ values for the 1:1 complex [CuX]⁺ (reaction: Cu²⁺ + X⁻ ⇌ CuX⁺), all at **25 °C**, aqueous solution.

### log K₁ Values: Cu²⁺ + Cl⁻ vs Cu²⁺ + Br⁻

| Ionic Strength (M) | log K₁ (Cu²⁺–Cl⁻) | log K₁ (Cu²⁺–Br⁻) | Δ (Cl − Br) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ~0 (extrapolated) | **+0.20** | **−0.04** | +0.24 |
| 1.0 | −0.20 | −0.64 | +0.44 |
| 2.0 | −0.10 | −0.49 | +0.39 |
| 3.0 | 0.00 | −0.40 | +0.40 |
| 4.0 | +0.11 | −0.22 | +0.33 |

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (beta_def_812: [ML]/[M][L])*

---

### 🏆 Winner: **Chloride (Cl⁻)** forms a stronger Cu²⁺ complex

At every ionic strength measured, **Cu²⁺–Cl⁻ has a higher log K₁ than Cu²⁺–Br⁻** by approximately 0.3–0.4 log units. At infinite dilution (I → 0), the values are:
- **Cu²⁺ + Cl⁻:** log K₁ = **+0.20**
- **Cu²⁺ + Br⁻:** log K₁ = **−0.04**

---

### Chemistry Insight

This result is consistent with **Hard-Soft Acid-Base (HSAB) theory** applied carefully: Cu²⁺ is a **borderline acid**, and while it does show some affinity for softer donors, the **smaller, more charge-dense Cl⁻** forms a slightly stronger electrostatic interaction than the larger, more polarizable Br⁻. Both constants are very small (log K₁ near 0), confirming that Cu²⁺ forms only **weak complexes with halides** in general — far weaker than with nitrogen or oxygen donors like ammonia (log β₄ ≈ 12) or EDTA (log β ≈ 18.8).

Notably, the **thermodynamic data** (ΔH, ΔS) at I = 3 M show both reactions are **endothermic** (ΔH > 0) and **entropy-driven** (ΔS > 0), suggesting the complexation is favored by release of water molecules from the coordination sphere — a classic signature of outer-sphere or weak inner-sphere halide binding.
