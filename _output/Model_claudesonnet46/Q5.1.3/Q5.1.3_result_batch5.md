## 5. Key Chemistry Insights

1. **Hard vs. Borderline:** Fe³⁺ is a classic **hard acid** (HSAB), strongly preferring oxygen donors (catecholates, hydroxamates, carboxylates) — hence siderophores exploit catechol/hydroxamate groups to sequester iron with extraordinary affinity (log β > 40 for enterobactin). Cu²⁺ is **borderline**, binding nitrogen donors (amines, imidazoles, pyridines) almost as well as oxygen donors, explaining its prevalence in blue copper proteins.

2. **Hydrolysis dominance:** Fe³⁺ hydrolyzes so readily that most stability constants in SRD-46 are measured at low pH or high ionic strength to suppress competing OH⁻ reactions — note the M₇(OH)₉L₆ species for Fe³⁺! Cu²⁺ hydrolysis is milder and more tractable.

3. **Data richness reflects research focus:** Cu²⁺'s ~5× larger ligand partner count in SRD-46 reflects decades of intensive study in analytical chemistry (Cu²⁺ as a spectrophotometric probe), bioinorganic chemistry (copper enzymes), and materials science (MOFs, catalysis).
