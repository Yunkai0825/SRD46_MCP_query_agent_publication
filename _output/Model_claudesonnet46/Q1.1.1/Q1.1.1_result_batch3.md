### Chemistry Insights

1. **Stepwise binding:** The stepwise constant log K₂ = log β₂ − log β₁ ≈ 6.5–7.5, consistent with a second glycinate chelating in the equatorial plane of the square-planar Cu²⁺ complex. The statistical factor (log 4/2 ≈ 0.3) accounts for only a small part of this difference.

2. **Enthalpy–entropy compensation:** Both complexes form with negative ΔH (enthalpically favourable N,O-chelation) and large positive ΔS (release of water molecules from the Cu²⁺ coordination sphere), making glycine one of the most thermodynamically stable amino acid chelators of Cu²⁺.

3. **Ionic-strength effect:** log β₁ drops from 8.56 (I = 0) to ~8.10 (I = 0.5 M) then rises slightly at higher ionic strengths — a classic Debye–Hückel / specific-ion-interaction pattern for a 2+/1− reaction.

4. **Physiological relevance:** At 37 °C and I = 0.15 M (blood plasma conditions), log β₁ = 7.99 and log β₂ = 14.69, indicating that Cu²⁺ is very strongly bound by glycine under physiological conditions — relevant to amino acid transport and copper speciation in biological fluids.
