### Key Chemistry Insights

1. **Polyaza macrocycles dominate the top spots** — [33]aneN11 and [36]aneN12 each have **11 pKa values**, reflecting one protonation step per nitrogen atom in their large ring systems. The more nitrogen donors in the ring, the more stepwise protonation equilibria are measurable.

2. **EDTA-family oligomers scale predictably** — PHOA (8 carboxylates + 6 amines) > TPHA > TTHA > DTPA, with pKa count tracking the number of ionizable groups. This makes them powerful chelators for multivalent metals in medicine (e.g., DTPA in MRI contrast agents) and industrial water treatment.

3. **Phytic acid (myo-inositol hexaphosphate)** is a notable outlier — a natural product found in seeds with 8 reported pKa values from its six phosphate groups, making it a potent sequestrant of nutritionally important metals like Fe²⁺, Zn²⁺, and Ca²⁺.
