Using the **25 °C, I = 0** SRD-46 data for **Cu²⁺ + NH₃**, the cumulative constants are:

| Species | log β |
|---|---:|
| Cu(NH₃)²⁺ | 4.02 |
| Cu(NH₃)₂²⁺ | 7.4 |
| Cu(NH₃)₃²⁺ | 10.2 |
| Cu(NH₃)₄²⁺ | 12.3 |

From these, the **stepwise** constants are:

| Step | Relation | log K |
|---|---|---:|
| K₁ | log β₁ | 4.02 |
| K₂ | log β₂ − log β₁ | 3.38 |
| K₃ | log β₃ − log β₂ | 2.8 |
| K₄ | log β₄ − log β₃ | 2.1 |

So yes, **K₁ > K₂ > K₃ > K₄**: they **decrease monotonically**.

That pattern is the statistical/steric trend you’d normally expect as successive NH₃ ligands bind. For **Cu(II)**, the fact that the first four ammines are all strongly favored and define a stable **ML₄** set is consistent with a **preferred 4-coordinate equatorial environment**, commonly described as **square-planar or square-planar-like within a Jahn–Teller-distorted octahedral Cu(II)** complex. In other words, the first four NH₃ ligands occupy a particularly favorable basal plane; any additional axial coordination is typically weaker.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46, Cu²⁺–ammonia stability data at 25 °C.
