SRD-46 lists **2 acid dissociation steps** for **oxalic acid (ethanedioic acid, ligand_8872)**, consistent with **HxL = H2L**.

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | T (°C) | I (M) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | H2L ⇌ HL + H+ | -1.20 | 25.0 | 0.1 |
| 2 | HL ⇌ L + H+ | 3.80 | 25.0 | 0.1 |

So, **oxalic acid is diprotic** and has **two dissociation steps**.

A small chemistry note: in this SRD-46 record, the first listed protonation/deprotonation step is unusually strong (**pKa = -1.2**), while the second is much weaker (**pKa = 3.8**), reflecting the large effect of charge buildup after the first deprotonation.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46.
