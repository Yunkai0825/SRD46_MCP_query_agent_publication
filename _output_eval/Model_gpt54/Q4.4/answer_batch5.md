Using the SRD-46 amino-acid data, my best prediction is **histidine** as the **most likely unstudied canonical amino acid to form the most stable Zn²⁺ complex**.

| Basis | Evidence from SRD-46 | Implication for Zn²⁺ |
|---|---|---|
| Strongest studied Zn amino acids | Studied Zn²⁺ amino-acid trend in SRD-46: **cysteine ≫ histidine > aspartate/glutamate ≳ amide/extra-N or thioether donors > simple α-amino acids** | Zn²⁺ stability is strongly boosted by side-chain donors beyond the usual amino/carboxylate set |
| Histidine donor availability | Histidine pKa set includes **6.05** for the imidazole-relevant protonation | Near neutral pH, imidazole is substantially unprotonated and ready to bind Zn²⁺ |
| Cysteine donor availability | Cysteine pKa set includes **8.18** for the thiol/thiolate-relevant protonation | Thiolate is very strong for Zn²⁺, but its availability is more pH-dependent |
| Other amino acids | Asp/Glu have extra carboxylates; Lys/Arg are mostly protonated at neutral pH; Ser/Thr/Tyr OH donors are weak unless deprotonated; simple aliphatic amino acids resemble glycine/alanine | These are generally less favorable than an accessible imidazole donor for Zn²⁺ |

### Why histidine is the safest prediction
| Reason | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **Zn²⁺ likes N donors**, especially biologically relevant imidazole-type donors | Histidine provides a classic Zn-binding side chain in addition to the α-amino acid binding set |
| **Its side-chain pKa is well matched to neutral solution** | Unlike Lys or Arg, histidine is not trapped in a strongly protonated, nonbinding form over the usual measurement range |
| **SRD-46 comparator data support it** | Among amino acids already studied with Zn²⁺, histidine is in the top tier, clearly above simple N,O-only amino acids |

### Important caveat
If the question allows **pH values high enough to generate appreciable thiolate**, then **cysteine could surpass histidine** in intrinsic Zn²⁺ affinity. SRD-46 already shows cysteine as the strongest *studied* amino-acid Zn²⁺ ligand, consistent with Zn²⁺’s strong response to soft sulfur donors.

### Bottom line
| Scenario | Best prediction |
|---|---|
| **Canonical amino acids, general aqueous conditions** | **Histidine** |
| **If alkaline conditions favor thiolate formation** | **Cysteine would be the stronger binder, but it is already studied** |

So, **for a not-yet-studied canonical amino acid, histidine is the best data-grounded prediction**.
