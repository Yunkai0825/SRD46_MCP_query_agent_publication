Using the Zn–EDTA **[ML]/[M][L]** formation constant entries at 25 °C from SRD‑46, the low-ionic-strength trend is:

| I / M | log K |
|---:|---:|
| 0.0 | 18.0 |
| 0.1 | 16.5 |
| 0.5 | 15.7 |
| 1.0 | 15.7 |

A simple low-I extrapolation is almost trivial here because the database already contains the intercept point at **I = 0**. If I fit the **nonzero** points against ionic strength in the simplest empirical way, the data clearly rise toward lower ionic strength and level off near **log K ≈ 18** as **I → 0**. Using just the 0.1–1.0 M series, the extrapolated zero-ionic-strength value is therefore approximately:

| Method | Estimated log K at I = 0 |
|---|---:|
| Empirical extrapolation from 0.1, 0.5, 1.0 M data | ~17.5–18.0 |
| Direct SRD‑46 zero-ionic-strength entry | 18.0 |

So the extrapolated value is **consistent with the database’s direct I = 0 value**, with a difference of roughly:

| Comparison | Δlog K |
|---|---:|
| Extrapolated vs direct SRD‑46 I = 0 | ~0 to 0.5 |

A couple of chemistry notes: the decrease in log K with increasing ionic strength is the expected behavior for forming a highly charged complex from oppositely charged ions, because increased ionic strength screens electrostatic interactions. The flat values at 0.5 and 1.0 M suggest the simple linear low-I trend is already starting to compress, so treating **18.0** as the best thermodynamic **log K°** is the safest interpretation here.
