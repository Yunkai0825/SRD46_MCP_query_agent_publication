SRD-46 has **two Ag⁺–ammonia aqueous complex formation constants** available, corresponding to **Ag(NH3)+** and **Ag(NH3)2+**. The database reports them as **logK** for the reactions shown below, with measurements at **25 °C** over a range of ionic strengths and media.

| Reaction | Species | Constant type | No. of measurements | logK range | Temperature range | Ionic strength range |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| [M] + [L] <=> [ML] | Ag(NH3)+ | logK | 13 | 3.2–3.8 | 25 °C | 0–5 M |
| [M] + [L]^2 <=> [ML2] | Ag(NH3)2+ | logK | 16 | 7.14–8.18 | 25 °C | 0–5 M |

SRD-46 also contains associated **thermodynamic data** for the same two complexes:

| Reaction | Constant type | No. of measurements | Value range | Temperature | Ionic strength range |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| [M] + [L] <=> [ML] | ΔH | 5 | -25.9 to -20.5 | 25 °C | 0–3 M |
| [M] + [L] <=> [ML] | ΔS | 4 | -12.1 to -5.4 | 25 °C | 0–3 M |
| [M] + [L]^2 <=> [ML2] | ΔH | 5 | -61.9 to -56.1 | 25 °C | 0–3 M |
| [M] + [L]^2 <=> [ML2] | ΔS | 4 | -55.6 to -46.4 | 25 °C | 0–3 M |

A couple of chemistry notes: the much larger constant for **Ag(NH3)2+** is consistent with silver(I)’s strong affinity for soft donor ligands like ammonia and its common **linear two-coordinate** ammine complex. The spread in reported logK values likely reflects **ionic strength and medium effects**, so individual measurements should be compared only at matched conditions.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46, Ag⁺ (`metal_2`) with ammonia (`ligand_10103`), beta definitions `beta_def_812` and `beta_def_840`.
