Yes — **EDTA forms the more stable Fe³⁺ complex**.

| Ligand | Fe³⁺ complex | log K | T (°C) | Ionic strength (M) | Definition |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| EDTA | FeEDTA | 25.1 | 25 | 0.1 | [ML]/[M][L] |
| NTA | FeNTA | 15.9 | 25 | 0.1 | [ML]/[M][L] |

At the same reported conditions (**25 °C, I = 0.1 M**), EDTA is higher by **9.2 log units**, so Fe³⁺ binds **much more strongly** to EDTA than to NTA.

For reference, SRD-46 also lists higher-ionic-strength values:

| Ligand | log K | T (°C) | Ionic strength (M) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| EDTA | 23.8 | 25 | 1.0 |
| NTA | 14.78 | 25 | 1.0 |

The trend is unchanged: **EDTA > NTA** for Fe³⁺ complex stability.

*Source: NIST SRD-46, Fe³⁺ + ligand stability data for beta_def_812, `[M] + [L] <=> [ML]`.*
