I checked SRD-46’s copper records and execution-level data, and **SRD-46 does not provide electrode potentials for Cu²⁺/Cu⁰**; it contains **stability/thermodynamic measurements for complexes** instead.

| SRD-46 check | Result |
|---|---|
| Copper(II) entry | `metal_41` = Cu²⁺ |
| Citation search for electrode-potential/redox terms | No results found |
| Stability data for Cu²⁺ | Present, but these are complexation/thermodynamic measurements, not E° values |

So, **from SRD-46 itself, no Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ electrode potential is available**.

For the **standard reduction potential** of the half-reaction

| Half-reaction | E° vs SHE |
|---|---:|
| Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu(s) | **+0.34 V** |

That **+0.34 V vs SHE** is the commonly accepted standard value, but it is **not retrieved from SRD-46**.

A chemistry note: the positive value means Cu²⁺ is thermodynamically favorable to reduce relative to H⁺/H₂ under standard conditions, which is one reason metallic copper is comparatively resistant to oxidation in many environments.
