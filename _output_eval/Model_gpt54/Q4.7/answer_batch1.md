Using SRD-46, the overlap between **Co²⁺** and **Co³⁺** is small, but there are clear shared amine ligands. The strongest clean comparisons I could retrieve are **ammonia** and **ethylenediamine (en)**; SRD-46 also shows broader Co²⁺ coverage for higher polyamines, while Co³⁺ coverage is much sparser.

## Interpretation with crystal-field theory

| Ion | d-count | Likely spin state with amines | Crystal-field consequence | Expected effect on stability |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Co²⁺ | d7 | usually high spin | modest LFSE in octahedral amine complexes | moderate stability, more labile |
| Co³⁺ | d6 | usually low spin | very large octahedral LFSE with N donors | much higher stability, often very inert |

The chemistry fits textbook crystal-field expectations:

| Factor | Co²⁺ | Co³⁺ |
|---|---|---|
| Charge density | lower | much higher |
| Metal–N bond strength | weaker | stronger |
| LFSE with octahedral amines | modest | large |
| Ligand substitution kinetics | relatively labile | often inert |
| Overall amine complex stability | moderate | very high |

## Prediction for a **new polydentate amine ligand**

If you introduce a **new polydentate amine ligand**—especially a tri-, tetra-, or hexadentate N-donor chelator—the likely behavior is:

| Metal ion | Predicted behavior |
|---|---|
| Co²⁺ | It should form detectable and probably moderately strong complexes, with chelation increasing stability over ammonia or simple diamines. However, complexes will likely remain comparatively labile and less thermodynamically favored than the Co³⁺ analogues. |
| Co³⁺ | It should bind **much more strongly** than Co²⁺, likely by many log units for a well-matched multidentate amine. If the geometry supports octahedral coordination, the Co³⁺ complex should be especially stable and often kinetically inert once formed. |

## Bottom-line prediction

| Scenario | Expected outcome |
|---|---|
| New open-chain polydentate amine + Co²⁺ | moderate-to-strong complexation |
| New open-chain polydentate amine + Co³⁺ | very strong complexation, likely far larger logβ |
| New preorganized or macrocyclic amine + Co²⁺ | stronger than simple amines, but still limited relative to Co³⁺ |
| New preorganized or macrocyclic amine + Co³⁺ | exceptionally stable complex, potentially extremely inert |

So the best prediction is: **a new polydentate amine ligand should favor Co³⁺ very strongly over Co²⁺**, with the difference expected to be large because **higher charge + low-spin d6 crystal-field stabilization + chelate effect** all act in the same direction.
