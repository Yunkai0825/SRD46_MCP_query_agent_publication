SRD-46 supports the hypothesis that **Hg²⁺ is strongly sulfur-loving (thiophilic)**: overall, **S-donor ligands > N-donor ligands > simple O-donor ligands**.

## Hypothesis

| Preference rank | Donor type | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sulfur donor** | Thiourea, thiocyanate, and thiodiethanol all give very large Hg²⁺ constants, with **HgL ≈ 8.8–11.9** and higher cumulative values up to **32.5** |
| 2 | **Nitrogen donor** | N-donors bind, but most are weaker: **ammonia 2.3**, **pyridine 2.6**, **imidazole 6.9**; chelating en is stronger at **9.1** |
| 3 | **Simple oxygen donor** | Simple O-donors such as carbonate are much weaker, e.g. **HgL = 3.2** |

## Conclusion

The SRD-46 numbers are most consistent with **Hg²⁺ preferring sulfur-donor ligands**. Nitrogen donors can bind moderately well, especially if they chelate, but simple oxygen donors are generally weaker. That pattern matches classic **HSAB** behavior: **Hg²⁺ is a soft acid**, so it binds best to **soft sulfur bases**.
