Hg²⁺ in SRD-46 appears to prefer **sulfur-donor ligands most strongly**, then **nitrogen-donor ligands**, with **oxygen-donor ligands generally weaker**:

**Hypothesis:** **S ≫ N > O** for Hg²⁺.

## Representative Hg²⁺ stability constants from SRD-46

These are the database-supported ranges or maxima retrieved for representative ligands/classes.

| Donor class | Ligand | Reported log K values in SRD-46 | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| S-donor | Thiourea (thiocarbamide) | **11.4–36** | Strongest values among the compared classes |
| N-donor | Ethylenediamine | **5.8–23.19** | Strong N-donor, boosted by chelation |
| N-donor | Ammonia | **8.8–19** | Stronger than simple O-donors, below top S-donor values |
| N-donor | Imidazole | **−1.54–18.19** | Broad range depending on conditions/speciation |
| N-donor | Pyridine | **5.2–10.6** | Weaker than en and ammonia |
| O-donor | Carbonate / hydrogen carbonate system | **−11.8 to 14.5** | Includes very weak/simple HgL behavior; more variable |
| O-/N-mixed chelator | EDTA | **3.2–21.5** | Strong, but not a pure O-donor comparison because it is multidentate and mixed-donor |

## What this suggests

| Rank | Donor preference | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Sulfur-donor** | Thiourea reaches **log K up to 36**, clearly above the rest |
| 2 | **Nitrogen-donor** | en reaches **23.19**, ammonia **19**, imidazole **18.19**, pyridine **10.6** |
| 3 | **Oxygen-donor** | Carbonate/hydrogen carbonate is much more variable and can be very weak, down to **−11.8** |

## Interpretation

The clearest signal is that **Hg²⁺ binds sulfur donors much more strongly than the representative N- or O-donor ligands**. Nitrogen donors can still bind well, especially chelating ones like **ethylenediamine**, but they do not generally match the top sulfur-donor values. Oxygen-donor systems are weaker and more variable, and in the Hg²⁺ case often compete with hydrolysis/speciation effects.

That trend is also chemically sensible: **Hg²⁺ is a soft Lewis acid**, so it should favor **soft sulfur bases** over harder oxygen donors.

## Caveat

| Point | Comment |
|---|---|
| Comparability | These values come from different ligand systems and not all are at identical ionic strength/medium |
| EDTA | High stability does **not** make Hg²⁺ an O-donor-preferring ion; EDTA is a special case because it is multidentate and mixed donor |
| Species effects | Some reported ranges reflect multiple species/conditions, not a single uniform experiment |

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 stability/equilibrium records for **Hg²⁺ (metal_71)**.
