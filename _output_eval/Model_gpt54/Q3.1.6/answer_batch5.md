Using SRD-46 data for La³⁺ 1:1 complexes with carboxylate-type ligands, the strongest ligands I could verify are aminopolycarboxylates. The top three from the retrieved La–ligand set are:

| Ligand | SRD-46 ligand ID | Reported La³⁺ complex | log K / log β | Conditions from retrieved records |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| DTPA (diethylenetrinitrilopentaacetic acid) | ligand_6356 | [ML]/[M][L] | **19.49** | 25 °C, I = 0.1 M |

| EDTA (ethylenedinitrilotetraacetic acid) | ligand_6277 | [ML]/[M][L] | **14.48–15.36** | 25 °C, I = 0.1–0.5 M |

For a weaker comparison point:

| Ligand | SRD-46 ligand ID | Reported La³⁺ complex | log K / log β | Conditions from retrieved records |
|---|---|---|---:|---|
| Malonic acid | ligand_8873 | [ML]/[M][L] | **3.06–4.94** | 25 °C, I = 0–1 M |

So, among the carboxylic-acid ligands found here, **DTPA > EGTA > EDTA** for La³⁺ complex stability.

A quick chemistry note: this trend makes sense because La³⁺ strongly favors **multidentate oxygen-rich chelators**, and DTPA offers more donor atoms and higher chelate preorganization than EDTA or simple dicarboxylates like malonate.

**Source:** NIST SRD-46 (Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).
