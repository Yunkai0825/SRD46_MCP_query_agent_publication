So, **not dramatically different overall**. The **carboxyl pKa is identical** in the matched SRD-46 entries, while the **amino-group pKa differs by 0.14 units**, which is small but a bit larger than typical experimental uncertainty often cited for good pKa measurements (~0.05–0.10). Chemically, that makes sense: the methyl group in alanine is slightly electron-donating relative to glycine, which can slightly increase the basicity of the amino group.
