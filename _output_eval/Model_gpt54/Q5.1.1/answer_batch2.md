## Hg(II): the dominant complex-forming state
The Hg(II) catalog includes halides, pseudohalides, hydroxide, sulfide-related ligands, neutral N-donors, soft S-donors, and chelators.

Representative Hg(II) ligand families seen in SRD‑46:

| Ligand class | Examples present in SRD‑46 |
|---|---|

| N-donors | NH₃, ethylenediamine, pyridine, imidazole |

## What stands out chemically
A few safe takeaways from the SRD‑46 coverage:

| Observation | Evidence from SRD‑46 |
|---|---|
| Hg(II) chemistry is much richer than Hg(I) | 20 Hg(II) systems and 92 species in the catalog vs a much smaller Hg(I) set |
| Soft-donor affinity is prominent for Hg(II) | strong coverage for thiourea, thiocyanate, cyanide, iodide, bromide, chloride |
| Hydrolysis matters | hydroxide is one of the most represented Hg(II) systems |
| Chelation is important but less dominant in coverage than soft monodentates | EDTA and ethylenediamine are present, but halides / hydroxide / thiourea have more entries |

## Short chemistry interpretation
Mercury follows classic **HSAB** behavior: **Hg(II)** strongly favors **soft donor atoms**, especially **S** and heavy halides, so it makes sense that thiourea, thiocyanate, iodide, bromide, and cyanide are all well represented. The appreciable hydroxide coverage also reflects that **aqueous speciation and hydrolysis** are important for mercury, especially outside strongly complexing media.
