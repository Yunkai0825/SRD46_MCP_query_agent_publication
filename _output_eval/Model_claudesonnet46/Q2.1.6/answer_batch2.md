### Chemistry Insight

This ranking is a textbook illustration of **Pearson's Hard-Soft Acid-Base (HSAB) theory**. SCN‚ĀĽ is a **soft base** that coordinates preferentially through its sulfur atom to soft acids. Among the three metals:

- **Hg¬≤‚Āļ** is the **softest** of the three (highly polarizable, large ionic radius relative to charge, low electronegativity difference), giving it an extraordinary affinity for the soft sulfur donor of SCN‚ĀĽ. The log K‚āĀ ‚Čą 17.47 is one of the largest first-step thiocyanate constants in the entire database.
- **Cd¬≤‚Āļ** is a **borderline-to-soft** acid, showing moderate affinity (log K‚āĀ ‚Čą 1.40).
- **Pb¬≤‚Āļ** is a **borderline** acid with significant hard character, giving the weakest interaction (log K‚āĀ ‚Čą 0.78).

This same softness ordering (Hg¬≤‚Āļ ‚Čę Cd¬≤‚Āļ > Pb¬≤‚Āļ) is also observed with other soft ligands such as iodide, sulfide, and thiosulfate ‚ÄĒ and is the chemical basis for mercury's extreme toxicity, as Hg¬≤‚Āļ avidly displaces essential metals from sulfur-containing biological sites (e.g., cysteine residues in enzymes).
