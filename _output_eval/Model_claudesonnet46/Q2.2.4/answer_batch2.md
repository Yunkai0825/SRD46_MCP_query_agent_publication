### Chemistry Insights

- **Amino acids** dominate the classified ligands, reflecting their biological importance and the extensive study of metal–amino acid complexes (e.g., Cu²⁺–glycine, Zn²⁺–histidine) relevant to biochemistry and medicine.
- **Pyridines and aliphatic amines** are nearly tied, underscoring the central role of nitrogen-donor ligands in coordination chemistry — both are excellent σ-donors and form stable complexes with transition metals.
- **Aza-macrocycles** (e.g., cyclam, cyclen derivatives) rank 5th despite being a structurally specialized class, a testament to their exceptional thermodynamic and kinetic stability that makes them prized in medical imaging (MRI contrast agents) and radiopharmaceuticals.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes*
