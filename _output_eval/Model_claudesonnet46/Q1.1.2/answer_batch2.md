## Chemistry Insight

Citric acid's three pKa values are relatively closely spaced (~2.9, ~4.35, ~5.67), which is characteristic of polycarboxylic acids where the central tertiary carbon bears both a hydroxyl group and three carboxylate arms. The hydroxyl group's inductive electron withdrawal stabilises the carboxylate anions and compresses the pKa spacing compared to a simple tricarboxylic acid. The tiny difference between NaClO₄ and KNO₃ media for pKa₃ (5.65 vs. 5.69) reflects subtle ion-pairing effects — perchlorate is a weaker ion-pairer than nitrate. This broad buffering range (pH 2–6) underpins citric acid's central role in the Krebs cycle and its widespread use as a food and pharmaceutical acidulant.
