## Citric Acid pKa Values (SRD-46)

Citric acid is a **triprotic acid (H₃L)** with three successive deprotonation steps:

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | Temperature (°C) | Ionic Strength (M) |
|------|-------------|-----|-------------------|--------------------|
| pKa₁ | H₃L ⇌ H₂L⁻ + H⁺ | **2.90** | 25 | 0.1 |
| pKa₂ | H₂L⁻ ⇌ HL²⁻ + H⁺ | **4.35** | 25 | 0.1 |
| pKa₃ | HL²⁻ ⇌ L³⁻ + H⁺ | **5.65** | 25 | 0.1 |

> All values measured at **25 °C**, ionic strength **I = 0.1 M** (aqueous solution).  
> Source: NIST SRD-46 (VLM IDs: vlm_157473, vlm_157459, vlm_157439)

---

### Chemistry Insight

The three pKa values of citric acid are relatively close together (2.90, 4.35, 5.65), reflecting the three carboxylate groups on the molecule — the hydroxyl group on the central carbon slightly modulates their acidity through inductive effects. The low pKa₁ (~2.90) makes citric acid a moderately strong organic acid, which is why it is so effective as a food acidulant and buffering agent in the pH range 2–6. The fully deprotonated citrate (L³⁻) is a powerful chelating agent for metal ions (as evidenced by the 19 metal systems in SRD-46 that include Ni²⁺, Cu²⁺, Fe³⁺, Al³⁺, and even NpO₂⁺), making it widely used in industrial metal cleaning, pharmaceuticals, and biochemistry.
