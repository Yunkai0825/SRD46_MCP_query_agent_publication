### Why This Gap Exists — Chemistry Context

This absence is chemically understandable for several reasons:

1. **Non-aqueous preference:** Uranyl–crown ether complexes (e.g., UO₂²⁺ + 18-crown-6) are predominantly studied in **organic solvents** (acetonitrile, propylene carbonate) or in **liquid–liquid extraction** systems, not in aqueous solution. SRD-46 focuses on aqueous equilibria.

2. **Geometry mismatch:** The linear O=U=O oxo groups of uranyl occupy axial positions, leaving the equatorial belt for ligand coordination. Crown ethers bind equatorially, but the large ionic radius of UO₂²⁺ and its high charge density make aqueous crown complexes thermodynamically marginal compared to competing hydration.

3. **Literature scope:** Quantitative log β values for UO₂²⁺–crown systems in water are sparse in the primary literature, and SRD-46 may not have curated them.

### Recommendation

For uranyl–crown ether stability constants, consult:
- **IUPAC SC-Database** or **NIST SRD-46 companion volumes** for non-aqueous data
- Primary literature: Moyer et al. and Lumetta et al. on crown ether extraction of actinyl ions
- The **OECD NEA Thermochemical Database** for uranium speciation in aqueous systems
