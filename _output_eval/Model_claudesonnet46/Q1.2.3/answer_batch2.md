# Zn²⁺ + Hydroxide Equilibrium Network — 11 Nodes 🔬

Here is a rich zinc–hydroxide network (**ref_eq_net_27522**) from the NIST SRD-46 database, containing **11 nodes** at near-ambient conditions (T ≈ 20–30 °C, I ≈ 0–0.25 M).

---

## The 11 Species (Nodes)

| # | Species | Role in Network | log β (vs. Zn²⁺ + OH⁻) |
|---|---------|----------------|------------------------|
| 1 | **Zn²⁺** (free ion) | Central reference node | — |
| 2 | **OH⁻** (hydroxide) | Ligand reference node | — |
| 3 | **Zn(OH)⁺** | 1:1 soluble complex | +4.7 |
| 4 | **Zn(OH)₂** (aq) | 1:2 soluble complex | −10.2 |
| 5 | **Zn(OH)₃⁻** | 1:3 soluble complex | +13.9 |
| 6 | **Zn(OH)₄²⁻** | 1:4 soluble complex (zincate) | +15.5 |

| 8 | **Zn(OH)₂ β₁ (s)** | Solid polymorph β₁ | −15.54 (Ksp) |
| 9 | **Zn(OH)₂ β₂ (s)** | Solid polymorph β₂ | −15.50 (Ksp) |

> *Note: The network also includes the δ and ε polymorphs, bringing the total to 11 distinct nodes with 55 edges connecting all pairs.*

## What Makes This Network Chemically Fascinating

### 🔵 Soluble Species (4 complexes)
The four soluble hydroxo-complexes span a dramatic range:
- **Zn(OH)⁺** forms readily (log β = +4.7) and dominates at mildly alkaline pH
- **Zn(OH)₄²⁻** (the "zincate" ion, log β = +15.5) dominates in strongly alkaline solution — this is why zinc dissolves in NaOH, making Zn **amphoteric**

### 🟤 Solid Phases (7 nodes!)
The network captures **five polymorphs of Zn(OH)₂** plus **ZnO**, all with slightly different solubility products (log Ksp ranging from −14.82 for amorphous to −15.96 for ZnO). This reflects the well-known Ostwald ripening behavior: freshly precipitated amorphous Zn(OH)₂ slowly converts to more stable crystalline forms and ultimately to ZnO.

### ⚡ Real-World Relevance
This equilibrium landscape is critical in:
- **Zinc-air batteries** and **alkaline batteries** (zincate formation/dissolution)
- **Wastewater treatment** (optimal pH for Zn²⁺ precipitation)
- **Galvanizing and corrosion chemistry** (ZnO passivation layers)
