### Key Notes

- **Only two species** are reported: the mono-ammine [Ag(NH₃)]⁺ and the di-ammine [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺. Higher complexes (tri-, tetra-) are not present in SRD-46.
- All measurements are at **25 °C**; ionic strength spans 0–5 M across 16 different reference equilibrium networks (various media such as KNO₃, NaClO₄).
- The constants are defined for **free NH₃** (the unprotonated form, pKₐ = 9.26), not total ammonia — important when working at lower pH.
- The spread in log β values (e.g., β₂ from 7.14 to 8.18) reflects ionic-strength dependence: higher ionic strength generally lowers the apparent constant.

### Chemistry Insight

The strong preference for the **linear di-ammine complex** [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ (log β₂ ≈ 7.5–8) is characteristic of d¹⁰ Ag⁺, which favors 2-coordinate linear geometry. This is the basis of **Tollens' reagent** ([Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ in base), widely used to detect aldehydes. The negative ΔS values indicate an entropy penalty from ordering ammonia ligands around the metal, but the enthalpic gain (ΔH ≈ −58 kJ/mol for β₂) drives complex formation.
