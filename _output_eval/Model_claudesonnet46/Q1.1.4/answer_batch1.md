### Chemistry Insights

- **Only two ammine complexes form**: Unlike Cu²⁺ or Ni²⁺ which form up to 4–6 ammine complexes, Ag⁺ (d¹⁰, linear coordination preference) stops at the diamine [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ — the classic linear two-coordinate complex responsible for dissolving AgCl in ammonia solution (Tollens' reagent).

- **The spread in log β values** (e.g., log β₂ = 7.14–8.18) reflects genuine ionic-strength dependence: constants measured at high ionic strength (I up to 5 M) are systematically lower than those at infinite dilution (I → 0), consistent with activity coefficient effects on charged species.

- **Negative ΔS for β₂** (−46 to −56 J/mol·K) indicates significant ordering of the coordination sphere and solvent upon binding two NH₃ ligands, even though the reaction is enthalpically driven (ΔH ≈ −59 kJ/mol).
