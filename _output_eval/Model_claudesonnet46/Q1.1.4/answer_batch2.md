### Chemistry Insights

1. **Linear geometry preference**: Ag⁺ (d¹⁰) strongly favors 2-coordinate linear complexes, which is why only [Ag(NH₃)]⁺ and [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ are observed — no tris- or tetrakis-ammine complexes form under normal conditions.

3. **Practical relevance**: The [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ complex ("Tollens' reagent") is widely used in silver mirror reactions and in analytical chemistry for distinguishing aldehydes from ketones. The high overall β₂ (~10⁷–10⁸) ensures near-complete complexation in ammonia-rich solutions.
