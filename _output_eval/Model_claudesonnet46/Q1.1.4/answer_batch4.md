### Chemistry Insights

1. **Linear diamine complex dominates**: The stepwise constant for the second NH₃ (log K₂ ≈ 7.22 − 3.31 = 3.91) is actually *larger* than K₁ (3.31), which is unusual — it reflects the strong preference of Ag⁺ for linear 2-coordinate geometry, making [Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ the overwhelmingly dominant species in ammonia solutions.

2. **Ionic strength insensitivity**: log β values are remarkably stable across I = 0–3 M (varying by only ~0.1–0.2 log units), suggesting the reaction involves species of similar charge type with minimal activity coefficient effects.

3. **Negative ΔS for β₂**: The large negative entropy (−55.6 to −46.4 J/mol·K) for the bis-ammine complex reflects significant ordering of the coordination sphere, consistent with the rigid linear [H₃N–Ag–NH₃]⁺ geometry.

4. **Real-world relevance**: This system is the basis of Tollens' reagent ([Ag(NH₃)₂]⁺ in alkaline solution), widely used to detect aldehydes via the silver mirror reaction.
