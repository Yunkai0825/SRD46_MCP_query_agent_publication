## 4. Chemistry Insights

1. **Hard vs. Borderline Acids:** Fe³⁺ is a classic **hard Lewis acid** (HSAB), strongly preferring oxygen donors — catecholates, hydroxamates, and carboxylates. This is why siderophores (biological Fe³⁺ chelators) are almost exclusively O-donor ligands. Cu²⁺ is a **borderline acid** with much broader ligand tolerance, explaining its ~5× larger ligand partner count.

2. **Hydrolysis & Polynuclear Chemistry:** Fe³⁺'s aggressive hydrolysis (visible in the SRD-46 catalog with Fe₂, Fe₃, and Fe₇ polynuclear species) makes speciation modeling complex. The database records species like [Fe₇(OH)₉L₆] — a testament to iron's tendency to form oxo/hydroxo clusters, relevant to rust formation and iron mineral precipitation in environmental chemistry.

3. **Cu⁺ vs. Fe²⁺ — The Soft/Hard Divide:** Both are +1/+2 ions, but Cu⁺ (d¹⁰) strongly prefers **soft donors** (thiols, cyanide, phosphines) and tetrahedral geometry, while Fe²⁺ (d⁶) favors **octahedral** coordination with N,O donors. This distinction is biologically critical: Cu⁺ is the active form in copper chaperones and blue copper proteins, while Fe²⁺ is central to heme and iron-sulfur clusters.
