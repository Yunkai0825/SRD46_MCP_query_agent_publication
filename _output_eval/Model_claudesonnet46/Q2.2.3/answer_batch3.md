### Chemistry Insights

- **Large polyamine macrocycles dominate the top spots.** [33]aneN11 and [36]aneN12 вЂ” cyclic rings bearing 11 and 12 nitrogen donors вЂ” each show 11 pKa steps, reflecting sequential protonation of every amine nitrogen. The large ring size allows all nitrogens to be protonated without excessive electrostatic repulsion.

- **Polyaminocarboxylate and phosphonate chelators** (DTPA, TTHA, PHOA, DTPP) rank highly because they combine multiple amine and carboxylate/phosphonate groups, each contributing one or more ionizable protons. These are widely used in medical imaging (MRI contrast agents) and nuclear medicine.

- **Phytic acid** (myo-inositol hexaphosphate, 8 pKa values) is a biologically important molecule found in seeds and grains; its many phosphate groups give it exceptional metal-chelating ability, which is why it is considered an antinutrient that sequesters dietary minerals like ZnВІвЃє and FeВівЃє.
