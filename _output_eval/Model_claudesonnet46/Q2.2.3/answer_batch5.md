## Chemistry Insights 🔬

1. **Aza-macrocycles dominate the top spots**: The cyclic polyamine ligands [33]aneN11 and [36]aneN12 each have **11 pKa values** — one per nitrogen atom — reflecting sequential protonation of each ring nitrogen. The large macrocyclic cavity means each protonation step is well-resolved and measurable.

2. **Polydentate chelators (DTPA, TTHA, PHOA)** accumulate pKa values from both amine nitrogens and carboxylate groups. PHOA, with 6 nitrogen donors and 8 carboxylate arms, reaches 10 pKa values, making it one of the most complex protonation systems in the database.

3. **Phytic acid** (myo-inositol hexaphosphate, ligand_9171) is a biologically important molecule found in plant seeds. Its 8 pKa values arise from the 6 phosphate groups (each with 2 ionizable protons), and its strong metal-chelating ability is why it is considered an antinutrient — it sequesters essential minerals like Fe²⁺, Zn²⁺, and Ca²⁺ in the gut.
