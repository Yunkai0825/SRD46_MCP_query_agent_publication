### Chemistry Insight

The overwhelming dominance of 25 °C data reflects a long-standing convention in solution chemistry: the vast majority of stability-constant measurements reported in the literature were deliberately performed at 25 °C (298.15 K) to enable direct comparison with thermodynamic reference data and with each other. This temperature is the standard reference for the NIST/IUPAC recommended values. The remaining ~15% of measurements span a range of other temperatures (often 15–45 °C or higher), typically collected to derive enthalpy and entropy of complexation via van't Hoff analysis — a much more labour-intensive undertaking that explains why such data are comparatively rare.
