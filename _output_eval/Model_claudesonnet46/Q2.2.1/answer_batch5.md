### Chemistry Insight

The overwhelming dominance of 25 °C (≈85%) reflects the long-standing convention in solution thermodynamics to report equilibrium constants at the standard state temperature of 298.15 K. This makes data directly comparable across laboratories and consistent with tabulated thermodynamic quantities (ΔG°, ΔH°, ΔS°). The second most common temperature, **20 °C** (~8.7%), likely reflects older European laboratory practice, while **37 °C** (~2.2%) represents biologically relevant conditions used in studies of metal–ligand systems of medical or biochemical interest (e.g., metal binding to amino acids or drugs). The near-complete absence of NULL temperature values is a testament to the careful curation of this database.
