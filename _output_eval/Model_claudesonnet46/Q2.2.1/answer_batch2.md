### Chemistry Insight

The overwhelming dominance of 25 °C (≈ 85 % of all entries) reflects a long-standing convention in solution thermodynamics: 298.15 K is the standard reference temperature for tabulating thermodynamic quantities, making results directly comparable across laboratories and publications. The remaining ~15 % of measurements span other temperatures (often 20 °C, 37 °C for biological relevance, or variable-temperature series used to extract ΔH and ΔS via van't Hoff analysis). This strong bias toward 25 °C is both a strength (internal consistency) and a limitation (poor coverage of real-world conditions such as body temperature or environmental systems).
