## Key Chemistry Insights

1. **Biological relevance**: The prominence of glycine, histidine, cysteine, and imidazole reflects Zn²⁺'s central role in metalloenzymes (carbonic anhydrase, carboxypeptidase, zinc fingers). The strong affinity for thiolate (cysteine, log β > 10) underpins structural zinc sites in proteins.

2. **Hydrolysis**: With 33 entries for hydroxide, Zn²⁺ hydrolysis is well-characterized. Zinc forms polynuclear hydroxo species (Zn₂(OH)³⁺, Zn₄(OH)₄⁴⁺) at neutral-to-basic pH, relevant to corrosion chemistry and water treatment.

3. **Macrocyclic enhancement**: The macrocyclic diamine ligand shows log K = 8.89 for 1:1 binding — significantly stronger than simple amines — illustrating the classic **macrocyclic effect** driven by pre-organization entropy.
