## Chemistry Highlights

🔹 **Hydrolysis is prominent** — Zn(OH)⁺, Zn(OH)₂, and polynuclear hydroxo species account for 11 distinct beta definitions, reflecting zinc's well-known tendency to hydrolyze at neutral-to-basic pH.

🔹 **Biologically relevant ligands dominate the top ranks** — glycine, histidine, cysteine, imidazole, and NTPs are all key biological zinc ligands, consistent with zinc's role as the second most abundant transition metal in biology (after iron), found in ~10% of all human proteins.

🔹 **Diverse donor chemistry** — carboxylate (46%), amine (25–27%), and aromatic N-donors (pyridine 13%, imidazole) all feature prominently, reflecting zinc's borderline Lewis acid character (intermediate between hard and soft), which allows it to bind both O- and N/S-donor ligands.
