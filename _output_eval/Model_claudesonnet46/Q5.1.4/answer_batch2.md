### 🧬 Chemistry Highlights

1. **Biological relevance**: The prominence of glycine, histidine, cysteine, and imidazole in the top ligands mirrors Zn²⁺'s role in metalloenzymes (carbonic anhydrase, carboxypeptidase, zinc finger proteins), where these amino acid side chains coordinate the metal.

2. **Ligand diversity**: Zn²⁺ forms complexes with simple inorganic ligands (NH₃, CN⁻, SCN⁻, OH⁻), amino acids, polyaminocarboxylates (EDTA, NTA, DTPA), N-donor aromatics (bipy, phen), and phosphonates — reflecting its borderline Lewis acid character (HSAB theory) and preference for tetrahedral or octahedral coordination.

3. **Hydrolysis**: The hydroxide system (`ligand_10076`) shows 13 distinct species including multiple solid-phase polymorphs of Zn(OH)₂ — important for understanding Zn²⁺ speciation in environmental and biological contexts.
