### Chemistry Insight

The **bis-complex ZnL₂** (log β ≈ 12.0 at 25°C, I = 0.1 M) dominates at physiological pH, consistent with zinc's well-known preference for nitrogen-rich coordination environments. Histidine acts as a tridentate ligand through its imidazole N, α-amino N, and carboxylate O, giving a stable pseudo-tetrahedral or distorted octahedral geometry. The ionic-strength dependence (log β rising from 12.04 at I = 0.1 M to 12.74 at I = 3.0 M) reflects activity-coefficient effects on the highly charged transition state. These constants are directly relevant to zinc speciation in blood plasma and metalloprotein active-site modeling.
