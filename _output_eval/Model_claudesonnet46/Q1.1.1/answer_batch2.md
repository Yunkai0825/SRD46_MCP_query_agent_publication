### Key Observations

1. **Ionic-strength effect:** At 25 °C, log β₁ passes through a minimum near I = 0.5 M (~8.10) and rises slightly at higher ionic strengths — a classic Debye–Hückel / activity-coefficient effect for a 2+/1− reaction.

2. **Temperature effect:** Both β₁ and β₂ decrease with increasing temperature (8.80 → 8.37 for β₁ from 10 → 40 °C), consistent with the negative ΔH values, meaning complex formation is **enthalpically driven** but also entropically favoured (positive ΔS), the latter reflecting release of water molecules from the Cu²⁺ coordination sphere.

3. **Chelate stability:** The log β₂ ≈ 15.7 (25 °C, I = 0) is nearly twice log β₁ ≈ 8.56, consistent with a stepwise addition of a second glycinate with a step constant log K₂ ≈ 7.1. The bis-glycinate complex Cu(Gly)₂ is a well-known square-planar chelate and is the dominant species at physiological pH when glycine is in excess.
