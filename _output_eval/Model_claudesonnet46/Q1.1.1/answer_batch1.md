### Key Observations

1. **Very strong complexation.** log β₁ ≈ 8.56 and log β₂ ≈ 15.7 at 25 °C, *I* → 0 reflect the well-known affinity of Cu²⁺ for the amino-acid chelate ring (5-membered, N,O-donor). These rank among the highest β values for simple amino acids with first-row transition metals.

2. **Ionic-strength effect.** At 25 °C, log β₁ drops from 8.56 (*I* = 0) to a minimum of ~8.10 (*I* = 0.5 M) before rising again at higher ionic strength — a classic Debye–Hückel / activity-coefficient pattern for a 1:1 charged complex.

3. **Enthalpy–entropy compensation.** The negative ΔH values (exothermic bond formation) are partially offset by a large positive ΔS (chelate ring formation releases water molecules from the Cu²⁺ coordination sphere), making these complexes both enthalpically and entropically favoured — a hallmark of chelate stability.

4. **Temperature dependence.** log β₁ decreases from 8.80 (10 °C) to 7.99 (37 °C, *I* = 0.15 M), consistent with the exothermic nature of complexation (Le Chatelier's principle).
