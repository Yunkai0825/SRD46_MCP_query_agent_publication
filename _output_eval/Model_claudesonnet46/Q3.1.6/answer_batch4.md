### Chemistry Insights

1. **Chelate effect dominates:** EDTA (log β = 15.5) and NTA (log β = 10.36) vastly outperform simple mono- or dicarboxylates because their multiple carboxylate + amine donor arms wrap around La³⁺ in a highly stable polydentate cage. La³⁺ has a large ionic radius (~1.16 Å) and readily accommodates coordination numbers of 8–9, making it an ideal match for these high-denticity ligands.

2. **Hydroxy-carboxylates beat plain dicarboxylates:** Citric acid (log β = 8.0), malic acid (5.3), and tartaric acid (5.1) all exceed malonic acid (4.7). The α-hydroxyl group provides an additional hard oxygen donor that stabilizes the La³⁺ complex — La³⁺ is a prototypical hard Lewis acid with a strong preference for oxygen donors.

3. **Simple monocarboxylates are weak binders:** Acetic acid (log β = 2.7) and glycolic acid (3.1) form only marginally stable complexes. Despite this, such weak interactions are environmentally relevant: La³⁺ mobility in soils and natural waters is partly governed by competition among these simple organic acids present in humic matter.
