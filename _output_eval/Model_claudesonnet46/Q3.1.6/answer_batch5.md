### Chemistry Insights

1. **Denticity drives stability.** The DTPA > EDTA > NTA ordering directly reflects increasing number of donor atoms (8 vs. 6 vs. 4). Each additional chelate ring contributes roughly 3–5 log units to the overall stability constant — a textbook illustration of the chelate effect.

2. **La³⁺ is a large, hard Lewis acid** (ionic radius ~1.03 Å in 8-coordination), making it an ideal partner for hard oxygen donors. Carboxylate groups are perfectly matched, which is why aminopolycarboxylates — combining carboxylate O and amine N donors — dominate the top of the stability ranking.

3. **Real-world relevance.** The high DTPA–La³⁺ stability (log β = 19.48) is the thermodynamic foundation for lanthanide–DTPA chelates used in MRI contrast agents and nuclear medicine radiopharmaceuticals, where both thermodynamic stability and kinetic inertness are essential for safe in vivo use.

> **Source:** NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes). All values are overall ML formation constants (log β) in aqueous solution at 25 °C.
