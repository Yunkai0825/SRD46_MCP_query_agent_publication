### Chemistry Insight

🔬 **Three key takeaways:**

1. **Stepwise log K values** (derived from consecutive β differences) are approximately **5.8, 5.5, and 4.7–4.9** for the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd bipy additions — a modest but typical statistical decrease reflecting reduced electrostatic attraction and steric crowding as the coordination sphere fills.

2. **The negative ΔH values** confirm that Co(II)–bipy binding is **enthalpically driven**, consistent with the strong σ-donor / π-acceptor character of bipyridyl toward a d⁷ metal center.

3. **Ionic strength has minimal effect** (log β shifts < 0.2 log units between I = 0.1 and 1.0 M), suggesting the dominant species are charge-symmetric enough that activity corrections largely cancel — a useful practical observation for solution modeling.
