## Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ Electrode Potential

The **NIST SRD-46 database** is specifically focused on **metal–ligand stability constants** (formation constants, β values) and **ligand pKa values** in solution. It does **not** contain electrochemical data such as standard electrode (reduction) potentials.

---

### Standard Answer from Established Electrochemistry Data

The standard reduction potential for the Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ couple is well-established:

| Half-Reaction | E° (V vs. SHE) | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu⁰ | **+0.342 V** | 25 °C, aqueous, unit activity |

> **Source:** NIST Chemistry WebBook / IUPAC standard tables (not SRD-46).

---

### A Little Chemistry Context

- The **positive E°** means Cu²⁺ is a moderately strong oxidizing agent — copper metal dissolves readily in oxidizing acids (e.g., HNO₃) but **not** in non-oxidizing acids like HCl under standard conditions.
- This value is central to understanding **copper corrosion**, **electroplating**, and **copper-based batteries**.
- If you're interested in how **complexation** shifts the *apparent* reduction potential (e.g., Cu²⁺ with ammonia, EDTA, or cyanide), SRD-46 **can** help — the Nernst equation links stability constants to conditional potentials. Just ask! 🔬
