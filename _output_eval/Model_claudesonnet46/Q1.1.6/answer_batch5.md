### 🔬 Chemistry Note
Amino acids are among the most extensively studied ligands in coordination chemistry due to their biological relevance. They coordinate metals through their **carboxylate** and **amino** groups (and sometimes side chains like imidazole in histidine or thiol in cysteine), making them key models for understanding metal–protein interactions. The 297 entries in SRD-46 reflect the breadth of this field, from simple glycine to complex modified amino acids and herbicides like glyphosate.
