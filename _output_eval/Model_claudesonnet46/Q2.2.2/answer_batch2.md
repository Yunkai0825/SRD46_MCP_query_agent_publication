## 🏆 Cu²⁺ Has the Most Ligands Studied

Based on the complete SRD-46 database ranking, **Cu²⁺ (copper(II) ion)** holds the record for the largest number of different ligands studied — by a wide margin.

> *Note: H⁺ appears at the top (4,081) but it is a pseudo-metal used for pKa measurements, not a true metal complex center.*

### Chemistry Insight

**Cu²⁺ dominates** with 1,951 distinct ligands — ~43% more than the runner-up Ni²⁺. This reflects copper(II)'s exceptional coordination chemistry: its d⁹ electronic configuration leads to Jahn-Teller distorted square-planar or elongated octahedral geometries that accommodate an enormous variety of donor atoms (N, O, S, P). Cu²⁺ is also biologically ubiquitous and industrially important, driving intense research interest across amino acids, macrocycles, Schiff bases, and countless other ligand families.

The top 5 are all first-row transition metals (Cu, Ni, Zn, Co, Cd), reflecting the central role of the Irving-Williams series in coordination chemistry research priorities.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
