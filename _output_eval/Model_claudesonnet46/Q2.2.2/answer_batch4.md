### Why Cu²⁺?

Cu²⁺'s dominance is no surprise to coordination chemists. It is a **borderline Lewis acid** (Irving–Williams series peak) with a strong preference for nitrogen and oxygen donors, making it reactive with an enormous variety of organic ligands — from simple amino acids to complex macrocycles. Its biological relevance (enzymes, proteins) and industrial importance (catalysis, electroplating) have driven decades of intensive study, resulting in nearly **twice as many ligand partners** as the second-ranked Co²⁺.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
