## Result: No Stability Constant Found for Pu(IV) + Aspirin

---

### Summary

The **NIST SRD-46 database does not contain a stability constant for plutonium(IV) with aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid, ligand_10913)**. A direct search for the Pu⁴⁺ / aspirin pair returned no measurements, and the ligand record for aspirin itself shows **zero VLM (value-measurement) entries** across all metals.

### Chemistry Note

This absence is chemically unsurprising for two reasons:
1. **Aspirin hydrolyzes rapidly in aqueous solution** to salicylate (2-hydroxybenzoate) and acetic acid, so any real-world Pu(IV)–"aspirin" interaction would actually be a Pu(IV)–salicylate interaction.
2. **Pu(IV) is a hard, highly charged cation** (charge +4) that strongly prefers hard oxygen donors like fluoride, oxalate, and hydroxide — the ligands that *do* appear in SRD-46. Carboxylate complexes of Pu(IV) with aromatic ligands are rarely studied under well-defined aqueous conditions.

If you are interested in the closest analogue, a search for **Pu(IV)–salicylate** would be the recommended next step.

> **Source:** NIST SRD-46 (NIST Standard Reference Database 46 — NIST Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)
