## Oxalic Acid — pKa Values from NIST SRD-46

**Oxalic acid** (Ethanedioic acid, SMILES: `O=C(O)C(=O)O`) is a **diprotic acid** with **two dissociation steps**:

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | T (°C) | I (M) |
|------|-------------|-----|--------|-------|
| 1st dissociation | H₂L → HL⁻ + H⁺ | **−1.2** | 25 | 0.1 |
| 2nd dissociation | HL⁻ → L²⁻ + H⁺ | **3.8** | 25 | 0.1 |

### Key Findings

- **Two dissociation steps** are confirmed (H₂L → HL⁻ → L²⁻), consistent with oxalic acid being a dicarboxylic acid.
- **pKa₁ = −1.2** — This is unusually low (strongly acidic), meaning the first proton is released almost completely even in moderately acidic solutions. This reflects the strong electron-withdrawing effect of the adjacent carbonyl group.
- **pKa₂ = 3.8** — The second dissociation is much weaker, as the negatively charged HL⁻ intermediate resists losing another proton.
- The large gap (~5 pKa units) between the two steps is characteristic of dicarboxylic acids where the two carboxylate groups are adjacent (α-position), maximizing inductive destabilization of the dianion.

> **Source:** NIST SRD-46 (ligand_8872, metal_68/H⁺), measured at 25 °C, I = 0.1 M (aqueous), with 56 total VLM entries across both steps.
