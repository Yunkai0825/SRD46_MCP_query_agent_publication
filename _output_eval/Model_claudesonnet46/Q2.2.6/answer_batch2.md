### Answer

**114 out of 230 metals (~49.6%) have fewer than 5 VLM measurements.** Nearly half the database's metal entries are data-sparse.

### Chemistry Insight

- **74 metals (32%)** have *zero* VLM measurements — these include noble gases (He, Ne, Ar…), superheavy elements (Rf, Db, Sg…), and highly unstable high-oxidation-state ions (Ag³⁺, Mn⁴⁺, Pt⁴⁺) that are either chemically inert toward ligand complexation or too short-lived to study in solution.
- The remaining 40 metals with 1–4 measurements represent real but poorly characterized systems — exotic organometallics (Et₃Sn⁺, Ph₃Pb⁺), rare actinides (Md³⁺, Pa⁵⁺), and unusual oxo-cations (TcO²⁺, Re⁵⁺).
- In contrast, the data-rich metals (Cu²⁺, Ni²⁺, Co²⁺, Zn²⁺, Cd²⁺, Pb²⁺) each have thousands of VLMs, reflecting decades of intensive study in analytical, biological, and environmental chemistry.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 — Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes*
