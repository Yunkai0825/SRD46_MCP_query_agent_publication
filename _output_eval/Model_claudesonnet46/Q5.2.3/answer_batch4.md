## Stability Constants at 200 °C and 50 bar — Not Available in SRD-46

After a thorough search of the NIST SRD-46 database, **no stability constants at 200 °C (473 K) or 50 bar pressure exist in this dataset.** Here's a summary of what was found:

| Pressure field in database | **Not indexed** |

### Why This Gap Exists

SRD-46 (NIST Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes) is primarily a **room-temperature database**. The vast majority of its ~200,000+ measurements were made at or near **25 °C** in aqueous solution at atmospheric pressure. The database's temperature ceiling is only **81 °C**, and **pressure is not indexed at all** — most measurements are implicitly at 1 atm.

### What You Would Need Instead

For stability constants at **200 °C / 50 bar** (hydrothermal conditions), you would need to consult:

- **Hydrothermal geochemistry databases** (e.g., SUPCRT92, GEMS, or the LLNL thermodynamic database)
- **Specific literature** on high-temperature aqueous speciation (e.g., work by Shock, Helgeson, or Sverjensky)
- **Extrapolation models** such as the Helgeson–Kirkham–Flowers (HKF) equation of state, which can predict stability constants at elevated T and P from 25 °C reference values

> **Note:** At 200 °C and 50 bar, water is still liquid (the saturation pressure of water at 200 °C is ~15.5 bar, so 50 bar keeps it liquid). This is a classic hydrothermal regime relevant to geothermal systems, nuclear reactor coolants, and ore deposit formation — but it is outside the scope of SRD-46.
