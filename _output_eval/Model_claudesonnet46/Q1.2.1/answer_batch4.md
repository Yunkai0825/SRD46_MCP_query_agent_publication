---

**Chemistry insight:** This landmark paper from Schwarzenbach's group at ETH Zürich was central to establishing EDTA as the quintessential hexadentate chelating agent. A log K of ~18.8 for Cu²⁺–EDTA reflects exceptional thermodynamic stability driven by the chelate effect — up to five fused five-membered chelate rings are formed upon simultaneous coordination of four carboxylate oxygens and two amine nitrogens to the copper center. This extraordinary stability underpins EDTA's widespread use in analytical chemistry (complexometric titrations), medicine (heavy-metal chelation therapy), and industrial applications (water softening, scale prevention) to this day.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
