### Chemistry Context

A log K of 18.78 reflects the extraordinary thermodynamic stability of the Cu²⁺–EDTA chelate, arising from EDTA's hexadentate coordination (four carboxylate oxygens + two amine nitrogens) that satisfies Cu²⁺'s preference for a distorted octahedral geometry. This 1954 paper by Schwarzenbach and colleagues was a cornerstone of modern coordination chemistry — Schwarzenbach is widely credited with establishing the quantitative framework for polyaminocarboxylate chelation, directly enabling EDTA's use in analytical titrations, medical chelation therapy, and industrial metal sequestration.
