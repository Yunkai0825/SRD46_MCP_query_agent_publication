### Chemistry Context

This 1949 paper by Schwarzenbach's group at ETH Zürich was part of the foundational work establishing EDTA as a powerful chelating agent. The exceptionally high log K of ~18.78 for Cu²⁺–EDTA reflects the strong thermodynamic driving force from the hexadentate coordination (four carboxylate + two amine donors), making EDTA one of the most effective ligands for Cu²⁺ in aqueous solution. This work laid the groundwork for EDTA's widespread use in analytical chemistry (complexometric titrations) and industrial applications.
