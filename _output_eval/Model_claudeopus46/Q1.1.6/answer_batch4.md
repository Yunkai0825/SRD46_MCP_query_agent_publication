## Result

The NIST SRD-46 database contains **297 ligands** classified under the **"Amino Acids"** class.

This is a large and diverse group, reflecting the wide variety of natural amino acids, their derivatives (e.g., N-methyl, N-acetyl, hydroxyl variants), peptides, and related compounds that have been studied for their metal-binding equilibria. Amino acids are among the most extensively characterized ligand families in coordination chemistry due to their biological importance and their ability to chelate metal ions through amino (–NH₂) and carboxylate (–COO⁻) groups.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
