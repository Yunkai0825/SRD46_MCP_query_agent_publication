### Chemistry Highlights

- **Zinc hydrolysis** (ligand_10076) is the most species-rich system with **13 distinct species**, including polynuclear hydroxo complexes and multiple solid-phase polymorphs — reflecting Zn's amphoteric character.
- **Biologically relevant ligands** are well represented: glycine, histidine, cysteine, and penicillamine all have extensive datasets. This is fitting since Zn²⁺ is the second most abundant transition metal in biology, essential in enzymes like carbonic anhydrase and zinc finger proteins.
- **Strong chelators** like EDTA (log K₁ typically ~16.5) and NTP are included, important for analytical chemistry and environmental remediation applications.
