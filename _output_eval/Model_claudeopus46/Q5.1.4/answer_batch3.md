### Key Observations

1. **Amino acids dominate** — accounting for ~16% of all measurements, consistent with Zn²⁺'s critical role in metalloenzymes and its strong affinity for nitrogen and sulfur donors (histidine, cysteine).

2. **Rich speciation chemistry** — Zn²⁺ forms stepwise complexes (ML, ML₂, ML₃, ML₄) with many ligands, and the hydroxide system alone has **13 distinct species** including solids (ZnO, various Zn(OH)₂ polymorphs) and polynuclear complexes.

3. **Broad condition coverage** — Measurements span temperatures from 5–45 °C and ionic strengths from near-zero to 5 M, making this dataset valuable for modeling Zn²⁺ behavior across environmental, biological, and industrial conditions.

4. **Chelator diversity** — Strong representation of EDTA/NTA-type chelators, macrocycles, and phosphonate ligands reflects Zn²⁺'s practical importance in water treatment, metal sequestration, and analytical chemistry.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
