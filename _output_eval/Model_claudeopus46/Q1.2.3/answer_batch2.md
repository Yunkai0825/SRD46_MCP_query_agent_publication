### Chemistry Insights

This network beautifully illustrates the **amphoteric** nature of zinc. In acidic solution, ZnВІвҒә dominates; as pH rises, successive hydroxo complexes form вҖ” Zn(OH)вҒә вҶ’ Zn(OH)вӮӮ(aq) вҶ’ Zn(OH)вӮғвҒ» вҶ’ Zn(OH)вӮ„ВІвҒ» (the zincate ion). The network also captures **five crystallographic polymorphs** of solid Zn(OH)вӮӮ plus ZnO, each with slightly different solubility products. The amorphous form (log K = вҲ’14.82) is notably more soluble than the crystalline phases (log K вүҲ вҲ’15.5 to вҲ’16.0), consistent with the general principle that amorphous solids have higher free energy and dissolve more readily. This rich speciation is why zinc hydroxide chemistry is central to understanding corrosion, galvanizing, and zinc-air battery electrochemistry.
