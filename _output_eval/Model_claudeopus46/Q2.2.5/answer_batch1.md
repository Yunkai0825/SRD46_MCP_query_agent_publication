## Distribution of Constant Types in SRD-46 Stability Measurements

The NIST SRD-46 database contains **79,063** stability constant measurements, distributed across three constant types:

| Constant Type | Count | Percentage |
|:---:|---:|---:|
| **K** (stepwise equilibrium constant) | 60,540 | 76.57% |
| **H** (protonation / mixed protonation constant) | 9,693 | 12.26% |
| **S** (overall stability constant, β) | 8,830 | 11.17% |

### Key Observations

- **K-type constants** dominate the database, accounting for over three-quarters of all entries. These represent stepwise equilibrium constants for successive ligand binding (e.g., K₁, K₂, …), which are the most commonly reported form in experimental coordination chemistry literature.
- **H-type constants** (≈12%) capture protonation equilibria or mixed-proton metal–ligand systems, reflecting the importance of pH-dependent speciation in real-world solutions.
- **S-type (β) constants** (≈11%) represent cumulative (overall) stability constants. Although β values are often derived from stepwise K values, many authors report them directly, especially for higher-order complexes where stepwise resolution is difficult.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
