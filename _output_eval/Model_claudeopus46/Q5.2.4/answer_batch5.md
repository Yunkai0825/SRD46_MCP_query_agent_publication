The NIST SRD-46 database contains **no stability constant data** for the system **plutonium(IV) (Pu⁴⁺) with aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid)**.

### Chemistry insight

The absence of Pu(IV)–aspirin data is not surprising. Aspirin is a weak monodentate carboxylate (pKₐ ≈ 3.45) that forms only modest complexes even with transition metals like Cu²⁺ (log K = 1.56). Pu⁴⁺ is an extremely hard, highly charged cation that hydrolyzes aggressively at low pH, making it difficult to study with weak ligands. Researchers have focused on stronger chelators (DTPA, oxalate, acetylacetone) that are more relevant to nuclear fuel processing and environmental remediation. If you need an estimate, salicylic acid (the hydrolysis product of aspirin, a stronger bidentate chelator) with an analogous tetravalent actinide like Th⁴⁺ might serve as a rough proxy — I can look that up if you're interested.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
