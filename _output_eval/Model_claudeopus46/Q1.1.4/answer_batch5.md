## Chemistry Insights

The **Ag(NH₃)₂⁺** (diamminesilver) complex is notably stable with log β₂ ≈ 7.2 at zero ionic strength — this is the basis of **Tollens' reagent** used in qualitative analysis for aldehydes. The stepwise log K₁ ≈ 3.3 and the derived log K₂ ≈ 3.9 (= 7.22 − 3.31) show a slight **positive cooperativity** (K₂ > K₁), which is unusual for simple metal–ligand systems and reflects the preference of Ag⁺ for linear two-coordinate geometry. The strongly negative ΔH values confirm that complex formation is **enthalpically driven**, while the negative ΔS is consistent with the loss of translational freedom upon binding.
