### Key Observations

1. **The spread in log K values** (e.g., 3.2–3.8 for β₁) reflects measurements at different ionic strengths (0 → 5 M) and in different background electrolytes (e.g., NaClO₄, KNO₃). Values at I → 0 tend toward the higher end of the range.

2. **Both complexes are strongly exothermic** (negative ΔH), consistent with the well-known affinity of Ag⁺ for nitrogen donors — a soft acid–soft base interaction. The large negative ΔS for Ag(NH₃)₂⁺ reflects the ordering of two solvent-displacing ligands around the metal center.

3. **Practical relevance:** These constants underpin the classic "ammonia dissolution" of AgCl (Ksp ≈ 10⁻¹⁰) — with β₂ ≈ 10⁷·², the diamine complex is stable enough to dissolve AgCl in concentrated NH₃, a reaction widely used in qualitative analysis and silver plating.
