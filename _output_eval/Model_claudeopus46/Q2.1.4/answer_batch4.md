**Chloride forms the stronger complex with Cu²⁺ at every comparable ionic strength.** At zero ionic strength, log K₁(CuCl⁺) = +0.20 vs. log K₁(CuBr⁺) = −0.04, meaning the chloride complex is roughly **1.7× more stable** (K₁ ratio ≈ 10^0.24 ≈ 1.7).

### Chemical Insight

This trend is consistent with the **Irving–Williams / hard-soft acid-base (HSAB) framework**: Cu²⁺ is a borderline-to-hard Lewis acid, and Cl⁻ is harder than Br⁻, so the harder halide binds more strongly. Both complexes are quite weak (log K₁ near zero), reflecting the generally modest affinity of divalent copper for monodentate halide anions in water — a stark contrast to the much larger stability constants Cu²⁺ forms with nitrogen- or oxygen-donor chelates like EDTA (log K ≈ 18.8). The increasing log K with rising ionic strength is also noteworthy and likely reflects medium effects (activity coefficient changes) rather than intrinsic binding enhancement.
