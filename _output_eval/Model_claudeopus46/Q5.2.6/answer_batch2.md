## Result

**No stability constants measured in supercritical CO₂ are available in the NIST SRD-46 database.**

The database's `solvent_name` field contains only **H₂O (water)** — all 79,063 measured stability constant records use water as the solvent. There are no entries for supercritical CO₂ (scCO₂), liquid CO₂, or any non-aqueous/mixed solvent system.

### Why?

NIST SRD-46 (the **NIST Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes** database) is focused on **aqueous-phase** equilibrium thermodynamics. It curates critically evaluated log β values for metal–ligand complexes in water, typically at controlled temperature and ionic strength.

Stability constant measurements in supercritical CO₂ are quite rare in the broader literature and tend to appear in specialized extraction/separation studies (e.g., chelation of lanthanides or actinides with fluorinated β-diketones in scCO₂). Such data would more likely be found in:

- Primary research articles (e.g., journals like *The Journal of Supercritical Fluids*)
- Specialized databases for supercritical fluid chemistry

**Source:** NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46).
