## Result

**No stability constants measured in supercritical CO₂ are available in the NIST SRD-46 database.**

The database's `solvent_name` field contains only **H₂O (water)** — all 79,063 stability-constant records in SRD-46 were measured in aqueous solution. There are no entries for supercritical CO₂, organic solvents, or any other non-aqueous medium.

### Why?

NIST SRD-46 (the **NIST Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes**) is specifically curated for **aqueous-phase** thermodynamic data. Supercritical CO₂ complexation studies do exist in the research literature (e.g., metal–β-diketonate or metal–organophosphorus complexes in scCO₂ for extraction applications), but they fall outside the scope of this particular database.

**If you need scCO₂ stability data**, you would need to search the primary literature directly (e.g., via SciFinder, Web of Science) or specialized reviews on supercritical fluid extraction chemistry.
