## Chemistry Insights

The enormous gap between aminopolycarboxylates (log K₁ ≈ 8–12) and simple carboxylic acids (log K₁ ≈ 2.8–3.6) highlights the **chelate effect**: multidentate ligands like EDTA wrap around La³⁺ with up to six donor atoms (4 N + 2 COO⁻ or 2 N + 4 COO⁻), dramatically boosting stability. Among pure carboxylic acids, **oxydiacetic acid** leads because its ether oxygen provides an additional coordination site beyond the two carboxylate groups, creating a tridentate chelate. Malonic acid's five-membered chelate ring also gives it an edge over monodentate carboxylates like acetic acid (log K₁ ≈ 1.8).

La³⁺ is a hard Lewis acid with a large ionic radius, so it prefers hard oxygen donors — explaining why carboxylate ligands bind it reasonably well, though the relatively low charge density of La³⁺ (compared to heavier lanthanides) keeps the absolute log K values modest.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
