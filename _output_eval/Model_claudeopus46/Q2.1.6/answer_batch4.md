### Chemistry Insight

This result is a textbook illustration of **HSAB (Hard-Soft Acid-Base) theory**. Thiocyanate (SCN‚ĀĽ) is a soft/borderline base that coordinates through its sulfur atom to soft metals. Among these three d-block metals, Hg¬≤‚Āļ is the *softest* acid (large, polarizable, d¬Ļ‚Āį configuration), which explains its extraordinarily strong affinity for the soft sulfur donor. Cd¬≤‚Āļ is borderline-soft and Pb¬≤‚Āļ is borderline, consistent with their progressively weaker binding. The large exothermic őĒH for Hg¬≤‚Āļ‚ÄďSCN‚ĀĽ (‚ąí49 kJ/mol) compared to Cd¬≤‚Āļ (‚ąí13 kJ/mol) and Pb¬≤‚Āļ (‚ąí1.3 kJ/mol) further underscores the dominant covalent character of the Hg‚ÄďS bond.
