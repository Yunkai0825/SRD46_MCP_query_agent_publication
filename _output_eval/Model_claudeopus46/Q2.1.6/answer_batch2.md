## Ranking of Cd²⁺, Pb²⁺, and Hg²⁺ by log K₁ with Thiocyanate

Using the stepwise formation constant **log K₁** for the 1:1 complex [ML]/[M][L] (beta_def_812) at **25 °C** from the NIST SRD-46 database:

### Comparable conditions (I ≈ 0 M, 25 °C)

| Metal | log K₁ | I (M) | T (°C) | vlm_id |
|-------|--------|-------|--------|--------|
| **Hg²⁺** | **9.64** | 0 | 25 | vlm_172457 |
| **Cd²⁺** | **1.93** | 0 | 25 | vlm_172411 |
| **Pb²⁺** | **~0.5–0.8** | 1–3 | 25 | vlm_172483–172485 |

> *Note: No I = 0 log K value is available for Pb²⁺ in SRD-46; at I = 1 M, log K₁ = 0.49. The true I = 0 value would be somewhat higher but still well below Cd²⁺.*

### At I ≈ 1 M, 25 °C (matched conditions for all three)

| Metal | log K₁ | I (M) |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **Hg²⁺** | **9.08** | 1 |
| **Cd²⁺** | **1.31** | 1 |
| **Pb²⁺** | **0.49** | 1 |

### Ranking (highest → lowest affinity)

$$\text{Hg}^{2+} \gg \text{Cd}^{2+} > \text{Pb}^{2+}$$

### Answer

**Mercury(II) has by far the highest affinity for thiocyanate**, with log K₁ ≈ 9.1–9.6 — roughly **7–8 orders of magnitude** larger than Cd²⁺ (log K₁ ≈ 1.3–1.9) and Pb²⁺ (log K₁ ≈ 0.5).

### Chemistry Insight

This result is a textbook illustration of **HSAB (Hard-Soft Acid-Base) theory**. Thiocyanate (SCN⁻) is a soft/borderline base that coordinates through sulfur to soft metals. Hg²⁺ is the quintessential soft acid, so the Hg–SCN bond is exceptionally strong (log K₁ ≈ 9.6). Cd²⁺ is borderline-soft and shows moderate affinity, while Pb²⁺ — more borderline-hard — binds thiocyanate only weakly. The large, favorable ΔH for Hg²⁺ (−49 to −50 kJ/mol) confirms the strong covalent character of the Hg–S interaction, consistent with soft-soft matching.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
