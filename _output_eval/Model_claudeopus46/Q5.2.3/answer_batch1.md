The NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes) database stores stability constants **per specific metal–ligand system**. To search effectively, I need you to specify:

1. **Metal ion** (and oxidation state) — e.g., Cu²⁺, Fe³⁺, Zn²⁺
2. **Ligand** — e.g., EDTA, glycine, oxalate, chloride

### A note on your conditions

- **200 °C** — SRD-46 does contain some high-temperature data, but the vast majority of measurements are at or near 25 °C. Coverage at 200 °C is limited to certain systems (e.g., some hydrothermal studies of simple ligands like chloride, hydroxide, or sulfate with common metals).
- **50 bar pressure** — SRD-46 does **not** index data by pressure. Stability constants in the database are typically reported at ambient or saturated vapor pressure. Pressure effects on aqueous equilibria are generally small at moderate pressures but can become significant under hydrothermal conditions.
