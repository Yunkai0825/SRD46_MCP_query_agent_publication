**Chemistry insight:** The largest "real" class is **Amino Acids** (297 ligands), which makes sense given their biological importance and the extensive study of metal–amino acid complexation in biochemistry. **Aliphatic amines** and **Pyridines** follow closely — both are classic nitrogen-donor ligands widely used in coordination chemistry. The top-ranked entry, "Ligands not selected," is a catch-all category for ligands that haven't been assigned to a specific structural class, accounting for nearly a quarter of all entries.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
