## Ligand Classes in SRD-46

There are **60 distinct ligand classes** in the database.

Here are the **five classes with the most ligands**:

| Rank | Ligand Class | Number of Ligands | % of Total |
|------|--------------------------|-------------------|------------|
| 1 | Ligands not selected | 1 339 | 23.3% |
| 2 | Amino Acids | 297 | 5.2% |
| 3 | Aliphatic amines | 250 | 4.4% |
| 4 | Pyridines (azines) | 248 | 4.3% |
| 5 | Carboxylic acids | 182 | 3.2% |

> **Note:** The top class, *"Ligands not selected,"* is a catch-all for ligands that have not been assigned to a specific chemical class — it accounts for nearly a quarter of all 5 750 ligands. If we exclude that placeholder, the top five "real" classes are **Amino Acids (297)**, **Aliphatic amines (250)**, **Pyridines/azines (248)**, **Carboxylic acids (182)**, and **Aza-macrocycles (165)**. These reflect the dominance of nitrogen- and oxygen-donor ligands in coordination chemistry, which is expected given their strong affinity for a wide range of metal ions.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
