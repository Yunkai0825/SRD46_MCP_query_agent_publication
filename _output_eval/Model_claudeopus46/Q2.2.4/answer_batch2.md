### Chemistry Insight

The dominance of **amino acids** and **aliphatic amines** reflects their central role in coordination chemistry — both provide strong nitrogen-donor sites that form stable complexes with a wide range of metal ions. **Pyridines** rank closely behind, consistent with their ubiquity as aromatic N-donors in catalysis and supramolecular chemistry. The large "unclassified" bin (~23%) suggests many structurally diverse or novel ligands that don't fit neatly into traditional categories.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
