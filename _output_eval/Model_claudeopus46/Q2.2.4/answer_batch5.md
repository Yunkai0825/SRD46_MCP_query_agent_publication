If we exclude that bin, the **top 5 named classes** are **Amino Acids** (297), **Aliphatic amines** (250), **Pyridines/azines** (248), **Carboxylic acids** (182), and **Aza-macrocycles** (165).

### Chemistry Insight

The dominance of amino acids and aliphatic amines reflects the historical importance of biologically relevant ligands in coordination chemistry — amino acids are ubiquitous metal binders in biological systems, while simple amines serve as fundamental N-donor ligands. Pyridine derivatives rank highly because the aromatic nitrogen is an excellent σ-donor, making pyridines workhorses in both analytical and industrial coordination chemistry.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
