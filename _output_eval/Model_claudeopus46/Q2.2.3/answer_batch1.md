## Ligands with More Than 6 Reported pKa Values in SRD-46

Yes! There are **two ligands** in the NIST SRD-46 database that have more than 6 distinct reported pKa values:

| Ligand ID | Ligand Name | Common Abbreviation | Distinct pKa Steps |
|-----------|-------------|---------------------|------------------:|
| ligand_6356 | Diethylenetrinitrilopentaacetic acid | **DTPA** | 8 |
| ligand_8412 | Nitrilotris(methylenephosphonic acid) | **NTP** | 7 |

### Chemistry Insight

Both of these results make excellent chemical sense:

- **DTPA** is an octadentate chelator with five carboxylate groups and three tertiary amine nitrogens, giving it up to 8 dissociable protons (5 from –COOH groups and 3 from –NH⁺– sites). It is widely used in MRI contrast agents (e.g., Gd-DTPA) and nuclear medicine.

- **NTP (Nitrilotris(methylenephosphonic acid))** contains three phosphonate groups (–PO₃H₂) plus a central nitrogen, providing up to 7 protonation steps. Phosphonates are industrially important as scale inhibitors and corrosion preventers.

These polyprotic ligands are among the most complex acid-base systems catalogued in the database, reflecting their rich stepwise protonation chemistry.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
