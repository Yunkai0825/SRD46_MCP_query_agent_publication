### Chemistry insight

Crown ethers are classic hosts for spherical, charge-dense cations (alkali and alkaline-earth metals) whose ionic radii match the cavity size. The uranyl ion UO₂²⁺ is **linear** and large, making it a poor geometric fit for the planar crown-ether cavity — which likely explains both the scarcity of experimental data and its absence from this curated database. Uranyl complexation chemistry is instead dominated by multidentate O/N-donor ligands (carbonates, phosphates, Schiff bases, etc.) that can coordinate in the equatorial plane of the linear O=U=O unit.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
