### Equilibrium Constants Reported

The study reports formation/protonation constants for **six distinct Zn²⁺–histidine species**, measured across a range of temperatures (20–41 °C) and ionic strengths (0–3 M):

| Species | Equilibrium Definition | Equation | # Measurements |
|---|---|---|:---:|
| **ZnL** | β₁ = [ML] / [M][L] | Zn²⁺ + L⁻ ⇌ ZnL⁺ | 8 |
| **ZnL₂** | β₂ = [ML₂] / [M][L]² | Zn²⁺ + 2 L⁻ ⇌ ZnL₂ | 12 |
| **ZnHL** (protonated 1:1) | K = [MHL] / [ML][H] | ZnL⁺ + H⁺ ⇌ ZnHL²⁺ | 4 |
| **ZnHL₂** (protonated 1:2) | K = [MHL₂] / [ML₂][H] | ZnL₂ + H⁺ ⇌ ZnHL₂⁺ | 3 |
| **Zn(HL)₂** | K = [M(HL)₂] / [MHL₂][H] | ZnHL₂⁺ + H⁺ ⇌ Zn(HL)₂²⁺ | 1 |
| **ZnL₂ (hydroxo)** | K = [ML₂] / [M(OH)L₂][H] | Zn(OH)L₂⁻ + H⁺ ⇌ ZnL₂ + H₂O | 1 |

The ligand reference form is **HL** (the zwitterionic form of L-histidine), with pKₐ values of approximately −1.7 (carboxyl), 6.05 (imidazole), and 9.10 (amino group).

### Chemistry Insight

Histidine is among the strongest amino acid chelators for Zn²⁺, owing to its imidazole side chain — the same motif that anchors zinc in zinc-finger proteins and many metalloenzymes (e.g., carbonic anhydrase). The rich speciation seen here — spanning protonated, neutral, and hydroxo complexes — reflects the multiple donor atoms (amino-N, imidazole-N, carboxylate-O) and the strong pH dependence of histidine coordination. The **ZnL₂** species (12 entries, the most measured) is the dominant complex near physiological pH, which is directly relevant to modeling zinc transport and bioavailability in biological fluids — precisely the context of the Berthon handbook in which this chapter appears.
