## Zinc–Histidine Equilibria: The 2005 Study

### Citation

> **Demirel, N., Bingoal, D., Doaanguen, F.E., Tuerkmen, H., Oezpozan, T.**
> *J. Solution Chem.*, **2005**, *34*, 839.
> (SRD-46 shortcut: **05Dm**)

### Reported Constants

The 2005 study by Demirel et al. is linked to **four VLM entries** in SRD-46, all measured at **37 °C** and **I = 0.15 M**. The constants they reported cover three Zn²⁺–L-histidine species:

| VLM ID | Equilibrium | Species | Type | Value | T (°C) | I (M) |
|--------|-------------|---------|------|-------|---------|-------|
| vlm_98900 | Zn²⁺ + L⁻ ⇌ ZnL⁺ | ZnL | log K | **6.35** | 37 | 0.15 |
| vlm_98909 | Zn²⁺ + 2 L⁻ ⇌ ZnL₂ | ZnL₂ | log β₂ | **11.67** | 37 | 0.15 |
| vlm_98920 | ZnL + H⁺ ⇌ ZnHL | ZnHL (protonated) | log K | **4.61** | 37 | 0.15 |
| vlm_98926 | ZnL₂ + H⁺ ⇌ ZnHL₂ | ZnHL₂ (protonated) | log K | **5.50** | 37 | 0.15 |

### Chemistry Insight

The stepwise formation constants (log K₁ = 6.35, log β₂ = 11.67, giving log K₂ ≈ 5.32) show the expected decrease for the second histidine binding step, consistent with statistical and steric effects at the zinc centre. The protonation constants of the complexes (log K ≈ 4.6–5.5) reflect protonation of the imidazole nitrogen in the coordinated histidine, which is notably lower than the free-ligand imidazole pKₐ (~6.05). This acidification upon coordination to Zn²⁺ is a well-known effect of metal-ion binding. The choice of 37 °C and physiological-like ionic strength (0.15 M) indicates the study was designed to model biologically relevant conditions — appropriate given histidine's prominent role as a zinc-binding residue in metalloproteins such as carbonic anhydrase and zinc-finger domains.
