## Standard Electrode Potential: Cu²⁺/Cu⁰

| Half-reaction | E° (V vs. SHE) | Temperature | Standard state |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cu²⁺(aq) + 2e⁻ → Cu(s) | **+0.340 V** | 25 °C (298.15 K) | Unit activities (1 M) |

### Key details

- This is the **standard reduction potential** measured against the **Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE)**.
- Under non-standard conditions, the potential shifts according to the **Nernst equation**: E = E° − (RT/2F) ln(1/[Cu²⁺]).
- **Do not confuse** with the related copper couples:

| Couple | E° (V vs. SHE) |
|---|---|
| Cu²⁺/Cu⁰ | +0.340 |
| Cu²⁺/Cu⁺ | +0.153 |
| Cu⁺/Cu⁰ | +0.521 |

### Chemistry Insight

The positive E° of +0.340 V means Cu²⁺ is thermodynamically easier to reduce than H⁺, placing copper **below hydrogen** in the activity series. This is why copper metal does not dissolve in non-oxidizing acids (e.g., dilute HCl). Also, the fact that E°(Cu⁺/Cu⁰) > E°(Cu²⁺/Cu⁺) explains why Cu⁺ is unstable in aqueous solution and **disproportionates** into Cu²⁺ and Cu⁰.
