### Chemistry Insights

The **reference values at 25 °C and zero ionic strength** are **log K₁ = 8.56** and **log β₂ = 15.70**, giving a stepwise log K₂ ≈ 7.14. The modest drop from K₁ to K₂ (Δ ≈ 1.4 log units) is characteristic of Cu²⁺ amino-acid complexes where the Jahn–Teller-distorted d⁹ ion readily accommodates two bidentate glycinate ligands in the equatorial plane. The large positive ΔS values reflect the release of coordinated water molecules upon chelation, which is a major thermodynamic driving force for these complexes. Increasing ionic strength generally lowers the constants slightly (activity-coefficient effects), while higher temperature also decreases log K, consistent with the exothermic ΔH values.
