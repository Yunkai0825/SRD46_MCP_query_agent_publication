## Chemistry Insights

1. **Soft-acid behavior:** Hg²⁺ is the quintessential **soft Lewis acid** (HSAB theory). This is clearly reflected in the data — its strongest and most-studied complexes are with **soft bases**: CN⁻, I⁻, SCN⁻, thiourea, and thiolates. The halide stability order follows Cl⁻ < Br⁻ < I⁻, opposite to hard acids like Fe³⁺.

2. **Exceptionally high coordination numbers:** Hg(II) readily forms ML₄ species with halides, cyanide, ammonia, and thiourea — consistent with its preference for linear (ML₂) and tetrahedral (ML₄) geometries. The database captures up to **6 distinct species** in some systems (e.g., Hg²⁺–hydroxide with polynuclear M₂L and M₃L₃ species).

3. **Environmental relevance of MeHg⁺:** The database's extensive coverage of methylmercury with biological ligands (glutathione, cysteine, methionine, glycine, BAL) reflects the intense research interest in mercury bioaccumulation and detoxification pathways. MeHg⁺ binds strongly to thiolate groups, which is the molecular basis for its neurotoxicity and bioconcentration through aquatic food chains.
