### Chemistry insight

The PbCl⁺ formation constant is one of the most extensively studied metal–halide equilibria, reflecting lead's environmental and geochemical importance. The sheer number of independent studies (spanning 1930–1994) underscores the difficulty of pinning down a precise value: different techniques (potentiometry, polarography, spectrophotometry) and ionic-strength conditions yield a spread of log β₁ values, typically around **1.4–1.6** at 25 °C and low ionic strength. This wealth of cross-referenced data in SRD-46 makes Pb²⁺–Cl⁻ an excellent benchmark system for evaluating measurement consistency.
