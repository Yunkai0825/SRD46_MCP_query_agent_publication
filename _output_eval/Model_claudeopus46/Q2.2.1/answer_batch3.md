## Stability-Constant Measurements at 25 °C in SRD-46

| Condition | Count | Percentage |
|-----------|------:|----------:|

**85.0% of all stability-constant measurements** in the NIST SRD-46 database were conducted at 25 °C.

This overwhelming preference for 25 °C (298.15 K) is standard practice in solution thermodynamics — it is the conventional reference temperature for reporting equilibrium constants, and most experimental setups (thermostated baths, calorimeters) default to it. The remaining ~15% at other temperatures are valuable for deriving enthalpy and entropy of complexation via van 't Hoff analysis, but they are comparatively scarce.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).*
