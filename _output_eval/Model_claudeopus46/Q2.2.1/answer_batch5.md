## Stability-Constant Measurements at 25 °C

| Condition | Count | Percentage |
|-----------|------:|----------:|
| At 25 °C | 67,237 | **85.0%** |
| All other temperatures | 11,826 | 15.0% |

**85.0% of all stability-constant measurements in the NIST SRD-46 database were conducted at 25 °C.**

This overwhelming preference for 25 °C reflects the long-standing convention in solution thermodynamics of reporting equilibrium data at "standard" conditions (25 °C ≈ 298.15 K). The next most common temperatures are 20 °C (8.7%) and 37 °C (2.2%) — the latter corresponding to physiological temperature, important for biochemical and clinical chemistry studies. Together, just these three temperatures account for roughly 96% of all measurements in the database.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
