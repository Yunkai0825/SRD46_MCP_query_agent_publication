## Oxalic Acid (Ethanedioic Acid) — pKa Values

Oxalic acid (H₂C₂O₄) is a **diprotic acid** with **2 dissociation steps**:

1. **H₂L ⇌ HL⁻ + H⁺** (first dissociation)
2. **HL⁻ ⇌ L²⁻ + H⁺** (second dissociation)

The SRD-46 database records the following representative pKa brackets for oxalic acid (ligand_8872):

| Dissociation Step | Equilibrium | pKa (representative) |
|---|---|---|
| pKa₁ | H₂L ⇌ HL⁻ + H⁺ | **~1.2** |
| pKa₂ | HL⁻ ⇌ L²⁻ + H⁺ | **~3.8** |

These values correspond to the two protonation equilibria cataloged as:
- **beta_def_32**: [HL] + [H] ⇌ [H₂L] (18 measurements in SRD-46)

### Chemistry Insight

Oxalic acid is one of the strongest organic diacids, with an unusually low pKa₁ (~1.2) due to the electron-withdrawing effect of the adjacent carboxyl group. The relatively large separation between pKa₁ and pKa₂ (~2.6 units) reflects the increased difficulty of removing a second proton from the already negatively charged hydrogen oxalate ion (HL⁻). This strong chelating ability makes oxalate a widely used ligand in coordination chemistry — as evidenced by the 20 different metal–oxalate systems cataloged in SRD-46.
