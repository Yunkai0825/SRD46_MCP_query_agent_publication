## pKa Values for Oxalic Acid (Ethanedioic Acid)

Oxalic acid is a **diprotic acid** with **2 dissociation steps**, consistent with its H₂L formulation (two carboxylic acid groups).

### Measured pKa Values (25 °C, I = 0.1 M)

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | vlm_id |
|------|-------------|-----|--------|
| 1st dissociation | H₂L ⇌ HL⁻ + H⁺ | **1.2** | vlm_151532 |
| 2nd dissociation | HL⁻ ⇌ L²⁻ + H⁺ | **3.8** | vlm_151494 |

*(Note: The database stores protonation log β values; the pKa values shown are the corresponding acid dissociation constants.)*

### Chemistry Insight

The unusually **low pKa₁ ≈ 1.2** reflects the strong electron-withdrawing effect of the adjacent carboxyl group, making oxalic acid one of the strongest organic diprotic acids. The relatively small gap between pKa₁ and pKa₂ (~2.6 units) is characteristic of short-chain dicarboxylic acids where the two acidic groups are in close proximity. These properties make oxalate (L²⁻) an excellent chelating ligand — the database lists complexes with **20 different metals** including Fe³⁺, Al³⁺, Ca²⁺, Cu²⁺, and UO₂²⁺.

*Source: NIST SRD-46 (ligand_8872, metal_68)*
