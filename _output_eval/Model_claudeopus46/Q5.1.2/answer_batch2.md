## Key Takeaways

**DTPA is an extraordinarily powerful chelator**, with log K₁ values spanning nearly 30 orders of magnitude — from K⁺ (1.3) to Hf⁴⁺ (29.5). A few notable highlights:

1. **Highest-affinity metals** are the highly charged ions Hf⁴⁺, Th⁴⁺, Zr⁴⁺, and Fe³⁺ (log K > 28), reflecting DTPA's ability to satisfy high coordination demands with its 8 donor atoms (3 amines + 5 carboxylates).

2. **Lanthanide series trend**: log K₁ increases smoothly from La³⁺ (18.36) → Lu³⁺ (22.44), beautifully tracking the **lanthanide contraction** — smaller Ln³⁺ ions bind more tightly to the preorganized DTPA cavity.

3. **Clinical relevance**: The Gd³⁺–DTPA complex (log K₁ = 20.85) is the basis of **Magnevist®**, one of the most widely used MRI contrast agents. The high stability constant ensures Gd³⁺ remains safely chelated *in vivo*, though it is notably lower than some macrocyclic alternatives like DOTA.
