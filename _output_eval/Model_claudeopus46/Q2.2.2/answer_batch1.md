## Which metal ion has the most different ligands studied in NIST SRD-46?

**H⁺ (the proton)** tops the list with **4,081 unique ligands**, but it is not a conventional metal ion — it appears in SRD-46 because protonation equilibria (pKₐ) are recorded for virtually every ligand.

Among **true metal ions**, **Cu²⁺ (copper(II))** holds the record with **1,951 different ligands** studied.

### Chemistry insight

Cu²⁺'s dominance is no surprise — its **d⁹ electronic configuration** gives it Jahn–Teller flexibility, allowing it to adopt a wide range of coordination geometries (square planar, tetragonal, octahedral). Its **borderline hard–soft character** means it binds effectively to N-, O-, and S-donor ligands alike, making it the most versatile and most-studied metal ion in solution equilibrium chemistry. The top five are all divalent first-row transition metals (plus Cd²⁺), reflecting the historical focus of coordination chemistry on these biogeochemically important ions.
