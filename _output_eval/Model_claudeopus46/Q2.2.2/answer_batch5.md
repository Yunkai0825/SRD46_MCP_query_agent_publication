### Chemistry Insight

Cu(II)'s dominance is well explained by the **Irving–Williams series** (Mn < Fe < Co < Ni < **Cu** > Zn), which predicts that Cu(II) forms the most stable complexes among first-row divalent transition metals due to Jahn–Teller-enhanced ligand-field stabilization. Its borderline acid character (Pearson HSAB) makes it versatile with both N- and O-donor ligands — indeed, 47% of its ligand partners contain carboxyl groups and 35% contain primary amines. This thermodynamic versatility, combined with copper's central role in bioinorganic chemistry, catalysis, and environmental science, has driven researchers to characterize an exceptionally broad range of Cu(II) complexes.
