### Chemistry Insight

Cu²⁺'s dominance is unsurprising — its d⁹ electronic configuration and Jahn–Teller-active geometry allow it to form stable complexes with an extraordinary range of N-, O-, and S-donor ligands. It is central to bioinorganic chemistry (blue copper proteins, superoxide dismutase), environmental speciation studies, and industrial catalysis, driving extensive thermodynamic characterization. Ni²⁺, the runner-up with 1,369 ligands, is also a coordination chemistry workhorse, but Cu²⁺ leads by a substantial margin (~42% more ligands).
