### Chemistry Insight

The stepwise log K values decrease in the expected order K₁ > K₂ > K₃, reflecting the statistical effect and increasing steric crowding as successive bipyridyl ligands coordinate to Co²⁺. The relatively modest drop (~1 log unit per step) is characteristic of the octahedral tris(bipyridyl) geometry, where the planar bidentate ligand packs efficiently around the metal center. The near-independence of log β from ionic strength (0.1 vs 1.0 M) is consistent with the formation of a neutral-charge-type equilibrium quotient for a 2+ metal binding an uncharged ligand.
