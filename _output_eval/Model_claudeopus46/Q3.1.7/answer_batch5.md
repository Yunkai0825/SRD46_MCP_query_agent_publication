### Chemistry insight

The stepwise constants decrease in the expected order K₁ > K₂ > K₃, reflecting the statistical effect and increasing steric crowding as successive bipyridyl ligands coordinate to the octahedral Co(II) centre. The relatively modest drop per step (only ~0.3–0.8 log units) is characteristic of bidentate N-donor ligands that pack efficiently around the metal. The overall log β₃ ≈ 16 indicates a very stable tris-bipyridyl cobalt(II) complex, consistent with the well-known [Co(bpy)₃]²⁺ species used in electrochemistry and as a redox mediator in dye-sensitised solar cells.
