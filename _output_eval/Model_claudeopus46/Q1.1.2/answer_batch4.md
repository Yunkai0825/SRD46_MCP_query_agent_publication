## pKa Values of Citric Acid (NIST SRD-46)

Citric acid (2-hydroxypropane-1,2,3-tricarboxylic acid, **ligand_9058**) is a triprotic acid defined as **H₃L** in SRD-46. Three measured pKa values are reported at **25 °C** and **I = 0.1 M**:

| Step | Equilibrium | pKa | T (°C) | I (mol/L) | VLM ID |
|------|-------------|-----|--------|-----------|--------|
| pKa₁ | H₃L ⇌ H₂L⁻ + H⁺ | **2.90** | 25 | 0.1 | vlm_157473 |
| pKa₂ | H₂L⁻ ⇌ HL²⁻ + H⁺ | **4.35** | 25 | 0.1 | vlm_157459 |
| pKa₃ | HL²⁻ ⇌ L³⁻ + H⁺ | **5.65** | 25 | 0.1 | vlm_157439 |

### Chemistry Insight

The three pKa values correspond to the successive deprotonation of citric acid's three carboxyl groups. The ~1.4–1.3 unit spacing between steps is typical for polycarboxylic acids where the carboxylate groups are close enough to interact electrostatically — each successive proton is harder to remove because the molecule carries increasing negative charge. These values are widely used in buffer preparation (especially the pKa₂ ≈ 4.35 region) and in modeling metal–citrate complexation in biological and environmental systems.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46)*
