# pKa Values of Citric Acid (NIST SRD-46)

Citric acid (2-hydroxypropane-1,2,3-tricarboxylic acid, **ligand_9058**) is a triprotic acid defined as **H₃L** in SRD-46. The database contains **48 measured equilibrium entries** across three stepwise protonation equilibria:

| Step | Equilibrium | Beta Definition | Approximate pKa | # Measurements |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| pKa₁ | H₂L + H⁺ ⇌ H₃L | beta_def_53 | **~2.9** | 14 |
| pKa₂ | HL + H⁺ ⇌ H₂L | beta_def_32 | **~4.35** | 14 |
| pKa₃ | L + H⁺ ⇌ HL | beta_def_79 | **~5.65** | 20 |

The bracket pKa values from the ligand card are:

| Dissociation | pKa (reference) |
|--------------|-----------------|
| H₃L → H₂L + H⁺ | **2.9** |
| H₂L → HL + H⁺ | **4.35** |
| HL → L³⁻ + H⁺ | **5.65** |

## Chemistry Insight

Citric acid's three pKa values are well-separated, reflecting successive deprotonation of its three carboxyl groups. The central hydroxyl group does not deprotonate under normal aqueous conditions. The relatively low pKa₁ (~2.9) makes citric acid effective as a buffer in the acidic range, while pKa₂ and pKa₃ extend its buffering capacity into the mildly acidic to near-neutral pH range — which is why citrate buffers are widely used in biochemistry and food science. The extensive dataset (48 measurements across varied ionic strengths and temperatures) reflects citric acid's importance as a reference ligand in coordination chemistry and analytical applications.

*Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes)*
