Based on my thorough search of the NIST SRD-46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes) database, **there are no data on metal–protein binding** such as transferrin, albumin, metallothionein, ferritin, or any other macromolecular protein.

### What SRD-46 covers

SRD-46 is focused on **small-molecule ligands** and their equilibrium stability constants with metal ions. The ligand classes in the database include:

| Ligand class | Count |
|---|---|
| Amino Acids | 297 |
| Peptides | 157 |
| Polypeptides | 98 |
| Tripeptides | 66 |
| Aliphatic amines | 250 |
| Pyridines | 248 |
| Carboxylic acids | 182 |
| EDTA and derivatives | 115 |
| … and ~50 more small-molecule classes | |

The closest entries to biological macromolecules are **amino acids**, **peptides**, **tripeptides**, and **polypeptides** — but these are small, well-defined molecules (e.g., glycine, glutathione, short oligopeptides), not intact proteins.

### Why proteins aren't included

Stability constants in the thermodynamic sense (log β, log K) are well-defined for metal–small-ligand equilibria in solution. For proteins like transferrin, the binding is far more complex — involving multiple binding sites, conformational changes, and kinetic factors — and is typically characterized by **binding affinities (Kd)** or **association constants** measured by specialized biophysical techniques (ITC, SPR, fluorescence quenching) rather than classical potentiometric/spectrophotometric methods used for SRD-46 data.

### Where to look instead

For metal–protein binding data, you might consult:
- **BioLiP** or **MetalPDB** — databases of metal-binding sites in protein structures
- **MESPEUS** — metal sites in proteins from the PDB
- Primary literature on transferrin iron-binding constants (log K ≈ 20–22 for Fe³⁺)

*Source: NIST SRD-46 — Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes*
