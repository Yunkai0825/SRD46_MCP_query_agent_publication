Answer: EDTA forms the more stable Fe³⁺ complex than NTA.

| Condition (aqueous) | log β1 (Fe³⁺–EDTA) | log β1 (Fe³⁺–NTA) |
|---|---:|---:|
| 25 °C, I = 0.1 M | 25.10 | 15.90 |
| 25 °C, I = 1.0 M | 23.80 | 14.78 |

Notes
- β1 definition: [ML]/([M][L]) for the reaction Fe³⁺ + L ⇌ FeL (no H⁺ or OH⁻ terms; no polynuclear species).
- The Fe³⁺–EDTA complex is ~9 log units more stable than Fe³⁺–NTA at 0.1 M ionic strength. Both constants decrease at higher ionic strength, but EDTA remains much stronger—consistent with its higher denticity (hexadentate) and stronger chelate effect compared to NTA (tridentate).

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46), stability measurements for Fe³⁺ with EDTA (ligand_6277) and NTA (ligand_6165), β_def_812 (ML formation) at 25 °C.
