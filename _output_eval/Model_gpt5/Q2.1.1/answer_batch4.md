Conclusion: EDTA forms a much more stable Fe³⁺ complex than NTA. At 25 °C and I ≈ 0.1 M, EDTA’s ML formation constant is ~9.2 log units higher (≈10⁹-fold in K). Increasing ionic strength lowers both constants, but EDTA remains far stronger.
