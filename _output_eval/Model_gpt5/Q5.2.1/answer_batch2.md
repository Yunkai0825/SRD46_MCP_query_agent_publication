Interpretation
- This absence is consistent with the chemistry: neutral crown ethers are outstanding for solvating alkali/alkaline‑earth cations, but they rarely form well‑defined, measurable 1:1 aqueous inner‑sphere complexes with the linear uranyl dication. Literature on “uranyl–crown” systems often concerns neutral adducts or extraction species such as UO2(NO3)2·L in nitric acid or organic solvents (ion pairs or solvate adducts) rather than true aqueous UO2L^2+ formation constants at defined ionic strength.
