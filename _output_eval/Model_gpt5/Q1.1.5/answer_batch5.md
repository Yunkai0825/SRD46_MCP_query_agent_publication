| Step | SRD‑46 bracket (from→to) | pKa | T (°C) | Ionic strength (M) |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|

| 2 | L → HL | 3.8 | 25 | 0.10 |

Answer to your question: oxalic acid is diprotic, so there are 2 dissociation steps.

Notes:
- SRD‑46 records the first entry in the protonation direction (HL → H2L) with pKa = -1.2 at 25 °C and I = 0.10 M; the second (L → HL) is 3.8 under the same conditions.
- As a diprotic dicarboxylic acid, oxalic acid shows a much stronger first deprotonation than its second, consistent with the stabilization of the anion and charge effects in solution.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD‑46), ligand_id = ligand_8872, vlm_151532 and vlm_151494.
