Ranking (highest to lowest affinity with thiocyanate, based on SRD‑46 catalog evidence): Hg2+ > Cd2+ > Pb2+. The soft metal with the highest affinity is Hg2+.

Brief insight: Thiocyanate is a soft base and binds via sulfur; Hg2+ (a very soft acid) forms especially strong complexes and higher stoichiometries (and even a solid Hg(SCN)2 phase), while Cd2+ shows moderate 1:1 binding (log K1 ≈ 1.7 at 25 °C, I≈1 M) and Pb2+ is weaker under comparable conditions.
