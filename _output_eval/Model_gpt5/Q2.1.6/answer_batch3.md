Short answer: Hg²⁺ > Cd²⁺ > Pb²⁺ for binding to thiocyanate (SCN⁻). The softest metal, Hg²⁺, shows the highest affinity.

Notes and provenance
- SRD‑46 coverage confirms ML (β1) data exist for all three pairs with thiocyanate at near‑ambient conditions:
  - Cd²⁺ + SCN⁻: ML (β1) entries present (beta_def_812; 15 entries)
  - Pb²⁺ + SCN⁻: ML (β1) entries present (beta_def_812; 6 entries)
  - Hg²⁺ + SCN⁻: ML (β1) entries present (beta_def_812; 6 entries)
- This ranking aligns with HSAB: SCN⁻ is a soft/ambidentate anion that favors S‑coordination; Hg²⁺ (soft) forms the strongest Hg–S bonds, Cd²⁺ (borderline‑soft) is intermediate, and Pb²⁺ (borderline) is weaker under comparable aqueous conditions.
