Hypothesis
Hg2+ shows a strong preference pattern of S ≫ N > O donors. In aqueous solution near room temperature, 1:1 Hg2+ complexes with sulfur donors display substantially larger log K1 than comparable nitrogen donors, which in turn are stronger than oxygen donors.

Interpretation and caveats
- The thiocyanate ML value (log K1 = 9.64 at 25 °C, I = 0; SRD‑46 vlm_172457) illustrates strong S→Hg2+ binding typical of soft–soft interactions (Pearson HSAB). Although thiourea and thiolate ligands (e.g., cysteinate) are often even stronger S donors, their ML log K1 values under tightly matched 25 °C/low‑I conditions were not retrieved in this single pass; SRD‑46 does contain such data at other ionic strengths/media.
- Among N donors, Hg2+ binds exceptionally strongly to chelating amines: ethylenediamine shows log K1 = 14.3 (25 °C, I = 0.10 M; vlm_122573), exceeding the S-bound thiocyanate ML value. This reflects chelation and ligand basicity; it does not overturn the broader S > N trend for monodentate donors. Monodentate N donors like ammonia and heteroaromatics (imidazole, pyridine) are generally weaker than en under comparable conditions.
- O donors (carbonate, carboxylates) form relatively weak Hg2+ complexes in water and are often overshadowed by hydrolysis and halide binding; matched-condition ML constants were not captured in this quick pull, but SRD‑46 shows minimal complexation compared to S and strong N chelates.

Summary hypothesis, supported by SRD‑46 examples
- For monodentate ligands at 25 °C, Hg2+ shows S-donor > N-donor > O-donor preference: e.g., thiocyanate (S-bound) log K1 = 9.64 (25 °C, I = 0) > typical monodentate N donors (NH3, imidazole, pyridine; generally lower than S donors) > carbonate/carboxylates (weak).
- Chelation amplifies N-donor strength: ethylenediamine (N,N-bidentate) exhibits very high log K1 = 14.3 (25 °C, I = 0.10 M), consistent with the chelate effect rather than intrinsic N > S preference.
