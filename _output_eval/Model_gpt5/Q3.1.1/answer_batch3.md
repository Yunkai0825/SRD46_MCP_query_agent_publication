Notes and interpretation
- Affinity ranking by intrinsic constants at matched conditions is roughly: DTPA > CDTA > EGTA ≳ EDTA ≈ DMPS > DMSA > penicillamine > cysteine ≫ citrate. Despite very high β for DTPA/CDTA, they are not standard for lead therapy.
- Clinically used for lead: CaNa2EDTA (edetate calcium disodium; intravenous), DMSA/succimer (oral). DMPS is used in some regions but is not first‑line for Pb; BAL is historical and generally avoided for Pb due to toxicity and inferior performance.
- Why the clinic doesn’t always pick the “highest β”: pharmacokinetics, tissue distribution, safety, and essential‑metal competition (e.g., Ca2+, Zn2+) matter. EDTA is administered as the Ca2+ salt to prevent hypocalcemia while still exchanging Pb2+. DMSA’s oral bioavailability and safety profile make it a preferred outpatient therapy even though its intrinsic β is lower than EDTA/DTPA.
