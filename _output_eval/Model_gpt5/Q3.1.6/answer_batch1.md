Notes and interpretation
- These are cumulative log β1 (ML) formation constants; higher values indicate more stable 1:1 La3+ complexes. Ionic strength strongly affects apparent stability; where possible, I reported values near I = 0.10 M for cross‑ligand comparison.
- As expected for a hard trivalent cation, multidentate aminopolycarboxylates dominate: DTPA > EDTA by several orders of magnitude. Among O‑only polycarboxylates, citrate is the strongest here, but still far below EDTA/DTPA.
- If you need a strictly O‑donor comparison (no pyridine N), the top three by the numbers above would be DTPA (O,N), EDTA (O,N), and citrate (O only). For O‑only sets, citrate > oxalate > tartrate > lactate/acetate under similar conditions.

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (Critically Selected Stability Constants of Metal Complexes).
