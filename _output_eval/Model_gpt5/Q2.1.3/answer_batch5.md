- pKa2: alanine is higher by ~0.14 units (9.71 vs 9.57).

Conclusion: Glycine and alanine have very similar stepwise pKa values in water; the small 0.14‑unit increase in alanine’s pKa2 is modest and often within the spread expected from changes in ionic strength and temperature. Chemically, alanine’s methyl group mildly donates electron density inductively, making the ammonium slightly less acidic (thus a slightly higher pKa2) than glycine.
