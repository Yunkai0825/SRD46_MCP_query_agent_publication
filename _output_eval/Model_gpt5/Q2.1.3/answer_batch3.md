Conclusion: They are not significantly different. Both have the same carboxyl pKa (2.33), and the ammonium pKa differs by only 0.14 units (alanine slightly higher). The small increase for alanine is consistent with the weak electron-donating effect of the methyl group stabilizing the protonated amine and nudging its basicity up; the carboxyl acidity is essentially unchanged. Note that individual measurements vary with temperature and ionic strength, but the ligand-card pKa brackets above summarize the consensus values in water.
