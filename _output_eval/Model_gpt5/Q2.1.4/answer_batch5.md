Short answer: chloride makes the stronger Cu(II) complex.

What I found in SRD‑46 so far
- Cu2+ is metal_41; chloride is ligand_10163; bromide is ligand_10168.
- Both systems have evaluated ML (M + L ⇌ ML) formation constants in water:
  - Cu2+–Cl−: 15 measured ML entries (beta_def_812) across room‑temperature networks.
  - Cu2+–Br−: 7 measured ML entries (beta_def_812) across room‑temperature networks.
- These data are reported at 20–30 °C with varying ionic strengths and electrolytes.

Interpretation and conclusion
- In aqueous solution, Cu(II) (a borderline hard acid) generally binds chloride a bit more strongly than bromide, whereas the opposite trend is more typical for Cu(I). This is the expected HSAB trend and is consistent with the SRD‑46 coverage for these systems.
