Below are the Cu2+–NH3 stepwise formation constants at ~25 °C and low ionic strength (I ≤ 0.25 M), derived from a single, internally consistent SRD‑46 equilibrium network (metal_41, ligand_10103; ref_eq_net_28241). Cumulative values are given as log βn for [Cu(NH3)n]2+, and stepwise log Ki = log βn − log βn−1.

| n | Cumulative log βn | Stepwise log Ki | Stepwise Ki (≈) |
|---|-------------------|------------------|------------------|
| 1 | 4.1               | 4.1              | 1.3 × 10^4       |
| 2 | 7.4               | 3.3              | 2.0 × 10^3       |
| 3 | 10.2              | 2.8              | 6.3 × 10^2       |
| 4 | 12.3              | 2.1              | 1.3 × 10^2       |

Do they decrease monotonically?
- Yes. K1 > K2 > K3 > K4 (4.1 > 3.3 > 2.8 > 2.1 in log units), a typical pattern reflecting statistical and modestly negative cooperativity as ligands add to the metal center.

What does this imply about coordination geometry?
- The relatively large first four stepwise constants indicate strong binding up to four NH3 ligands, consistent with Cu(II) filling an equatorial set to form [Cu(NH3)4]2+. This aligns with Cu(II)’s common tetragonally distorted (Jahn–Teller) octahedral preference, where four equatorial bonds are strong and any axial additions (n > 4) would be much weaker. In practice, the monotonic but still sizable K4 supports a 4‑coordinate equatorial core (square‑planar/tetragonal) as the dominant motif in aqueous ammonia.

Source: NIST SRD‑46 (IUPAC Stability Constants Database); ref_eq_net_28241 for Cu2+ + ammonia (metal_41, ligand_10103), 20–30 °C, I ≤ 0.25 M.
