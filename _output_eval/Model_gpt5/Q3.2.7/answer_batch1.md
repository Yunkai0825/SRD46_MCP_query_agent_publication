Below is one self-consistent aqueous dataset for Cu2+–ammonia near room temperature. The network reports cumulative “log βn” values; I converted them to stepwise “log Kn” via:
log K1 = log β1; log K2 = log β2 − log β1; log K3 = log β3 − log β2; log K4 = log β4 − log β3.

Conditions (ref_eq_net_28241): water, T 20–30 °C (≈25 °C), low ionic strength (≈ −0.05 to 0.25 M).

| n (NH3) | cumulative log βn | stepwise log Kn |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 4.1 | 4.1 |
| 2 | 7.4 | 3.3 |
| 3 | 10.2 | 2.8 |
| 4 | 12.3 | 2.1 |

Monotonicity: Yes — K1 > K2 > K3 > K4. The steady decrease indicates negative cooperativity and site saturation: each added NH3 binds less strongly as the first, second, and third sites are filled. The fact that four stepwise bindings are all favorable yet progressively weaker is consistent with Cu(II)’s characteristic 4+2 (Jahn–Teller–elongated octahedral) preference: four strong equatorial NH3 donors form readily (approaching a square-planar equatorial set), while any 5th/6th donors are much weaker (axial, elongated).

Source: NIST Standard Reference Database 46 (SRD-46): Cu2+ (metal_41) + Ammonia (ligand_10103), reference equilibrium network ref_eq_net_28241 (beta_def_812, 840, 872, 894).
