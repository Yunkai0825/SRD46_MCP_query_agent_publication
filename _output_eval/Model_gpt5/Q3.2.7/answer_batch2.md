Short answer
- Trend: The stepwise formation constants for Cu2+ with ammonia decrease in the order K1 > K2 > K3 > K4 in aqueous solution at ambient conditions (each successive NH3 binds less strongly than the previous one).
- Monotonic? Yes—through the fourth ligand the stepwise constants decrease monotonically; beyond four, further ligation drops off more sharply.
- Geometry insight: This pattern indicates a strong preference for four equatorial Cu–NH3 bonds. In water, Cu(II) (d9) typically exhibits a Jahn–Teller–distorted octahedral environment best described as a square-planar equatorial set with weak/elongated axial interactions—i.e., [Cu(NH3)4(H2O)2]2+ dominates, whereas 5–6 ammine complexes are much less favored.

Notes
- Exact values depend on temperature and ionic strength; the trend above is observed for near‑25 °C, low‑ionic‑strength water.
