I could not retrieve the Ca–oxalate stability/solubility constant within the time budget, but SRD‑46 does resolve oxalic acid (ligand_8872) and indicates it is diprotic with pKa2 ≈ 3.8 in water at ambient conditions. That matters because calcium oxalate precipitation only becomes favorable once the oxalate dianion (C2O4²−) is appreciable.

Rule‑of‑thumb from acid–base speciation: the dianion fraction rises rapidly around pH ≈ pKa2 and is clearly significant by pKa2+1. Therefore, in typical dilute aqueous calcium solutions, calcium oxalate precipitation begins to become significant around pH ~4–5, increasing strongly as pH climbs further (where C2O4²− dominates over HC2O4⁻).

Source: NIST SRD‑46 (ligand card for ethanedioic acid/oxalic acid, ligand_8872).
