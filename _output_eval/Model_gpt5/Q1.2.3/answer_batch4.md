Chemistry insight: This network captures Zn(II) hydrolysis from monomeric aquo ion to stepwise hydroxo complexes, and onward to polynuclear species. The presence of multiple Zn(OH)2(s) polymorphs and ZnO(s) nodes reflects precipitation pathways under basic conditions, while higher hydroxo stoichiometries ([ML3], [ML4]) and clusters ([M4L4]) typically grow with increasing pH and metal concentration.
