Short answer: yes—EDTA is widely used because it forms very stable 1:1 complexes with many divalent and trivalent metal ions. In SRD-46 it is linked to hundreds of measured metal–ligand entries, and its chelation strength is strongly pH‑dependent.

What this means
- As pH increases, EDTA loses protons and approaches its highly chelating Y4− form; conditional stability constants for metal–EDTA complexes are therefore largest in moderately basic solutions.
- The extensive number of metal–EDTA measurements in SRD‑46 reflects its effectiveness across many common metals (e.g., alkaline earths and first‑row transition metals), although exact stability varies by metal and oxidation state.
