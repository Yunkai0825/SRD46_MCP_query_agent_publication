What these numbers suggest
- Hg(II) forms very strong 1:1 complexes with multidentate aminocarboxylates in dilute media (logK ≈ 18–19), and those ML adducts can further transform with OH−/H+ (logK ≈ 7 for hydroxo conversion; ≈5 for protonation), indicating pH-sensitive speciation around the chelated core.
- Hg(I) is dominated by solid Hg2Cl2 in chloride media; the strongly negative dissolution logK for [M2L2(s)] ⇌ [M]^2 + [L]^2 reflects the sparing solubility and limited aqueous Hg+ stability. In contrast, modest complexation with organic anions (e.g., chloroacetate) appears at high ionic strength.
- Organomercury cations show ligand-specific behavior: hydroxo binding is strong for PhHg+ (logK ~9.75), and chloride binds EtHg+ appreciably in saline conditions (logK 5.32). For MeHg+, the entry here provides thermodynamics (ΔS) but no logK for MeHgOH at 25 °C, I = 0.10 M.
